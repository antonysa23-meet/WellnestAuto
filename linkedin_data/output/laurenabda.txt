Food and agriculture may be one of the largest sectors of the economy, but it's still one of the most misunderstood by venture capital.

I recently joined the Investment Climate Podcast with Alex Shandrovsky to discuss Branch Venture Group, what we've learned from investing in food and agriculture innovation over the past decade, and why we're excited about the next generation of companies transforming the food system.

A few themes from our conversation:
- The current market has rewarded companies solving real industry problems, not just chasing growth at all costs.
- Food and agriculture remain significantly undercapitalized relative to their economic importance and impact.
- The strongest founders often possess a unique insight, expertise, or market access that creates an enduring advantage.
- Building in food requires patience, operational excellence, and a deep understanding of how the industry actually works.

At BVG we've invested in 29 companies since 2017, including two unicorns, and nearly half of our portfolio companies are currently cash-flow break-even. 

Those results have reinforced our conviction that there is tremendous opportunity in backing founders building the infrastructure, technologies, and businesses that will shape the future of food.

As we continue building, I'm excited about the opportunity ahead not only for investors, but for the entrepreneurs tackling some of the food system's biggest challenges.

Thanks very much, Alex, for the thoughtful discussion. And a special shoutout to Mothership Materials and EdiMembre, Inc. covered in our discussion!
