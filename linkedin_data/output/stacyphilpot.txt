About
I am inspired by the opportunity to impact people’s lives every day all over the world, which is why as a leader, I believe my purpose is to make it easier for others to achieve their goals. This often means practicing discernment and flexibility in the face of challenges. As founder and managing partner of Cherryrock Capital, I get to help catalyze the success of the next generation of entrepreneurs every day.  From my experience managing global operations at Google and TaskRabbit, I know what it takes to execute at scale, and I’m not afraid to take calculated risks. However, I also know that things don’t always go the way we expect them to, and you’ve got to have a plan if things don’t work out. But most importantly, you’ve got to have the courage to stick to the plan when things get tough and trust your team to follow through. I know if I continue to do good work and follow my heart in a way that allows me to accomplish more, it will pay off. … more

We just raised a $22M Series B for Vori to make every supermarket in America autonomous.

Grocery is a $1.5T domestic market — bigger than restaurants, bigger than hotels. But it's running on technology from the Reagan administration. 

This industry is massive, essential, and near and dear to my heart.

My parents met in a supermarket. My grandparents owned a grocery store. My mom Tori works at Vori.

Walmart and Amazon have invested heavily in this space, capturing 25% of U.S. grocery spending. But no one is building tech for the other 75% of the market.

Vori is the engine for grocery: an AI-native operating system that handles checkout, payments, pricing, ordering, inventory, and loyalty.

→ A system of record: tracks all activity happening in the store in one place. 
→ A system of action: agents leverage what the store "knows" to actually do the work.
→ A system of transaction: Vori processes every dollar that flows through the store, accounting for 60% of our revenue.

Since launch, we have processed $500M+ in payments across 140+ stores in 55+ cities, serving over 1M consumers nationwide.

In the past six months, we have doubled payment volume, with new stores being onboarded every single day. The revenue we used to close in a year, we now close in a month.

This round — led by Adrianna Samaniego at Cherryrock Capital with participation from Mike Duboe at Greylock Partners and Factory — brings our total funding to $50M. The capital will go toward expanding our engineering and product teams, shipping the next generation of AI agents for grocery, and bringing Vori to thousands more stores.

To Tremaine, Robert, and the entire Vori team: there's no one I'd rather build this with.

To the grocers who trust us with their stores every day: you are load-bearing pillars of your economies, and you inspire us every day to do work that matters.

Our mission is to power grocery stores with the technology to run more profitably and increase the GDP of Main Street.

We're just getting started (and we're hiring).… more

The media industry is rapidly adopting AI for video understanding, but there hasn’t been a standard way to evaluate which models actually perform best on real media workflows. Until now, thanks to the team Coactive AI. 

MediaPerf is the first open benchmark for AI video understanding built around the data and tasks the media industry actually uses. It tests models on content tagging, summarization, and tag refinement using real video content, and reports quality, cost, and throughput together so teams can make informed decisions.

Cody Coleman

Today, we’re launching MediaPerf, the first open benchmark for AI video understanding in the media industry.

We built this at Coactive AI because we kept running into the same problem with our customers: the benchmarks available for video AI don’t reflect how media teams actually use these models. Most benchmarks test short clips on generic tasks. Media production teams need to evaluate models on content tagging, summarization, and tag refinement across real video content. And they need to see quality, cost, and latency together, because in production, you can’t optimize for just one.

MediaPerf v. 2026.02 benchmarks 8 multimodal models on 2,003 ad videos with real media industry tasks. The governance board includes leaders from Amazon Web Services (AWS), Business Insider, Harvard University, Georgia Institute of Technology, NBCUniversal, and NVIDIA. The codebase is open source under Apache 2.0. The evaluation data is CC-BY.

This is v1. The benchmark will expand to more models, more tasks, and more data. If you work in media or AI and want to get involved, check it out: https://mediaperf.org/
