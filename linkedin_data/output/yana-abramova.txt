DavisAI just raised $5.5M led by @Balderton and Heartcore Capital, and their bet is the opposite of what most AI companies are doing.

No existing approach really solves architectural floor-plan generation. Image generation models produce visually plausible floor plans but break under real-world constraints; they were never built to reason about discrete architectural elements like rooms, walls, and layouts. Combinatorial optimization handles constraints natively but doesn't scale.

Davis AI is building a third path: a true generative model operating in discrete space, generating buildings as structured compositions of architectural elements. Constraints aren't a post-processing step; they're baked into generation itself. Gaudi-1, their first proprietary model, already achieves SOTA on RPLAN and MSD. And this is just the start. Floor-plan generation is one piece of a much larger system. 

@Davis is the AI-native real estate company compressing early-stage development timelines from months to days.

But what got us equally excited: the founders. The moment you meet Mehdi Rais and Amine Chraibi, you sense something rarer, the kind of team that makes you want to back them before the pitch is over.

We at Pretiosum Ventures are proud to back them. 

And they're only getting started.

Today, Davis AI is exiting stealth. We're announcing a $5.5M pre-seed round led by Heartcore Capital and Balderton with participation from Yellow, Evantic Capital and a group of exceptional angels.

2mo • Edited • 

Agentic AI has an infrastructure problem. We built the solution,... and it's FREE for OpenClaw users.

Anthropic's recent restrictions on using OpenClaw as a tool within Claude exposed a real and growing inefficiency in enterprise AI infrastructure: agentic workloads are being served on compute stacks that were never designed for them.

Agentic inference is fundamentally different from the inference era we have been building for.

Majority of agent workloads are background tasks. Latency-tolerant, routine, and always-on. Yet most teams run them on the same premium compute topology designed for real-time sessions. That mismatch is expensive, fragile, and unnecessary.

OpenInfer routes each workload based on its SLA and characteristics to the right compute topology automatically. The result: a major compute dependency unlock and genuine cost control at scale — without migration pain, without re-architecting your stack.

This is what Weave (https://lnkd.in/evSHBEmB), our inference orchestration engine, was built for: treating execution strategy as a first-class variable, not an afterthought.
Today, we are launching OpenInfer Beta.
