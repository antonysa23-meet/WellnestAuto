About
Lindsey Taylor Wood is Founder and General Partner of The Helm, a London- and New York City-based venture firm investing in female-founded healthcare and sustainability technology companies. 

She has spent two decades driving capital formation, funding infrastructure, and strategic partnerships that advance women through innovation, philanthropy, media, and politics. Previously founded LTW, where she advised leading foundations, philanthropic institutions, investors, and global brands.

The Helm has invested in more than 35 companies spanning healthcare, aerospace, advanced manufacturing, and climate technologies, often alongside firms including General Catalyst, Kleiner Perkins, Goldman Sachs, Bezos Expeditions, and Y Combinator.

Before founding The Helm, Lindsey founded LTW, an advisory practice focused on capital formation and strategic partnerships for leading foundations, philanthropic institutions, investors, and global brands. Her clients included AOL's MAKERS, Women Moving Millions, The Harnisch Foundation, The Conversation with Amanda de Cadenet, actor and activist Matt McGorry, and filmmakers Jill Bauer, Ronna Gradus, and Rashida Jones. She also contributed to fundraising and donor engagement efforts supporting women running for national office, including Hillary Clinton and Stacey Abrams. Through this work, she developed initiatives and partnerships spanning the White House, Sundance Film Festival, and USC Annenberg.

Earlier in her career, Lindsey worked at Women Deliver, one of the first organizations to build a rigorous economic and development case for investing in girls and women. There she led the Women Deliver 50, a global initiative recognizing innovative solutions advancing the lives of girls and women worldwide, and trained youth advocates across 11 sub-Saharan African countries in support of UN Millennium Development Goal 5. She later joined the founding team of Catapult, the first crowdfunding platform dedicated exclusively to girls and women, helping build a funding platform that directed capital to women-led projects around the world.

Lindsey began her career in journalism and media, producing at NBC's TODAY and reporting on U.S. foreign policy for Voice of America. Her work has been featured in Bloomberg, The New York Times, Fortune, Forbes, CNBC, Vogue, Fast Company, and PitchBook. She is a frequent speaker at global conferences and events, including the White House's United State of Women, SOCAP, and the WIN Summit, and serves as a board observer at Testmate and advisor to Métier and Mysha.
