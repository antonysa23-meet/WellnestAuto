View company: Wayfinder Biosciences
Wayfinder Biosciences

1yr • 

We are thrilled to share Wayfinder Biosciences has been awarded a grant by the Andy Hill Cancer Research Endowment (CARE) Fund! This grant will support Wayfinder’s oncology pipeline and our program focused on drugging c-Myc using orally-available RNA-targeting molecules. 

c-Myc is major cancer driver that is involved in many of the cellular processes at the heart of cancer — in fact, MYC is deregulated in >50% of human cancers. Despite its importance, c-Myc remains an elusive target for therapeutic intervention with traditional drug development approaches.

At Wayfinder, we’ve developed a proprietary technology that allows us to discover small molecules that can bind to a specific mRNA and stop it from producing the protein it encodes. By applying this platform to traditionally inaccessible targets such as c-Myc, we can turn them off at the source, eliminating their toxic effects. Our platform combines unique screening and computational technologies to rapidly discover these biologically active RNA-targeting small molecules.

We’re honored to receive this non-dilutive funding, which will significantly accelerate our efforts to develop new innovative therapies for cancer, and to contribute to Washington State's history of groundbreaking therapies. Congratulations to the other awardees as well, please check them out too!
https://lnkd.in/g5pUzadp
