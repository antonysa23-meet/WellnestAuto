About
I’m the founder of Basis Set, one of the earliest AI-focused venture funds, with over half a billion dollars in assets under management. We strive to be the earliest institutional partner to founders who are exceptionally fast learners. I’ve been fortunate to partner early with companies like Quince, Scale, Drata, Path, Workstream, Ergeon, and CuspAI. Before starting Basis Set, I worked with some of the best people at Dropbox and McKinsey and supported a generation of iconic founders during my time in Corporate Development. My background is in academia, and I continue to pursue research through Pascal, which I’ll share more about soon.

3w • Edited • 

Sam Levin and I are super excited to announce Neion Bio’s $23M Series A, led by our long-time partners Caffeinated Capital. We are thrilled to be joined in this round by Digitalis Ventures, Ensemble VC, and Trust Ventures, as well as deepen our partnerships with Haystack and Basis Set.

Supply chain challenges in semiconductors and rare earths have been well established, but issues in biologics have only recently entered the zeitgeist. Our critical, life-saving medicines are reliant on supply chains that are vulnerable to weaponization by bad actors, and fragile in times of pandemic. 

Most of the proposed solutions to this issue involve shifting manufacturing sites onshore, however this does little to disentangle the reliance on complex and brittle webs of inputs. These solutions almost always increase costs significantly, which is a non-starter for most pharma and biotech companies.

The central challenge lies in how biologics are actually manufactured. 

For 50 years this has been done in steel and plastic tanks, in infrastructure that costs billions of dollars and is reliant on 100s of specialized inputs. This came to be because the biological engineering tools at the time were limited — we could only engineer simple cells, so we used complex hardware to compensate. A common misconception is that these hardware-based systems are efficient and precise — in fact they are merely attempting to replicate the body of an organism (doing so poorly). 

But recent technology breakthroughs have changed all of this. 

At Neion Bio, we are unlocking new biology that allows us to leapfrog these traditional systems. Using cutting edge genome engineering we harness chicken eggs — an evolutionarily optimized protein factory — as bioreactors to manufacture biologics. We not only onshore manufacturing, but completely localize it, since our primarily inputs are grain and water. We do this while simultaneously reducing the costs of production, and dramatically decreasing the capital requirements. 

This latest fundraise will enable us to expand our product portfolio and partnerships, as well as commercialize novel technologies that significantly speed up time to production.

Read more about it in Anna Brown’s exclusive coverage in Endpoints News (link in comments).
