About
Regina “Gina” Kline is an investor, entrepreneur, civil rights lawyer, and thought leader dedicated to building the future of work by advancing the rights and interests of people with disabilities as innovators, workers, business owners, and consumers. As Founder and Managing Partner of Enable Ventures and Founder of SmartJob, Gina is singularly focused as a leader on closing the disability wealth gap through impact investment.
​
Gina has worked to challenge and end some of the most exploitative practices in the country affecting workers with disabilities, and has built meaningful, long-term relationships throughout the disability community.
​
For nearly 15 years as a civil rights attorney, Gina led efforts to dramatically improve working conditions and the employment of people with disabilities. As a Trial Attorney at the U.S. Department of Justice, Civil Rights Division, Gina successfully fought for workers with disabilities in segregated workplaces, where they were often paid pennies per hour, helping secure court orders to support and move over 11,000 workers to competitive integrated employment.
 
Gina filed and resolved the nation’s first cases under the ADA to challenge these segregated and often exploitative work settings. These landmark cases set a new standard in American law impacting millions of students and adults with disabilities. Gina received awards from Attorney General Eric Holder and Attorney General Loretta Lynch for her work. 
​
During the Obama administration, Gina also served as Senior Counsel to the Assistant Attorney General for Civil Rights at the U.S. Department of Justice, where she provided legal and policy counsel regarding efforts to implement the Americans with Disabilities Act and the Supreme Court’s landmark Olmstead v. L.C. decision. She then represented students and workers with disabilities in civil rights matters across the country as a Partner at the law firm of Brown, Goldstein & Levy, LLP. While at the firm, she was the Co-Leader of a national disability consulting practice that she co-founded.
 
Gina received her J.D. from the University of Maryland School of Law where she graduated cum laude and her B.A. from Columbia University. Gina is the author of multiple government reports and evaluations of the U.S. disability employment system, has provided public testimony in front of federal and state government entities and commissions, and has advised public and private leaders and organizations on disability employment.

→ Specialized mentorship in assistive, rehabilitative, and mobility tech and high-impact business
→ Certification and compliance support
→ Workspace inside the ONOVOLAB innovation hub in São Carlos — one of Brazil's leading science and technology ecosystems

Kalogon is an AI-driven smart seating company, led by Founder and CEO Timothy Balz, on a mission to make sitting healthier by starting with the people who need it most and who have strengthened its design.

Kalogon's flagship product, the Orbiter Med, delivers custom air pressure distribution for wheelchair users, improving posture and significantly reducing the risk of pressure injuries. It's high-tech and deeply human.

Kalogon's impact extends beyond the wheelchair market. Their empathy-driven, data-rooted design process means every cushion they ship teaches them something new about how people sit. That knowledge compounds. What they're building for wheelchair users today will become the standard of seating for everyone tomorrow.

We're honored to back a team that leads with both technical rigor and impact. Kalogon is redesigning the future of seating and we're thrilled to be part of that journey.

I'm proud to announce Enable Ventures' investment in Wheel the World! 

Led by visionary Co-founders Alvaro Silberstein and Camilo Navarro Bustos, Wheel the World is building a travel economy that is truly inclusive. Its vision is to be the trusted AI global intelligence layer for accessibility information which would fundamentally change how people with disabilities find and explore new places across the globe, at scale.

Wheel the World enables travelers with a disability to have a more predictable and accessible experience by curating a list of accessible accommodations and activities tailored to their specific travel needs. This lowers the barrier to travel and enables participation in the broader travel economy; allowing people with disabilities to engage in more business, and education-related travel, tourism, and leisure activities.

Wheel the World has raised $3.5 million to close their $11M Series A round, which was co-led by Enable Ventures and Kayyak Ventures and had participation from Vulcano, WeBoost, Samaritan Partners, ImpactVC, and Miles Partnership, as well as follow-on support from strategic leaders in the travel and hospitality industry, including Erik Blachford, former Expedia CEO, and Gillian Tans, former Booking.com CEO. 

New funding will be used to expand their Accessibility Verified program, integrate AI, and validate and distribute accessibility data.
