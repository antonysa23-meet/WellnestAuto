About
Tina Henry Bou-Saba has been investing in the consumer space since 2016. She founded CXT Investments to support purpose-driven entrepreneurs who are building the next generation of great consumer companies, including emerging brands and the technology that powers them. Tina has spent her career in the consumer and retail sectors, with extensive experience in both private and public markets.  She is a trusted partner to founders, who value her strategic and supportive approach, passion for consumer products and trends, and sharp instincts for commercial opportunities. Tina's investment focus areas include beauty, personal care, health and wellness, e-commerce, and enabling technology.… more

I’ve spent the past decade working alongside founders, investors, and operators who are building the next generation of great consumer companies.

Now, I’m excited to share the real stories behind entrepreneurship.

“There Is No Alternative: Lessons in Entrepreneurial Leadership with Tina Bou-Saba” is here. (Yes, that’s the "T.I.N.A." podcast :) In each episode, we dive into the moments that define the best builders: the grit, the vision, the strategy, and the quiet conviction that there’s no other choice but to keep going.

If you’ve ever created something from nothing, backed a founder in their earliest days, or just love learning about how great companies are really built, I’d love for you to listen, share, and come along for the ride.

Our first episode launches tomorrow on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and CXTinvestments.com.

This one’s for the builders – because for us, There Is No Alternative.

Thank you to our first guests Matt Mullenax (Huron) Patricia Santos (Volition Beauty) Benjamin Davis (TryNow.com) Sarah Foley (InviNext Growth Partners)!

Bath & Body Works holds a special place in my heart. I spent many hours exploring its mall stores as a consumer, and later as an equity analyst doing retail channel checks. I also got to see the BBW business up close when I worked at L Brands, then its parent company. In my mind, BBW is iconic.

Alas, I have been puzzled by the business’s underperformance in recent years despite a strong fragrance cycle. I don’t think that the brand has gotten adequate credit for product innovation and affordability. But what matters is consumer perception, and it is clear that they no longer see BBW as the fun and engaging destination it once was. Of course, declining mall traffic has not helped.

Erica La Sala at Beauty Independent explores the implications of BBW’s recently announced deal with Ulta Beauty. I don’t see this partnership as significantly needle-moving for BBW’s core business. I enjoyed reading these expert perspectives, many of which were more bullish than mine. I love a good debate! Let’s see how this plays out over the next few quarters. I will be watching closely.… more

I have known Sergio Tache and Dossier Perfumes since 2020, when the business was just getting going. I was struck then by something that I rarely see in consumer brands – genuine business model innovation. Not only a great product and brand, but also a brilliant, unique way to sell fragrance to consumers online. I have been following Sergio and Dossier’s journey ever since, and I was honored to host him on the There Is No Alternative Podcast to share it.

Dossier is Sergio’s third entrepreneurial venture. He came to the fragrance industry with an outsider’s eye for disruption, and an experienced operator’s obsession with unit economics. His insight was deceptively simple: why is fragrance so expensive? What followed was a masterclass in operating discipline, influencer-led growth, and relentless execution. 

This episode is tactical, timely, and inspiring. Thank you, Sergio!

I am excited to share my conversation with Brent Ridge, MD, Founder of Beekman 1802. Brent is a physician, longevity researcher, bestselling author, and one of the most thoughtful brand builders I know.

Beekman 1802 started with a note in a mailbox from a neighbor who was about to lose his herd of goats - “the original act of kindness,” as Brent describes it. Two years later, he started wrapping bars of soap at his dining room table, the beginning of what is now an 18 year-old brand with a Harvard Business School case study, Ulta Beauty distribution, and PE partnership with Eurazeo.

Some of my favorite moments from our conversation:

- Brent drove three and a half hours every day for eight weeks to sell soap at Henri Bendel
- His Instagram post asking "Ulta vs. Sephora" prompted Ulta’s CEO to fly Albany to visit the farm
- Beekman made the difficult decision to fire its first CEO the night before a major deal closed
- Kindness is an official ingredient in Beekman’s products, and the science behind this is fascinating

Brent's advice for early-stage founders: the world does not need another beauty product. Differentiation is crucial, and community is more powerful than ever.

Our full conversation is live. Link in comments.… more
