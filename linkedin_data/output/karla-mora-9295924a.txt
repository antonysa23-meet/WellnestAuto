View company: Hardware to Save a Planet
Hardware to Save a Planet

4w • 

Can replacing petroleum-based plastics with seaweed and methane-derived biomaterials become one of the biggest untapped levers in climate action?

Julia Marsh, CEO and co-founder of Sway , Molly Morse, CEO at Mango Materials, and Ryan Starling, Design Director at frog, believe it can, and they’re building solutions designed to work within the realities of today’s manufacturing systems.

In this live episode of Hardware to Save a Planet from San Francisco Climate Week, Dylan Garrett and his guests explore why reduction alone won’t solve the plastic crisis, and how scalable biomaterials could transform packaging, supply chains, and consumer products.

They unpack how seaweed naturally provides the flexibility and durability needed for packaging, why methane-fed bioplastics can achieve carbon-negative production, and how rigorous life cycle analysis helps sustainable materials compete commercially.

The conversation dives into supply chain resistance, composting policy barriers, infrastructure compatibility, and why climate solutions only scale when sustainability aligns with business incentives and operational realities.
Discover how biomaterials and industrial design could reshape the future of plastics without rebuilding global manufacturing from scratch.
