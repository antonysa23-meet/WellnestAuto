About
I am Her Majesty Queen Wa Arian Simone of Dawa Grand Bereby in Côte d'Ivoire, Africa. My journey is rooted in resilience, entrepreneurship, and a fearless commitment to creating opportunities for others. With a Bachelor's and MBA from Florida A&M University, I have dedicated my life to advancing empowerment, global economic inclusion, and transformative leadership.

I began my career in PR and marketing, working with billion-dollar brands such as Sony Pictures, Universal Pictures, and Walt Disney Pictures. That path led me to launch Fearless Magazine and eventually to found the Fearless Fund, the first venture capital firm built by women of color for women of color. Through the fund, I've been able to champion diverse founders at the pre-seed, seed, and Series A stages, while demonstrating to the world that investing in underrepresented entrepreneurs is both powerful and profitable.

As Queen Wa, my royal responsibilities allow me to partner with the Ivorian government and my community in Dawa Grand Bereby to focus on sustainable development and uplift under-resourced populations.

Education has always been a cornerstone of my mission. In 2022, I founded the Arian Simone Fearless Leadership Academy in Côte d'Ivoire, West Africa, to nurture and empower the next generation of leaders. I also established the Arian Simone Fearless Endowed Scholarship Fund at FAMU, which will launch in 2026 to provide long-term support for students throughout their academic journey in the School of Business and Industry.

At the core of all that I do is a commitment to creating pathways to global economic equality and inclusion for all. Ensuring equity in access to capital, education, and opportunity is not only necessary but essential for building stronger communities and a more sustainable world.

I've had the honor of sharing my insights on global stages, where I speak on economic justice, equity, and empowerment. Whether in business, philanthropy, or service, my mission remains the same: to leave a lasting legacy of justice, opportunity, and fearless leadership for future generations.

For years, I have kept my personal life extremely private. It is something I have always been intentional about. But when God does something this beautiful, you share it.

Last month, Benjamin and I got married in Ghana and it was beautiful.

There is something so profound about being found by the right person. Not just someone you love, but someone you genuinely admire. Someone you respect. Someone whose faith makes your faith stronger and whose purpose aligns so deeply with your own that building together doesn't feel like a compromise. It feels like clarity. Benjamin is that for me. My partner in faith and purpose.

Our wedding week was one of the most beautiful, meaningful, and unforgettable experiences of my life. Seven days. Two ceremonies. A community of people who traveled from near and far to celebrate with us. And a God who outdid Himself at every turn.

We began with our Royal Welcome celebration. The energy, the color, the music, the community. I kept thinking, this is what joy looks like when it is rooted in something real.

Then came our traditional engagement ceremony. Centuries of tradition. Two families becoming one. Prayers spoken over us that carried the weight of generations. That moment reminded me that marriage is so much bigger than two people. It is legacy. It is history. It is a commitment to build something that outlasts you.

And then came our Royal Wedding at Osu Castle, the former Presidential Palace and fortified House of Government under Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, the father of Ghanaian independence. Black tie. The Atlantic Ocean as our backdrop. Standing on that historic ground, in my gown, surrounded by every person who has loved and prayed for me, I felt something I still cannot fully put into words. It felt like reclamation. Like every prayer I had ever prayed was standing right there with me.

I am so grateful for every single person who celebrated with us, prayed for us, and poured into this moment.

God is faithful. Legacy is real. And the best is still to come.

My heart is still overflowing with gratitude and joy.

On Saturday, March 21, 2026, at the Accra Marriott Hotel, we officially launched Fearless Fund Africa — and what we experienced together was far more than an event. It was a bold declaration that economic freedom for women across Africa is not just possible — it is happening now.

As I stood on stage during my keynote address expressing the importance of our African female entrepreneurs and the massive opportunity that exists for investment on the continent, it was almost unimaginable to think that opposing forces tried to stop what God had in store. To think that people even sued us in federal court for alleged discrimination, but they didn’t succeed. We are still here doing the work in the United States and abroad! 

The energy in the room was unforgettable. Five brilliant African women entrepreneurs took the stage and delivered powerful pitches that showcased the immense talent and potential. Khadija Talalawa, Co-founder and CEO of Cheer Natives, emerged as the winner of the grant competition, while four outstanding finalists each received small grants. Watching these fearless women step boldly into their power was pure magic.

We were honored by the presence of inspiring cultural and global leaders. Mrs. Hawa Agyemang reminded us that women are the backbone of our economy — running businesses, creating jobs, and holding communities together — yet they continue to struggle for the capital they deserve. Ambassador Dr. Erieka Bennett applauded our efforts to bridge the empowerment gap and urged every woman to pursue her passion fearlessly.

We started our global expansion in 2022 by deploying capital to founders across various countries. The progression to Africa was quite organic. We already had 5 African co-founders in our venture portfolio plus we had deployed grants in Ghana, Botswana, Tanzania, and Côte d’Ivoire. 

Today, Fearless Fund Africa is offering micro loans to FMCG entrepreneurs. These loans are designed to empower women entrepreneurs and strengthen local economies.

To every financial supporter, to everyone who has worked for us, to everyone who has prayed for us. I say thank you. 

Let us continue to rise higher, push further, and stand fearless! 

With love and gratitude,

HM Queen WA Arian Simone

Two years ago this time, August 2, 2023 Fearless Fund was sued by Edward Blum, a conservative legal strategist, in federal court for alleged discrimination. 

1️⃣ They filed a TRO (temporary restraining order) — requesting all business cease by August 17, 2023.

2️⃣ They filed a PI (preliminary injunction) which is a stop on one of our grant programs that was specifically designed to fund Black female entrepreneurs.

3️⃣ They filed the lawsuit itself alleging we were discriminatory, using the same Civil Rights Act of 1866 that was created to protect us.

4️⃣ And yet....none of their 3 attempts worked, our case was permanently dismissed September 11, 2024. 

We were inaugural defendants of one of the most defining lawsuits of our time, the beginning of the Anti-DEI war. 
 
Black and Brown women are underfunded. 
Less than 1% of venture capital goes to businesses led by women of color.  

Fearless Fund was founded to confront this racial disparity head-on. Since our inception, we’ve invested multi-millions in over 45 companies, awarded over 500 grants, and have educated thousands of founders.

That lawsuit wasn’t just an attack on our business model. It was an attack on our belief in equity. On our commitment to justice. On our refusal to leave people behind.
 
During the court case that moment sparked a movement. We created “Fearless Freedom” rooted in the belief that economic equality is not a privilege—it’s a right!
 
Our mission is clear. And we are here to protect it.
 
Through storytelling, policy conversations, and creative programming, Fearless Freedom is reclaiming space, spotlighting the real challenges, and championing the people building powerful solutions.

Fearless Freedom is not just an initiative; it’s advocacy in action.
