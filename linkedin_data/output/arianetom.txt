About
Dr. Ariane Tom is the Founder & Managing Director of Kaleida Capital, a U.S.-based venture firm leading the development of NeuroAI—the convergence of neural data, brain–machine interfaces, and cognitive AI systems. The firm has established early exposure to category-defining platforms including Anthropic, Synchron, Paradromics, Science Corp, and Manifest Technologies (acquired by Johnson & Johnson).

Ariane brings a rare combination of deep technical training and investment track record to the NeuroAI landscape. Trained as a neuroengineer at Stanford University, she developed a systems-level understanding of how advances in neuroscience transition into scalable, computable platforms. She later worked across patent prosecution and venture capital in Silicon Valley, driving early investments linked to Nobel Prize–winning research and frontier technology companies. Her early publications on neural interfaces and bioelectronic materials continue to contribute to fundamental advances to today’s fast-growing neurotechnology market, and have been featured in Science, Nature, Scientific American, Time, and TechCrunch.

She is a frequent speaker across technology, finance, and public policy forums, offering informed perspective on neural data, brain health, and the evolving intersection of intelligence and capital. Ariane holds B.S., M.S., and Ph.D. degrees from Stanford University across Materials Science & Bioengineering, and is a member of the Milken Institute Young Leaders Circle and the NVIDIA VC Alliance.

Aurenar, Inc

 

4d • Edited • 

A patient survives a ruptured brain aneurysm. Then, a few days later, the real danger begins.

That second wave is cerebral vasospasm — a narrowing of the brain's blood vessels that can turn a survivable hemorrhage into permanent disability or death. After decades of research, there is still no FDA-approved device to prevent it.

Today, that picture is changing.

The FDA has granted Aurenar, Inc Breakthrough Device Designation for V-Link, our non-invasive, single-use neuromodulation platform, to reduce cerebral vasospasm in patients with aneurysmal subarachnoid hemorrhage in the ICU.

V-Link works through the ear — transauricular vagus nerve stimulation (taVNS) — to calm the inflammatory cascade that drives secondary brain injury. No surgery. No implant. In a WashU randomized trial, taVNS reduced moderate-to-severe vasospasm by more than 40%.

Breakthrough designation means we now work hand-in-hand with the FDA on an accelerated path to patients. It's a validation of the science — and, more so, of the urgency.
