View company: Oralucent
Oralucent

 

1mo • Edited • 

Phototherapy has a 40-year clinical track record. Your patients just don't know it yet.

Photobiomodulation (PBM) isn't an emerging science, its well documented with more than 11,100 peer-reviewed studies that have been published in the last two decades alone (PubMed, 2004–2024) — spanning wound healing, dermatology, oncology, oral health, antimicrobial applications, and post-surgical tissue recovery.

The institutional validation is unambiguous: the World Health Organization and the Multinational Association for Supportive Care in Cancer both formally recommend photobiomodulation for prevention and treatment of oral mucositis in chemotherapy patients — making it one of the few non-pharmacological interventions with WHO endorsement in oral tissue management (Zadik et al., Supportive Care in Cancer, 2019).

The periodontal evidence base is equally compelling. NIH-funded research from the University of Pennsylvania's Department of Periodontics demonstrated that LED PBM of human gingival keratinocytes challenged with viable oral microbes induced antimicrobial peptide production, enhanced epithelial cell viability, promoted ROS scavenging, and down-modulated proinflammatory activity, shifting the gingival epithelial response from inflammatory to antimicrobial (Tanum et al., Journal of Dental Research, 2024).

Separately, red LED irradiation of human gingival fibroblasts has been shown to increase cell viability, stimulate elastin gene expression and activate matrix remodeling genes (HYAL1), supporting collagen-rich tissue healing (Oral & Implantology, 2017).

What hasn't existed until now is a delivery mechanism that integrates this science into the most consistent daily health behavior your patients already perform.

Oralucent brings clinically relevant red (660nm) and blue (430nm) light therapy to every two-minute brushing session, at a clinically relevant light energy dose, AI-modulated for consistent delivery, in a format that requires zero additional patient compliance beyond what you're already asking for.
The science isn't new. The application is already here.

This week in Project Female Founder, we focused on something that is rarely talked about honestly in the startup world: The human side of fundraising.

Not the deck.
Not the valuation.
Not the term sheet.
The founder...  the human behind it all.

Over the past month, we’ve taught business readiness, financial models, funding strategies, and pitch decks. But this week’s conversation may have been the most important of all because behind every pitch is a human nervous system trying to navigate rejection, uncertainty, pressure, self-doubt, comparison, burnout... and the very real emotional weight of asking people to believe in their vision.

Christina Qi, CEO of Databento (and this year's UBS Female Founder Award Winner) shared openly about almost being removed as CEO by her own board before ultimately going on to raise a $96M Series B. And how she navigates stressful times with her "founders therapy" group and good friends not in the startup world.

Gail Wong, PCC, my APAC counterpart who leads Cohort B and is a certified coach and financial healer, spoke candidly about the “inner game” of fundraising — how deeply our money stories, self-worth, and nervous systems impact the process.

And finally, Jill Faherty Lloyd and Tanja Trattnig “TT“, UBS Client Advisors in the US and Europe, led the powerful conversations around women, wealth, investing, and the importance of founders building personal financial security alongside building their companies, including preparing for exit well before it happens.

Success is not just raising capital. Success is being able to build without losing yourself in the process.

True Wealth Ventures

7mo • Edited • 

The True Wealth Ventures cross-fund LP gathering two days ago revealed a shift in how sophisticated capital allocators think about their role, and it's worth paying attention to. 

The TWV LP community gathered at Page Home Design, a space intentionally curated for meaningful connection. The environment unlocked something important: the kind of candid, substantive conversations that don't happen in typical conference rooms. Rather than viewing themselves solely as financiers, the LPs in the room recognized three distinct value levers:

- Capital is diversifying. Beyond traditional venture checks, forward-thinking impact investors are accessing alternative capital structures: qualified charitable investments, donor-advised funds, below-market rate loans, and grant partnerships.

- Networks matter operationally. Board diversity, founder connections, market introductions. These aren't nice-to-haves anymore. They're core to LP differentiation. The LPs creating outsized returns are those systematizing their network activation, not leaving it to chance.

- Trust changes everything. The most candid conversation centered on founder vulnerability. Portfolio companies won't share real challenges with investors they don't trust. LPs who build psychological safety through structured engagement models see better outcomes. 

True Wealth Ventures is grateful to Page Home Design (pagehomedesign.com) for hosting, and to the moderators for their thoughtful facilitation. The conversations that emerged will shape how the firm deploys capital and support founders in the year ahead.… more

View Kerry Rupp’s profile
Kerry Rupp

  • 3rd+

General Partner at True Wealth Ventures, investing in early-stage women-led companies improving human and/or environmental health

1yr • Edited • 

Ten years ago, during the week leading up to the holidays, I was introduced to Sara Brand, Ph.D., and we met for lunch ... where we ended up staying and talking for three hours!  At the time, she was looking to start a VC fund focused on women-led companies and realized she'd never met another woman in venture capital, even though she'd worked in venture capital in the Bay Area herself.  As she was networking in Austin, she told several people that, and both Kyle Cox and Krishna Srinivasan said "Oh, no, there IS one - you should meet Kerry Rupp!" (So thanks for the intro, guys!)

We thought that was obviously an exaggeration, but found that - after then raising our first fund together at True Wealth Ventures - we were probably the only women General Partners in a VC fund in the state of Texas for the next several YEARS! (Note: That's being specific about the role, as there were certainly a number of women angel investors, family office principals, and women GPs in private equity and a woman VC who was wrapping up her fund.) Now, this many years later, I'm happy to report there's a bustling scene of women in VC in Austin, Dallas and Houston - including many more women General Partners. (Though the ~2% of VC funding going to all-women-led companies unfortunately hasn't changed, so we still have our work cut out for us!)

But, ten years since that auspicious meeting, Sara and I have raised two funds, with AUM close to $60M, and have invested in 18 women-led companies (https://lnkd.in/duvTRdtQ) that are improving human and/or environmental health - and just agreed to terms yesterday with our 19th company. (Watch for that announcement in the new year!)

Happy 10 year anniversary, Sara!  Here's to the next 10 (plus)!
