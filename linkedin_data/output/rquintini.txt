About
I've spent more than 15 years in venture looking for one thing: founders building product that becomes critical infrastructure for industries the world can't function without.
 
At Renegade Partners, we deeply partner with 6 to 7 founders a year, at seed and Series A, to help them build a company around a product that is working. It's a specific kind of work, and we do it on purpose.
 
The dots that connected to get me here: a lot of student debt as I got my LLM at Stanford only to discover I wanted to be a VC and pursued an MBA to get there. Started my investor career on the LP side at Stanford's endowment where I built their emerging manager venture portfolio from the ground up, learning how the world's best institutions think about capital allocation before I ever wrote my first check as a GP. Then Felicis, which I joined in its 0-1 phase, then Lux Capital, then Renegade, each stop sharpening what I was looking for: founders with enormous vision and the clarity to execute it, in markets where the cost of getting it wrong is high enough that great software becomes truly irreplaceable.
 
Couldn't be more excited about what the next 15 years will bring!

New product capabilities: priceless. Faster workflows: priceless. Automating the processes that used to take your team three hours every day: priceless. The token bill at the end of the month: not priceless.

For enterprise AI, transformation might be priceless. But tokens are not. 
Yesterday, Microsoft Build made something official that the industry has been dancing around: cost control is now a product feature, not an afterthought. They shipped Autopilots -- always-on agents that act autonomously on your behalf -- with consumption off by default and admins setting billing policies themselves. Microsoft didn't bury that in the fine print. They led with it. That's a signal.

It's not hard to understand why. Microsoft canceled most of its own Claude Code licenses after internal adoption scaled so fast the bill became untenable. Uber burned through its entire 2026 AI coding budget in four months. These are the most resourced technology companies in the world. If they hit the wall, everyone will.

The common assumption is that token prices will keep falling fast enough to make this a non-issue. I don't buy this. As usage expands from individuals to teams to enterprise workflows running millions of processes daily, aggregate spend grows faster than per-token price compression can offset. You can't out-discount scale.

The founders I'm most excited about right now have already internalized this. They treat token efficiency as a product design principle, not a finance problem. They make deliberate architecture choices about when and where to use expensive compute, and when not to. Their cost architecture is designed to get better with volume, not worse.

The founders who have figured that out are building something durable. The ones who haven't are one growth spike away from a very uncomfortable conversation.… more

Great to be part of The Wall Street Journal's coverage on forward-deployed engineers in AI. This is one of the most important strategic questions facing startups right now.

Here's what I believe: FDEs aren't inefficiency. They're strategic proximity. AI is evolving so fast that deployment has become part of product discovery. The frontier is shifting month to month, and field engineers are your sensors at the edge of what's possible. They accelerate learning, build trust, and help you understand which problems are actually worth scaling into product.

The discipline is in the evolution. Forward deployment should transform into product leverage over time. You have to productize what those engineers uncover and reduce human dependence with each deployment. If every sale needs engineers in the field, you're scaling people, not software.

Thanks to Yuliya Chernova and Marc Vartabedian for the thoughtful reporting on this trend.

I dive deeper into this from both the founder and investor lens in Renegade’s upcoming blog. Keep an eye out on Renegade Partners.
