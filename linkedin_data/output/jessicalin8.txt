About
I am a co-founder and general partner at Work-Bench, a Seed-stage enterprise software VC fund in NYC.

At Work-Bench, we invest in enterprise software & SaaS seed startups in NYC and throughout the country, with an obsession on all things 0 to 1 GTM - see our writing and templates on our Playbooks page: www.work-bench.com/playbooks.

We've also been building home and hub for enterprise tech over past 10 years here in NYC, with 200+ events a year to bring together startups and Fortune 500s - "suits and hoodies."

I lead our application-layer investments - across mental health, customer success, financial data management, recruiting, sales, and more.

I've had past lives in both the corporate and startup world: working on Agile software transformation at Cisco Systems, and supporting student startups from Harvard University in South Africa.

I am also a longtime GED instructor, and am passionate about transforming workforce development through tech.

I write an enterprise SaaS newsletter on sales tips, GTM hacks, and more here: https://jerseejess.substack.com

🎉 🎙️  SO excited to officially launch my new podcast, 'The Drafting Table'. Here's what it's all about:

After a decade of investing in founders in enterprise software, I've noticed something fascinating: 

While everyone talks about becoming a founder someday, almost no one discusses the tactical skills you need to master before making that leap.

The reality is that the best founders I've backed didn't just wake up one day and start a company.

They spent years deliberately building specific capabilities that would later become their unfair advantage.

The most successful founders often come from operator backgrounds, but they're distinguished by their mastery of specific skills that most operators never think to develop.

That's why I'm launching The Drafting Table — a podcast and content series dedicated to uncovering the tactical skills that separate successful operators-turned-founders from those stuck in the dreaming phase. 

Each week, I sit down with heads of product, VPs of sales, marketing leads, and engineering directors to craft detailed breakdowns of specific capabilities they believe founders should know before launching their companies, starting with the one and only David Politis. 

Each episode delivers specific tactics you can implement today to become a stronger founder.

hot take thursday 🌶️

most startup blurbs sound...exactly the same right now 😬 

We help X do Y.
Founder was early at Stripe.
Customers include Acme and Globex.
Grew from $0 to $1M ARR in very fast amount of time 🙃 

the problem is...everyone has those now: strong background; live product; early traction 

after reading hundreds of of these blurbs, i've realized they often tell me what has happened in the last 6 months - but they don't tell me why this company will absolutely dominate in the next 10 years
 
what i'd actually love to hear is:
👉 why everyone else is wrong
👉 why other players can't see what's coming
👉 why others have overlooked this market
👉 and why this is an $800M ARR business

ideally it should read:
"we are building X to solve Y. what everyone else is missing is Z." 

the contrarian insight is what makes me lean in: because the best seed companies don't just have traction - they have a point of view 🔭 … more

hot take thursday 🌶️ 

most Seed founders bury the lede...!

when fundraising, it’s tempting to start with what you’ve done to date:
“we got to $X ARR in Y months”
 “we signed these customers”
 “we launched this product”

but it’s not where i’d actually start the pitch

my one actually useful fundraising pro tip that i will shout from the roof:

*****start with your ending first!!!!!*****

the best Seed pitches go in this order:
1. “we are setting out to rethink how [massive 10-year problem] is done.”
2. “to get there, we’re starting with [Act 1]. here’s what we’ve proven so far in the last 6 months.”
3. “from there, we will then go on to tackle [Act 2] and [Act 3]…”

remember: even at Seed, investors are not only betting on what you've done to date...they’re betting on how big your ambition is and the future you're trying to create. 

so make sure you're going big!!!… more

i can usually tell within 5 minutes whether I'm investing in a founder 👀 

(that doesn't mean i'm always right 🙃 )

but over the last decade, my best-performing founders have been able to articulate 2 things within the first 5 minutes:

1. velocity - moving fast, shipping, learning, momentum with early customers and pipeline 🚀 

2. 💥 💥  a big, ambitious vision - right from the jump 💥💥  

in today's world - where there are more companies being started than ever - it's feels rare to hear a vision that is different

it used to be "good ideas don't matter. execution is everything."

now, it's very clear: good ideas matter again

it's a reminder that at Seed - we are fundamentally backing **ambition**

most Seed founders today are focused on their current ARR and growth rate and how to hit $2M ARR - but not enough are articulating how they're going to get to $800M+ in ARR in the next 10+ years

check out what i say on this and more on Not Another CEO pod with David Politis - such an honor to be on!!! ✨ 

i'm so lucky to call David longtime friend, mentor, coach - to myself, Work-Bench, and so many of our founders. thank you for all you do for our community David!!… more

this might be the least hot take thursday post…but it still happens pretty frequently so figured it's still a post

12 years in, i still get on 1st calls with founders without the opportunity to see a few slides of an overview deck

my goal everyday = i *hope* i can come to a 30 min intro call with even 1-2 smart questions 🤞 

if i don't have a deck, i have to ask the most elementary questions, that i know are just as painful for founders to answer and repeat over and over (i see it in your eyes)

sure there is risk of sending an investor an overview deck - “what if they share it?” “what if someone copies it?”

but in reality, a few slides shouldn't give away all your secret sauce :)

our best convos happen when we've had a chance to see a deck - and it allows us to get into the real, real talk faster...

thus...we’re testing something new at Work-Bench! 🧪  

→ we're just going to be way more upfront and transparent about what we actually want to spend time on in a 1st meeting with a founder...more on that next week...! 👀

🌶️  hot take thursday

most seed pitches fail on one question:

***why now?***

we’re meeting a ton of impressive companies right now but this is the question we keep coming back to at Work-Bench:

what tailwinds/market pressures/urgency is driving a buyer to act TODAY - not punt to next quarter or the one after? ⏰

because the reality is just because AI can make something more efficient isn't enough - every enterprise has 100+ things they could optimize (and most require some form of painful change management 🫠)

what actually moves deals fast → clear, immediate, direct revenue lift

the best AI pitches are painfully simple:

💰 we help you make / recover / drive $X 💰

in addition to: save time / reduce headcount / streamline workflows

if you’re a founder, ask yourself - how does my product drive revenue right NOW (and also be blissfully easy to adopt/onboard/setup)?… more
