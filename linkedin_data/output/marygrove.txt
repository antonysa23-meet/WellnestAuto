About
Mary Grove is Managing Partner of Bread and Butter Ventures. She brings two decades of leadership experience in technology, early stage investing, and startup ecosystem growth. She began her career working on the Google IPO, and went on to lead new business development partnerships, negotiating early stage product and technology deals worldwide. Mary then served as the founding director of Google for Startups, leading the company’s global efforts to support entrepreneurs in over 100 countries. 

After her 15 year career at Google, Mary worked as an investment partner at Revolution’s Rise of the Rest Seed Fund where she led dozens of investments in a range of sectors including healthcare, enterprise software, and fintech. She also built the fund’s portfolio support platform and built a network of over 150 mentors and partners to guide startup growth.

She is the Co-founder and Executive Director of Silicon North Stars, a nonprofit that she and her husband Steve founded in 2013 to help young Minnesotans from underserved communities pursue careers in tech.  Mary serves on the boards of the Bush Foundation where she chairs the Investment Committee and serves as a Trustee of The Minneapolis Foundation. She is on the Board of Directors of HealthPartners. She has served on the boards of Vital Voices, The Techstars Foundation, and the Stanford Alumni Association. She earned her BA and MA from Stanford University. Mary and her family moved from Silicon Valley to Minneapolis in 2018 and she loves cooking, hiking, and getting to know Minnesota through the eyes of her nine-year-old twins. Mary's bread and butter is helping organizations build teams and infrastructure to operate at scale, building partnerships, and home cooked Thai recipes.

Today was a special day, June 30th. My Aunt Bennie celebrated 30 years of working in the deli department at Target in Davenport, Iowa. She was one of the first employees the year the store opened in 1996. We marked the day with a special visit to the Target HQ in downtown Minneapolis where senior leadership thanked her personally for her long tenure!

This is significant to me for many reasons. My Aunt Bennie moved from Thailand to Davenport, Iowa in her early 20s to take care of my sister Annie and me so that my mom could work full time as an operating room nurse while my Dad was in chiropractic school. Mom supported our entire family, and so did Bennie. She met and married the love of her life in Davenport and remained there after the rest of our family moved to CA. I owe her such a debt of gratitude as she helped shape me into who I am today.

In the last decade, we have lost her beloved husband and both my parents, and Aunt Bennie is the elder of our family and a second parent to us. It meant the world to have her recognized on her big day but a company that we have loved for so many years. Every VC firm has their own unique process for due diligence. We're refined ours over the years at Bread and Butter Ventures and now have a three step process that I broke down in my latest blogpost. The fundraising process can often feel opaque and unclear, and we want to make it as transparent (and enjoyable!) as possible.

Step 1: Deep dive with the lead partner. This is where we really get to know you — your story, your vision, how you think, how you've gotten to where you are. We're not running through a checklist. We're having a real conversation over the course of a few meetings.

Step 2: Meet the other General Partner. If things are moving forward, you'll meet our other GP. This is intentional. We want you to feel the full team dynamic, and we want both of us to have a direct relationship with you. And later in our process, you’ll meet the whole team. An investment at B&B is a partnership with the whole firm — not just one person.

Step 3: Tailored diligence. This looks different for every deal. It typically includes some combination of customer calls, reference checks, and a conversation with one of our enterprise partners. Our enterprise partner network, the Bread & Butter Innovation Circle, is one of the things we're most proud of at B&B — we know that these enterprise relationships can help accelerate go-to-market and we hope this part of the diligence will be beneficial in both directions.

The in-person meeting. We ask to meet in person before we close an investment. This isn't about formality — it's about the relationship. We believe we're both signing up for a decade-long partnership. We want to make sure the energy and alignment are there in a way that a Zoom call simply can't fully capture. And we want you to feel that too.
