4mo • Edited • 


This past Saturday, the Atlanta Lacrosse Invitational brought eight D1 programs together for four back-to-back games—a full day of women’s lacrosse, broadcast on ESPN+. 

I wouldn’t usually have this circled on my calendar, but Fielding Kidd Jamieson was on the mic for two matchups.  So for the first time ever I watched a lacrosse game—while Fielding, for the first time ever, color commentated.

I was blown away by the play on the field—and by Fielding. Her color commentary was researched and rich with strategy and story, giving each athlete her due. Check out the fast sequence in the first 14 seconds of the Army West Point vs. Florida State game.  

Here is the week leading up to the games- what you don’t hear in Fielding’s voice:

Monday & Tuesday: an all-nighter with her little guy—snuggles and sniffles— coupled with disassembling and reassembling a crib

Wednesday: flight to New York, going immediately into back-to-back meetings

Thursday: presentation on the landscape of investing in women’s sports for How Women Invest on behalf of Tipt Ventures

Friday: flight home, going immediately into research and prep, capped off with a midnight shift of snuggles and sniffles with her bigger little guy

Saturday: Fielding’s premiere—and she executed perfectly.

Women don’t have to be all things. Sometimes, they just are.

Happy National Girls and Women in Sports Day #NGWSD

Grateful to have spent the day on Capitol Hill alongside the Women's Sports Foundation’s advocacy team as a WSF Board Trustee—meeting with Senators, House representatives, and their staff to discuss the issues shaping the future of women’s sports. It was truly a privilege to stand in service for girls and women everywhere who deserve access, equity, and the opportunity to play.

Deep appreciation for the trailblazers who paved the way to this moment. A special shoutout to our Founder, Billie Jean King, whose words rang especially true today: “Pressure is a privilege.”

There is still so much work ahead—but today left me encouraged. A few key takeaways:
• There is still a genuine willingness across this country to listen, learn, and engage—across differences.
 • While perspectives may feel far apart, finding common language (and not getting stuck on nomenclature) can move us closer to real solutions.
 • The next generation of leaders is already in the room. We met inspiring young professionals supporting today’s policymakers who will soon be shaping the future themselves.

Progress happens when advocacy meets action—and today was a reminder that both still matter deeply.
