About
I am an early stage investor and operator based in NYC + Mexico City. My background is deeply rooted in entrepreneurship; I own a business development consulting firm where we spearhead sales distribution and growth strategies for beauty and wellness CPG brands. Some of my current and past clients include: Topicals, Veracity, SolaWave, Supergreat, FUR, Peachy, Duradry, Stryx, HIGHR, Blume, SILKE London, Golde, Aevi, Mad Rabbit etc. 

I thrive being in the early stage ecosystem. I have been first employee and several startups and have first hand experience in scaling 50+ co's. 

I received my JD from Cardozo law and have angel invested in several portfolio companies ranging from fem tech, consumer tech, fintech, future of work, CPG fintech etc. I’m genuinely passionate about working with like-minded founders and growing businesses from the ground up in a meaningful way by adding significant value at a very quick pace. I mentor founders and advise companies on an ongoing basis.

You’ve heard of 10X engineers. You’ve heard of value-add investors. Now let’s talk about value-subtract investors…

It’s a label that Kara LaForgia, Esq. and I came up with during a two-hour car ride to the airport while stuck in traffic.

Kara and I are both early-stage, consumer-focused investors who obsess over providing value to the companies we back. 

We make customer intros. We help find new hires. We provide strategic advice. We even coach founders through the fundraising process.

Very few investors go to these lengths to support their portfolio founders. After writing the check, many investors are net-neutral, at best.

But, there is also the dreaded value-subtract investor. 

This is the investor who writes the smallest check and demands the most in return. This is the investor who constantly asks favors of their portfolio founders. This is the investor who tries to use their position to influence the company’s strategy. This is the investor who is willing to force an early sale in order to get their liquidation preference and target return at the expense of all other shareholders.

On that ride to the airport a few weeks ago, Kara revealed that these “value-subtract” investors have influenced her approach to raising capital for her own fund.

She brought up some great points — For GPs, choosing your LPs is just as important as choosing your investments.

Those value-subtract LPs will damage your ability to execute and, even worse, impact the founders you invest in. 

I think Kara has a great set of requirements when assessing potential LPs: People who have invested in funds before and people who have worked with GPs (or investors) in some capacity before.

It’s never been easier to stand out amongst your peers: Hustle your ass off and be value add!

Co-Founder + CEO, Dandi Fertility | Building brands + experiences that connect on a deep human level

2yr • Edited • 

It's hard to find the right combination of words to express the emotion of launching Dandi Fertility into the world. No matter how much conviction you have in a creation, the journey to building a startup is never without moments of anxiety, uncertainty and fear. In those tough moments, I always came back to one thing: the why.

We often get asked the problem Dandi is trying to solve. 

The physical pain and emotional distress of IVF? ✅ 

The personalized care and guidance that is so critically-needed during intense moments of fertility struggle? ✅ 

The fact there hasn't been a single brand in which those impacted by infertility can feel seen or heard? ✅ 

But at its core, I believe the why -- what Dandi is solving for -- is something deeper; something more universally human: vulnerability.

In connecting with hundreds of awe-inspiring fertility patients who let us into some of their toughest moments, a common thread quickly emerged -- an overwhelming sense of how much was at stake. Finances, relationships, mental health, and of course, the unimaginable uncertainty of being able to bring a child into the world.

For most, the whole of this experience was far more devastating than the sum of its parts, amounting to a lived experience that felt like a perpetual state of discomfort. A lack of safety. Vulnerability in its deepest sense. So many were being suspended into the unknown, asked to get by with so little support, and to do so with no timestamp of when things might get better.

That is the why.

Dandi Fertility is here to support millions of people through their most vulnerable moments of uncertainty. To bring care, comfort and reassurance to the parts of the journey where it has been sorely missing. To bring fertility struggles out of the shadows for too many suffering in silence. And above all else, to serve as a reminder that even in the hardest and most vulnerable moments, you're not alone.

In the coming days, I hope to expand on the topic of vulnerability, including my own, and to share much more about the incredible journey to build Dandi. But for now, I must express deep gratitude to every human who has helped to bring Dandi into the world, including...
