The real risk is hidden in the fine print.

2️⃣ Tag-along rights — Protects you if others exit.

3️⃣ Redemption rights — Investors can pull cash back.

4️⃣ No-shop clause — Limits your leverage mid-process.

5️⃣ Confidentiality — Controls what you can disclose.

Bottom line?
The terms you sign today define the outcomes you live with later.

I should have talked about the value of mentorship when Maureen Stancik Boyce, asked me for a quote for her new book.

Instead, I wrote about conviction and navigating conflicting advice.

But the reality is, I wouldn’t have developed either without strong mentors.

I met Maureen while I was at MIT, where she was my assigned mentor through the Sandbox Fund. She encouraged me early, long before Base Operations had real traction, and wrote the first personal check into our bank account. Good Growth Capital followed and has been a consistent partner in every round since.

She also pushed us in very practical ways. One example: she connected us to Microsoft’s Make What’s Next program, which ultimately led to securing our first patent. That kind of support compounds.

Her book, Launching from the Lab (MIT Press), reflects what it actually takes to turn ideas into companies. Not just the technical side, but the judgment, persistence, and support systems behind it.

Grateful to be included, and even more grateful for her role in my journey.

I’m excited that our book “Launching from the Lab: A guide to building a deep-tech startup” is launching on Feb 10.  My co-author, Lita Nelsen (former head of MIT's Licensing office for 20+ years) and I poured multiple decades of experience into it (helped and inspired by Sophie Hagerty and Amy Salzhauer of Good Growth Capital).  Lita and I are also both mentors in the MIT Sandbox Innovation Fund Program helping students as they begin their entrepreneurship endeavors. 

Lita has deep experience in helping Massachusetts Institute of Technology and other universities spin out companies based on research-derived inventions. She is the former President of AUTM (Association of University Technology Managers) advising other universities.  Lita and I figured that combining my experience investing in start-ups with her deep experience could result in something practical and useful.
