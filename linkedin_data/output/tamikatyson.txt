About
I'm the Co-Founders of TGC Impact Growth Fund, a fund that invests growth capital in diverse and women-led or managed companies in the Southern Region, and the President of Impactus Consulting, a risk management advisory company. 

As an industry thought leader and executive partner, I’ve supported highly strategic business initiatives across the globe. I am pragmatic and thorough in my due diligence which has proved highly valuable in response to the tremendous pressures in the energy industry.

With a supportive and natural leadership style, I am highly intentional about establishing the right culture from day one. I believe in empowering teams and creating space for continuous learning and growth. I am equally passionate about fostering community engagement among cross-functional groups to improve synergy and results. 

My background includes board-level leadership experience, speaking engagements, and a master's and bachelor's degrees in business administration with an emphasis on finance and economics from Sam Houston State University.

Most people think a market crisis ends when the panic stops.

That is usually when the next crisis starts forming.

In my latest piece, I walk through a twelve-stage framework for how market crises repeat across the investment cycle, credit cycle, and political cycle. The pattern is not identical each time, but the structure keeps showing up: fear, recovery, complacency, mispriced risk, leverage, overconfidence, blowoff, crash, intervention, and reset.

The problem is that most investors can only see through Stage 4.

By then, the crisis feels over. Markets are recovering. Policy support is working. Risk appetite is coming back.

But the dangerous part often begins after that.

That is when spreads compress, underwriting loosens, capital chases yield, leverage rebuilds, and the conditions for the next correction quietly assemble.
I mapped the full cycle from 1971 through today, including stagflation, the dot-com bubble, the housing crisis, the post-2008 reset, the pandemic shock, the 2023 banking crisis, and the current moment.

This is not a prediction. It is a diagnostic.

The goal is not to call the exact date of the next correction. The goal is to understand where we are in the cycle before the market forces everyone else to see it.
