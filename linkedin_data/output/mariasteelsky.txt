About
As a founding and managing partner at SteelSky Ventures, I invest in companies that improve access, care, and outcomes in healthcare. With an MBA in Finance from NYU Stern School of Business and a BS in Information Systems from Wake Forest University, I have a strong background in finance and technology. 
In addition to my work at SteelSky Ventures, I am an Eisenhower Justice Fellow and a member of various boards, where I support founders and the venture ecosystem. My goal is to create positive change in the world through innovative and impactful investments.

Yesterday, I had the incredible privilege of hosting the First Lady of The United States on behalf of SteelSky Ventures and The City of Atlanta, to discuss the first-ever White House Initiative on Women’s Health Research, an effort led by Dr. Jill Biden and the White House Gender Policy Council. 

This groundbreaking program was explicitly designed to catalyze innovation and address the significant gaps in women’s health research. According to a 2022 McKinsey report, Only 1% of Healthcare research and innovation is invested in female-specific conditions beyond oncology. 

Dr. Biden is taking action to rectify these research disparities, which have the power to unleash transformative potential for the future of women’s healthcare at global scale. We were joined by a phenomenal group of industry pioneers, who shared how the collective efforts of our respective organizations are working to drive impact and better outcomes in women’s health. 

Thank you to all the corporate, civic and social leaders who supported the event, including Mayor Andre Dickens, Donnie Beamer Jr., CFA, Charlinda Catchings, Howard Palefsky, Cisco, The Office of the First Lady and my new friends at the Secret Service.
