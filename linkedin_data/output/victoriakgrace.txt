Neuron Factory

What happens when you combine the architect of the hardware powering LinkedIn's Economic Graph with the engineer who helped convince people to trust their lives to a Waymo driving at 65 mph?

You get a team that's not afraid of hard problems.

Neuron Factory CEO Zaid Kahn spent his career building infrastructure at massive scale—LinkedIn, Microsoft Azure, the AI training systems behind OpenAI. He deployed 1.5 gigawatts of datacenter capacity. And he lost count of how many times he had to deliver the same message to his executive committee: we're six months delayed. The reason? Construction. Always construction. That frustration became a mission.

CTO Salil Pandit came from Waymo, where the challenge wasn't just engineering—it was context. An autonomous vehicle has to understand everything happening around it and make the right call, every time. Preconstruction faces the same fundamental problem: from the first phone call to handoff, context gets lost, institutional knowledge walks out the door, and good teams make bad bids because critical information lives in someone else's head.

At LinkedIn, Zaid helped build one of the world's largest knowledge graphs. That experience proved what's possible when you connect information at scale.

Now that expertise is focused on construction. Neuron Factory is building an AI-native platform for preconstruction—one that captures context, preserves institutional knowledge, and helps teams make better decisions faster.

It's time to extend the grid to outer space. After 3 years in stealth, we’re ready to show the world what we’ve been building at Overview Energy.

We’re building satellites that turn uninterrupted sunlight in space into directable energy on Earth. It’s the pragmatic path to space solar energy, completely safe and optimized for the real-world constraints of cost and infrastructure: a wide-beam, geosynchronous, near-infrared system that delivers energy to existing solar projects on Earth.

Our satellites will be the first power plants that can move, directing energy across regions in seconds and across continents in minutes.
