About
Venture Capitalist, Serial Entrepreneur, Physicist, Innovation Hacker, Idealist. Mentor in women in technology, the art of balance, growth, and overcoming odds

Originally from Ukraine, I moved to the US to earn my PhD in Physics from the University of Pennsylvania. That experience opened doors to strategic roles at McKinsey and Motorola.  It turned out that my being trained as a physicist is phenomenal albeit unusual launchpad into the world of business and VC.  

My favorite thing in the world is meeting founders with an unusual spark and an unrelenting drive to make their vision a reality. They already live in the future, and we get to co-create the future with them.  As the Managing Partner of Sputnik ATX VC, I'm growing the fund with a focus on investing in maker-founders outside Silicon Valley. So far, we’ve supported 68+ companies across different industries, from fintech to fashion supply chains. Global reach with the epicenter in Austin TX

I am convinced that talent is everywhere, but opportunities are unevenly distributed. We at SputnikATX VC are betting on this insight by seeking out the founders that are geographically or demographically overlooked.

Exploring decision-making biases in VC & Entrepreneurship

2mo • 

How are elite VC firms like Sequoia Capital, Kleiner Perkins, and Khosla Ventures able to catch the startup outliers that’ll define entire eras?

I spent years collecting data on their Investment Committee processes.

Here are the three take-aways from my research:

1️⃣ Their processes (IC Memo, voting rules, IC members) are optimized to reward conviction, not consensus 

2️⃣ They favor the “champions rule:” one partner can decide to do a deal even if other Investment Committee members disagree

3️⃣ They built a culture where dissent is welcome, internal politics minimized, and failure tolerated 

The objective is not to make decision-making more comfortable, but to avoid missing the few companies that matter. 

“The truth is not in the middle where everybody agrees.” - Alfred Lin

I’m sharing these best practices in a series of small, in-person workshops for VC professionals in major US tech hubs.
