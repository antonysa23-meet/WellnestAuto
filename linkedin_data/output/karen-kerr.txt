memQ

2mo • 

Big news for the memQ team. 🎖️

DARPA has selected memQ to develop a hardware- and network-aware quantum compiler as part of its Heterogeneous Architectures for Quantum (HARQ) program.

HARQ is built on a clear premise: no single qubit modality excels at everything. The path to utility-scale quantum computing runs through a "interconnected, heterogeneous quantum systems" architecture, leveraging different qubit types connected across quantum networks. The program aims to cut resource demands by a factor of 1,000 through this approach, with distributed compiler and component technology that can provide the high-fidelity interconnects that make it possible.

That's exactly the problem memQ was built to solve. 🔬

We'll lead a multi-organization team including qBraid and researchers from MIT, Yale, and the University of Chicago to deliver a qubit-agnostic compiler that maps and partitions optimized logical circuits across heterogeneous QPUs connected via quantum networking links.

This builds directly on our xDQC work announced at NVIDIA GTC, and on the xQNA portfolio we've been developing since day one.

Honored to be part of this. The work continues. 🚀

Advanced Modular Enterprise Systems for Autonomy

8mo • 

📢 Today we’re announcing that Composabl is now AMESA.

AMESA stands for Advanced Modular Enterprise Systems of Autonomy. The new brand showcases how we're pioneering agentic orchestration as well as building a true #AI proving ground where agents can practice performing high-value enterprise tasks. 

Eight years ago, I started building intelligent agents that could make complex decisions in real industrial environments. Not just analyze, but act.

This work has never been theoretical for me.

It’s come from long days in plants and control rooms, watching how real systems behave when things don’t go according to plan.

When I launched Composabl, I made a deliberate choice to start in the toughest place possible: industrials. 

If autonomous agents could deliver measurable ROI there – optimizing yield, improving efficiency and quality, and earning the trust of expert operators – they could work anywhere.

But I’ve seen multi-agent systems work for a huge variety of enterprise use cases as diverse as: machine and control for manufacturing, supply chain and logistics, robotics, retail, banking, and even commodity trading.

So, as AMESA, we’re expanding that same engineering-first approach to give enterprises across industries a proving ground for agent systems to practice and learn before they’re deployed.

The future of AI belongs to those who prove it works.

AMESA is the proving ground. I can't wait to see what you build with it.

We’re launching two new products today as well.

✅ AMESA Agent Orchestration Studio ✅ 
Our no-code platform and supporting APIs serve as a command center for designing, managing, and optimizing teams of agents. 

Fortune 500 companies have used this product for over a year to build agent teams with specialized skills, and organize hierarchies with orchestrators managing other agents, all supported by built-in feedback.
 
❇️ AMESA Agent Cloud ❇️
We’ve formalized our RL training infrastructure, and embedded LLMs, that can run on just about any major cloud platform or on premises to train autonomous agents. It’s a secure proving ground where intelligent agents can learn, test, and improve

Connect data, attach digital twins, or use simulations to create realistic environments where agents experiment safely and learn from feedback.

✳️ AMESA Assist Agents ✳️ 
We've added LLM-based agents that you can build into your team of agents to interact with the humans on your team. They can explain what the team of agents is doing, and why. They can also provide insights and decision-support recommendations. 
 
In addition to communication agents, we’ve built AMESA Assist Agents. They act as built-in guides that help teams configure and run agentic systems without requiring deep AI expertise. Assist agents simplify setup, design, and performance review while bridging the gap between technical and operational teams.… more

📣 Big News! memQ has won two U.S. government awards to advance critical technologies that support our mission of building scalable quantum networks.

🚀 NASA award for Integrated Photonics Control 
• memQ will build on its existing technology portfolio for next-gen photonic integrated circuits (PICs) that use advanced materials
• Commercial applications will include the controlled delivery of light to qubits, enabling a range of use cases from board-to-board communication to data center networks to satellite implementations

⚓ Navy award for Cryogenic Modulators 
• Working with the Navy, memQ will build on its existing technology portfolio for PIC-based control systems that can operate at cryogenic temperatures
• Commercial use cases will include quantum and non-quantum applications.
