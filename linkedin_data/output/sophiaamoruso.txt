About
I'm the Founder & Managing Partner at Trust Fund, an early-stage venture fund backing software companies that help people and teams start, manage, and scale businesses. Our founders are killers building painkillers. 

Our LPs include Marc Andreessen, Jason Calacanis, David Sacks, Jeremy Liew, Andrew Chen, Chris Dixon, Jeff Jordan, Ev Williams, Paris Hilton, and Phil Hellmuth. 

Previously wrote an 18-week New York Times Bestseller, #GIRLBOSS, which was adapted into a scripted Netflix series that premiered on Netﬂix, produced by Charlize Theron.  

Started fashion e-tailer Nasty Gal at 22 years old and bootstrapped it to $30 million profitably, raised $60 million from Index Ventures and Thrive Capital, then scaled it to over $100 million in revenue. 

Went on the victory lap and was featured on the cover of Forbes magazine's 2016 America's Richest Self-Made Women issue (returned the dress I wore in the photo) and featured in the Fortune 40 under 40, Forbes 30 under 30, Inc. 30 under 30, and Fast Company’s Most Creative People in Business lists. 

My interests include drinking coffee on an empty stomach, choosing the best seats on commercial aircraft, and harvesting my hard-won experience for the next generation of founders.

Congratulations to Alex Yancher and the Passport team on the news today that Global-e has acquired the company for $425M. Nine years of patient, operational work building a global shipping network that actually performs and earns the trust of brands and 3PLs around the world.

International shipping may sound unsexy, and it is. But when I met Alex in 2017, what he envisioned for Passport was sexy, at least in comparison to the decade of pain I'd experienced at Nasty Gal, with lost packages, a lack of transparency, and awful service from international shipping providers. 

I had the money to invest, but wasn't actively writing angel checks at the time, so Alex and his co-founder Aaron Schwartz gave me advisory shares at a few pennies each.

Today, those advisory shares became life-changing money. And not to sound greedy, but god, do I wish I'd invested when I had a chance.

This is the version of investing I believe in, and the one I try to practice at Trust Fund. You meet someone early. You make yourself useful in whatever way the company actually needs — intros, hard feedback, opinions they don't always want to hear. You stay in touch through the years. And if the founder is great, the market is there, and the product removes their pain, everyone wins.

Alex is a great founder. He let me in at the beginning, followed through for nearly a decade, and built something that genuinely earned this outcome. 

Thank you, Alex, and congratulations!

Plot just raised $10M.

I’m thrilled for Megan Duong and her team. And yes, a little smug, because Trust Fund backed this one early.

Here’s what she built and why I wrote the check.

Most of what people buy, wear, eat, and believe now lives inside social video. Trillions of frames a year. Almost none of it tagged. None of it legible. For a decade brands have been guessing at a thing sitting right in front of them, unreadable.

Plot made it legible. The first AI Video Social Intelligence platform that doesn’t rely on tags at all. It watches video the way a person would, at a scale no person ever could.

I spent the better part of two decades on the other side of this. Building brands, building media, trying to read culture in real time with tools that always lagged a season behind. So when Megan showed me what Plot could see, I didn’t need the rest of the deck. I’d wanted this my whole career. Nobody had built it.

The other tell: every team wanted in, and every team used it differently. Community, influencer, insights. One intelligence layer, three completely different jobs. That’s not a feature. That’s infrastructure.

Now they’re turning seeing into doing: growing community, sourcing creators end to end, handing every team its own agentic analyst. Fenty, lululemon, CAVA, Pepsi, Visa already on board. ~3.8x in a year.

Megan has spent her whole career in social. She’s building the tool she always wished she had. That’s the type of founder I invest in, and my entire thesis — invest in things I wish I had when I was building Nasty Gal. .

Congratulations to Megan and the team!

If you run insights at a consumer brand, Pogo is worth a look.

The hard truth about most research is that it cannot actually tell you whether the person answering ever bought your product. You ship something new, you want to know what real buyers think, so you run a study, and the panel fills up with people who clicked the right box to qualify and collect the incentive. Some of them are bots, some are professional survey takers, and a lot of them have never touched your category. Best case you get noise, worst case you end up changing a product based on a stranger.

Pogo just launched the fix, and it is the only AI researcher that lets you talk to verified buyers of any product, verified by what they actually purchased rather than what they claim. You can reach the person who bought the SKU you put on shelves last week, or the high value customer who just churned to a competitor, and you can reach them while the memory is still fresh. Hundreds of real interviews, synthesized in hours instead of months.

That is the difference between feedback you hope is real and feedback you can confidently take to your CEO.

Congrats to Dom Wong and the entire Pogo team. $32M in, and this is the first of many launches coming in 2026.

If you have ever presented a finding and quietly wondered who you were actually talking to, go look at what they built.
