About
I bring my startup operating experience to venture investing and fund management.  I co-founded the venture firm First Row Partners and I am a Venture Partner at 2048 Ventures.  Previously, I held leader roles as a angel and fund manager within.angel groups and the Seattle Angel Conference. I teach a hands-on, graduate level, angel investing class at the University of Washington Foster School of Business. 

In April 2020, I co-founded The $1k Project, a volunteer collective that directs cash relief to vulnerable families impacted by the pandemic.  As of December 2020, we have helped $3.3M reach 1,100  to families in need.  This peer to peer giving model has inspired a case study and and is seen as an innovation in collective giving. 
 
My career began in management consulting for large enterprise systems integrations.  Since the  late 1990s to I  have been "lynchpin" employee or co-founder 4x.  As a generalist that combines curiosity with critical thinking, I've enjoyed advising and supporting founders and emerging investors along the way.  I believe that developing the right framing questions is the key to unlocking and evaluating new ideas and approaches.

As an startup ecosystem leader, mentor startups and entrepreneurs at University of Washington, Princeton, Santa Clara University and more. 

I am also passionate about civic and community service, the visual arts, and tennis.

Today we're launching Hanno AI — a fleet of autonomous research agents built specifically for private credit and private equity. 

NVIDIA just put OpenClaw on the main stage at GTC. Jensen Huang called it "the operating system for personal AI."

He's right. But the harder question is what that means for enterprises — and that's a problem NVIDIA's hardware stack wasn't designed to solve.

Finance teams want autonomous agents, but they can't deploy them without audit trails, network isolation, and customized workflows. And they're not going to rip out their infrastructure to do it. Luckily we've been working on a solution that is purpose-built to solve these problems. 

Last week we released Exoclaw 🦀 -- our open source framework for running autonomous agents in enterprise environments. Any tech stack. Full compliance controls. No migration required.

Building on that, we created Hanno — our autonomous agent platform built specifically for private credit, private equity, and corporate strategy.

Hanno runs continuously across your portfolio. It researches to bring you new insights. It surfaces the signals that would otherwise stay buried until it's too late. Using our predictive models, it alerts you to risk before it becomes problematic. Every step is auditable, and every output is verifiable. Hanno is built for the compliance officer and the CFO, not just the engineer.

The analysis that could change your next decision is already in your data. Hanno finds it.

We're opening early access to a small group of firms who want to shape how this works in practice.
