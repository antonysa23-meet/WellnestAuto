Part 2: AI on Quicksand: AI-driven Debt and the Fragmentation Trap

Toan Huynh on LinkedIn • 2 min read

14 reactions · 1 comment14 · 1 comment

Post

AI adoption isn’t about chasing use cases—it’s about identifying the right processes to transform.
One challenge I often hear leadership teams issue to their business leaders is to find a single, high-impact use case where AI can meaningfully assist your business. You don't need 10 - you need just transformative process that moves the needle meaningfully for the business.  Finding that process is hard but not impossible - this is where our team Naitiv Partners comes in.  As someone who sat at multiple innovation curves before, it always comes down to choosing what problems need fixing.  
In regulated industries like insurance, that’s harder than it sounds. It requires both:
A clear process lens (where are the bottlenecks, manual effort, and decision friction?)
A practical understanding of the tooling and orchestration needed to enable change
That’s where we’ve been spending time with clients, where we bring together:
Business and process leaders
AI advisory and platform experts
Client functional and business unit leaders with goals for their business 
In that session, we:
Identify high-value use cases grounded in real workflows
Rapidly prototype concepts to accelerate learning and decision-making
Begin defining the governance and guardrails required for enterprise-scale implementation

Because the real inflection point isn’t just “adding AI”—it’s rethinking how work gets done, where AI can augment it, and how to do that responsibly at scale.
Most leaders are already feeling this: we’re at a crossroads. The organizations that move fastest  in this innovation curve aren’t experimenting more—they’re focusing better.
