About
Marisa has more than 26 years experience in tech as a VC investor, board member and operator working for companies like SAP, Microsoft and Workday.  As Founder and Managing Partner of ALIAVIA Ventures and Founder of ELEVACAO, Marisa invests in female founded tech startups across the U.S. and Australia.  Investments include; Loupe (Exit to Stingray (TSX: RAY.A; RAY.B)), InsightWise, Human Health, GoFigr, HowToo, Eugene, Othelia, Neighbourlytics, Digiphy and Ginko.

Beginning her career in enterprise software sales in 2001, Marisa went on to build the Microsoft Partner Development Centre for Australia and Southeast Asia. Marisa was responsible for building the business from concept to facilitating programs for the Microsoft Dynamics partners that focused on accelerating business growth and profitability. Marisa's efforts resulted in customer acquisition targets exceeding 230% and received the Microsoft Global Best-Practice award.

Whilst managing the Southern Region channel business at SAP Australia, Marisa conceptualized and built the SAP Partner Sales Academy, a program supporting partners to source, train and deploy SAP-qualified and revenue-ready salespeople into the channel. Marisa's efforts resulted in receiving an Innovation Excellence Award for developing the SAP Sales Academy program, growing the customer base from 140 to 850 in 3 years and a 100% Sales Club Award.

Marisa left big corporate in 2015 to pursue her passion for early-stage businesses.  Whilst living in New York, Marisa founded ELEVACAO a global pre-accelerator for women tech founders.  Fast forward to today and ELEVACAO has empowered 175 women across the U.S. and Australia, who have raised $120m+ and produced 5 Exits.

In 2020, Marisa co-founded ALIAVIA Ventures, a California-based venture capital firm investing in early-stage female founders building world-changing technology companies in the United States and Australia.

Marisa frequently speaks at events as MC, keynote speaker, judge or moderator, including; SXSW Austin & Sydney, TechCrunch Disrupt New York and San Francisco, Startup Grind APAC, Microsoft, Dreamforce, SAP, Macquarie Bank, Commonwealth Bank, CISCO, Australia-Japan Business Conference, Mums & Co an IAG business, FIRST Robotics, Harvard Club NYC.… more

8mo • 

I didn’t start Human Health because I wanted to be a founder. I started it because I was terrified. Angry. Desperate for answers.

My son Leo was unwell, and our healthcare system - the one meant to help - couldn’t see the full picture. I was trying to piece together his health on my own, and I have never felt more powerless.

When Georgia V. and I met at Canva, where we were both product leaders, we discovered a shared experience - watching the people we love struggle with chronic health conditions. It sparked a shared conviction that there had to be a better way.

Which is why our announcement today, that Human Health has just secured $8.5M AUD to take our precision health platform to the world, is more than just a funding milestone.

It’s proof that something born from fear and frustration can become a force for hope, for change and for helping all those who’ve felt unseen by the system.

The day my son started to receive the correct treatment due to the health insights I’d gathered on Human Health was a day that changed everything for me. The concept of personalized healthcare was no longer a dream, but a reality. If Human Health could change one life, why not millions more?

Backed by our incredible partners, including our recent funders LocalGlobe (Julia Hawkins and Yvonne Bajela), Airtree (Elicia McDonald), Skip Capital (Kim Jackson and Olivia Sioud (Grivas)), ALIAVIA Ventures (Marisa Warren), Scale Investors Pty Ltd (Samar Mcheileh and Roo Harris), Arvind Rajan(Cricket Health), eric salama (former Kantar CEO), David Shein (GP at OIF Ventures) and Jarred Shein, together with our supporters from the beginning Everywhere Ventures (Jenny Fielding, Scott Hartley and Todd Deacon), Robert Kawalsky, Hamish Corlett, Melanie Corlett, First Light (Dan Gammell and Eleanor Cheetham Gammell, Michael G., Dan Fitzgerald and Samuel Killin, I could not be more hopeful for the future of health - powered by people, technology and the belief that every human deserves to be seen, understood and cared for as an individual.

Our mission is well underway and there is a movement building behind us.
So, when life feels cruel or chaotic, it just goes to show that we can, if we try, choose to create something better - for ourselves, for our families and for all those around us.

This is personal. 💜
