View company: Interius BioTherapeutics
Interius BioTherapeutics

 

1yr • 

What a way to kick off the holiday week with Interius BioTherapeutics's first peer-reviewed paper being published!

The development of CAR T cell therapies has greatly impacted the treatment of B cell malignancies; however, manufacturing these patient-specific, autologous cell therapies is complex, costly and requires preconditioning chemotherapy prior to infusion, limiting patient access. In the publication we describe the development of a lentiviral platform based on a novel, detargeted viral fusogen (Gen 2.1 Fusogen) and a membrane-bound targeting moiety to enable in vivo targeted delivery of stably integrating genetic medicines without the need for lymphodepletion. 

INT2104 employs an scFv targeting CD7 (“CD7 Binder”) to deliver a CAR20 transgene to CD7+ T and NK cells. Preclinical data generated in mouse and cynomolgus macaque models indicate INT2104 results in both CAR T cells (CD4+ and CD8+) and CAR NK cells with subsequent depletion of CD20+ B cells following a single intravenous administration. Thus, INT2104 could potentially provide a more accessible, off-the-shelf treatment option for patients who may benefit from CAR therapies. 

To read more, follow the link: https://lnkd.in/efbTVUdS

📢 “ICT01 Boosts AML Remission and Survival in EVICTION Trial” — Featured in Targeted Oncology ’s ASCO 2025 Media Coverage 📢
We’re proud to see ImCheck’s lead candidate ICT01 featured in @Targeted Oncology, as part of their editorial coverage of the ASCO 2025 Annual Meeting, spotlighting promising advances in immuno-oncology.
🧬 The article highlights encouraging data from the ongoing EVICTION phase 1/2a trial of ICT01 in combination with azacitidine and venetoclax in newly diagnosed AML patients:
✔️ 74% Complete Response rate — nearly twice the historical standard
 ✔️ 83% 9-month Overall Survival with the selected 10 mg ICT01 dose
 ✔️ Favorable safety profile, with no unexpected immune-related events
By activating γ9δ2 T cells via BTN3A, ICT01 introduces a novel immune mechanism in AML, addressing the limits of conventional immunotherapies in this challenging setting.
📈 As we shared in our ASCO presentation, next steps include a randomized Phase 2 trial in AML, expansion into MDS, and exploration in select solid tumors—advancing our mission to harness γ9δ2 T cells across oncology and beyond.
