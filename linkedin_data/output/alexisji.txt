Alexis Ji

  • 2nd

Founding Partner at Primer Ventures | ex-Illumina Ventures, WuXi, ARCH

1mo • 


I’ve been investing in life science tools for over a decade, and one pattern has become increasingly clear to me: breakthrough diagnostic categories are rarely created by a single technology alone. They emerge from an ecosystem.

NGS did not transform oncology by itself. It took targeted panels, liquid biopsy platforms, bioinformatics infrastructure, translational research, and years of clinical validation to make precision medicine actionable at scale. I believe the same will be true for women’s health.

Many of the biggest unmet needs in women’s health — including endometriosis, ovarian cancer, preeclampsia, and PCOS — remain fundamentally measurement problems. Solving them will require coordinated progress across the full stack: biomarker discovery, high-sensitivity quantification technologies, clinical validation, data interpretation, and the long, often underappreciated work of clinical translation.

No single company can do all of this alone. The strongest ecosystems are built by companies that deeply understand their role in the stack and collaborate effectively with others advancing the field.

That is one of the reasons we invested in Countable Labs — a team that understands where highly sensitive single-molecule quantification can unlock value within the broader diagnostics ecosystem.

The uncounted deserve an entire ecosystem working on their behalf.

A perfect way to wrap up the JPM week with the highly received Primer Ventures Annual Meeting!

View company: Primer Ventures
Primer Ventures

5mo • Edited • 

What an inspiring and engaging Primer Ventures Annual Meeting we had on Thursday! We’re incredibly grateful for the energy, thoughtfulness, and support from our community.

We welcomed a full house of investors, founders, co-investors, and entrepreneurs for a day that was candid, intellectually rich, and deeply engaging. Several attendees told us it was the most interesting and engaging meeting they attended during JPM week, feedback we were thrilled (and humbled) to hear.

Our speakers generously opened up about their journeys, lessons learned, and hard-earned perspectives on building and investing in transformative companies. Giovanna Prout, CEO, gave an impressive introduction about Countable Labs and its game-changing PCR platform.  We’re deeply thankful to Steve Fodor (Curio Bioscience), Giovanna Prout (Countable Labs), and peter van vlasselaer (AllInBio) for sharing their entrepreneurial journeys with such openness and clarity, and to Keith Crandell (ARCH Venture Partners) for sharing four decades of wisdom building and backing cutting-edge companies.

We were inspired by the presentation of Kejun (Albert) Ying, Ph.D., a rising scientist from Stanford and University of Washington, on AI-driven discovery in aging biology, an exciting demonstration of how powerful computational approaches can unlock insights hidden in existing data and accelerate anti-aging drug discovery. Building on that, Nigam Shah, Chief Data Scientist at Stanford Healthcare, shared a compelling perspective on how AI and drug development must be thoughtfully integrated into real-world healthcare practice, connecting scientific possibility with clinical reality in a way that resonated strongly with the room.

The enthusiasm and positive feedback throughout the day reflected the tremendous support Primer Ventures has from the ecosystem we’re fortunate to be part of. We’re deeply thankful to our investors, founders, co-investors, partners, entrepreneurs, and friends for your trust and engagement.

We’re excited to keep the conversations going and to continue building together at the frontier of science and innovation. 

Please reach out to us if you are interested in exploring partnering, co-investing, or new startup ideas!

A heartfelt thank-you to Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati and Stifel Bank for your partnership and sponsorship, and for the beautiful venue and fantastic food.
