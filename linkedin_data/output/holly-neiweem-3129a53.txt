About
Holly has over two decades of experience as an investor, operator, and board member.  She is currently the founder and Managing Partner of Apprentis Ventures.  She is interested in early-stage startups working to digitally reinvent and secure legacy industries.  She's honored to be selected for Recast Capital's Accelerate program, with initial backing from Pivotal Ventures (Melinda French Gates' family office) and Catalyze's 2025 Fund Fellowship.  Holly began her career in the industrial space (GE Industrial Systems/Sensing) before moving to a $4.5B principal investing business at Morgan Stanley where she invested in essential industries, early-stage venture through distressed.  She co-founded and ran a successful $200M spinout fund, early-stage to distressed, heavily focused on legacy industries (Eight Bar Partners), and served on multiple global boards (U.S, U.K., Japan, Germany, Canada). Before founding Apprentis Ventures, she served as the CFO and COO of a venture-backed cybersecurity company.  Holly is a fierce advocate for mental health and suicide prevention (founder of The Benny Fund), and is ALL IN on women’s sports 💪.

Introducing Nyad, the AI co-pilot for wastewater.

Wastewater systems quietly underpin modern life, yet they still rely heavily on decades of operator expertise at a moment when much of that knowledge is nearing retirement. Nyad is built for this transition. Their platform encodes human judgment, preserves it, and scales it, helping utilities run more resilient, efficient operations without replacing the people who keep this infrastructure moving.

Apprentis Ventures believes many of the next category-defining AI companies will emerge from legacy industries where intelligence can immediately strengthen essential systems. Nyad is exactly that kind of company.

Building Ampere

2w • 

One piece of equipment was responsible for 35% of Boordy Vineyards Inc total electricity bill — $16,500 a year. They had no idea.

Here's what we found when we looked closer:
1️⃣ All electricity ran through a single meter with no equipment-level breakdown
2️⃣ A 40 HP glycol chiller had been running continuously in the background
3️⃣ That one chiller was driving more than a third of total electricity use

This isn't unusual. Most facilities can see their total bill, but very few can see what's driving it. Without that breakdown, there's no way to know which upgrades are worth it and which ones can wait. We love working with the Boordy Vineyards Inc team. And partnering with Sara Keith and Mike Kelleher at Maryland MEP and Maryland Wineries Association

Ampere used existing meter data to build an equipment-level picture with no new sensors and no interrupted operations. One utility bill became a clear operating picture, and a smarter starting point for future investment decisions.

Want to know what's behind your meter? Reach out: skye@ampereenergy.ai

The jacket you just bought has a barcode. But the barcode dies at checkout.  After that, brands have no idea where their product goes...

♻️ EON: Giving every garment a digital identity — and keeping it alive long after the sale.

Most brands lose sight of their products the moment they're sold. No visibility on resale, repair, or recycling. Without that data, circular fashion is an aspiration with no infrastructure behind it.

EON, founded in New York in 2017 by Natasha Franck, is solving this through digital product passports. Franck spent seven years in urban planning before realizing that the connectivity and intelligence is an important piece in achieving circularity in fashion.

Through EON's platform, each garment receives a unique digital ID that stays with it throughout its entire lifecycle. Brands can track resale, enable repair services, verify authenticity, and gain visibility on how products are consumed and re-consumed — even long after the first sale. The technology also allows brands to embed new capabilities directly into each product: instant resale listings, authentication, and repair options, all unlocked through the product's digital identity.

What sets EON apart isn't just the product — it's the standard they built around it. EON developed the CircularID Protocol, a common language for brands to communicate across the fashion product lifecycle, created in collaboration with leading fashion brands and retailers. Crucially, they made it open and available to the entire industry. That's the difference between building a tool and building infrastructure.

Just as barcodes once revolutionized how businesses operate, EON believes digital product passports will do the same — requiring brands to disclose origin, materials, and sustainability information in ways that move the entire industry closer to circularity. EON has already partnered with Microsoft to bring digital passports to 400 million products.

🔁 What other industries could benefit from this kind of product traceability technology?
