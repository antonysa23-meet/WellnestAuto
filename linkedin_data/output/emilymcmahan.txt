About
I love investing, working with startups, and connecting people, technologies, and companies for good. I am a Co-Founder/General Partner of AIN Ventures, a seed-stage venture fund investing at the intersection of dual-use and deep technology, as well as in military-veteran-led startups. AIN Ventures also hosts the Academy Investor Network, an investment syndicate/platform for graduates of the five U.S. military service academies. The Academy Investor Network invests and supports the next generation of startups, helping our country overcome its toughest challenges. 

Before becoming an investor, I was a founder/early employee for several organizations. One of my favorites was Capitol Post, a veteran-focused incubator that advised hundreds of startups to leverage the backdrop of Washington, DC's unique business and startup community. Capitol Post drew upon my positive experiences at a successful startup technology firm serving the federal government.  

I hold an MBA from the University of Virginia's Darden School of Business and a Bachelor of Science from the United States Military Academy at West Point. After graduating from West Point two months before 9/11, I was commissioned as a U.S. Army military police officer and deployed to support Operations Enduring Freedom, Iraqi Freedom, and Noble Eagle. I am also the proud mom of three kids who are pretty much always one step ahead of me!… more

On Mother’s Day, my 6 year old son Alistair gave me a note.

Like most things from kids, it was simple, honest, and impossible to ignore.

It made me stop and think.

Over the last few years, I’ve spent an enormous amount of time building. Building a fund. Building partnerships. Building for a mission I deeply believe in.

At UA1, we often talk about supporting the people and technologies helping defend freedom. It’s work that matters.

But this year has also reminded me that no mission, no matter how important, can replace the people waiting for you at home.

Alistair has given us more than a few scares over the years. As a baby, he turned blue from RSV and ended up in the PICU. Later, he suffered an anaphylactic reaction to peanuts.  And then, just last week, he gave me another reminder.

We were at the beach, and I was trying to squeeze in a work call. No one was in the water - but the waves were getting bigger, and somewhere between focusing on the meeting and keeping an eye on the kids, I lost sight of Alistair.

For fifteen minutes.

Every parent reading this knows exactly how long fifteen minutes can feel.

Nothing happened. He was fine. But those fifteen minutes were enough to put a lot into perspective.

I spend a lot of time thinking about responsibilities, obligations, deadlines, investors, portfolio companies, and the mission. Those things matter.

But the change I'm making going forward:  when it’s time to be with my kids, it’s time to be with my kids.

Since that day, I’ve tried to be a little more intentional about putting the phone down, closing the laptop, and being fully present. Not because the work isn’t important, but because the people I’m doing it for are standing right in front of me.

So for the next ten days, I’m going to be a little quieter than usual. Fewer emails. Fewer meetings.

Instead, I’ll be spending time with my four kids, showing them a little more of the world, and reminding myself that while the work will always be there, these moments won’t… more

This past weekend, I watched my son, MJ, raise his right hand and take the Oath as an officer in the United States #MarineCorps.

He took that oath from his father, Mike Greentree, MBA, PMP, a #Navy #veteran, and I feel incredibly privileged that, in very real time, our family is an example of one generation passing the torch of service to another. 

MJ grew up a #militarykid. He knew early on what sacrifice looked like - not in the abstract, but in the empty seat at dinner, the missed holidays, the different schools - all the things we worry about impacting our #milkids. 

And, instead of turning away from that life, he ran toward it. Every “I want to be a ___ when I grow up” sign since 3rd grade has said, “I want to be a #Marine. And, today, he is one. 

I believe deeply in the #allvolunteerforce and the importance of #civicengagement to a strong democracy and know the young men and women who choose to raise their hand in service of their country - in all the myriad of ways young people can - are the backbone of everything we hold dear as a nation. 

Conviction and character, the willingness to say: I’ll go, even when (perhaps especially when!) they aren’t sure where that oath will lead them. 

That spirit of a #patriotismofbenevolence is what keeps our republic alive. And, it only continues if the next generation believes it’s worth it.

MJ believes it’s worth it.

And, it’s my personal and professional mission to make sure young people like him keep stepping forward. To make sure the all-volunteer force remains a place worthy of their sacrifice, their ambition, and their idealism.

🔬 Adaptyx Biosciences, a Stanford University spinout, has presented the first continuous, multi-day measurement of free #cortisol in humans at the American Diabetes Association’s Scientific Sessions. The #wearable #patch sensor reads cortisol from dermal interstitial fluid, capturing the hormone’s daily rhythm with minute-level resolution – something conventional blood, saliva, and urine tests cannot do.

💬 “Cortisol is one of the most consequential hormones in the human body, and glucocorticoid receptors are present in nearly every cell. Clinical care has treated the downstream effects, including diabetes, hypertension, and depression, without ever being able to monitor the upstream signal driving much of the response. Adaptyx built the device that finally does.” Vijit Sabnis, Adaptyx Biosciences Co-founder & CEO
