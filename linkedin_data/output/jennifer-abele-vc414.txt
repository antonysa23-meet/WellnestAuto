COnovate, Inc.

2w • Edited • 

COnovate Appoints Battery Industry Veteran Craig Rigby as CEO

We're excited to share a significant milestone for COnovate: Craig Rigby has been appointed President and Chief Executive Officer, effective immediately.
Craig brings more than two decades of executive leadership in battery technology and manufacturing, including senior roles at Clarios, the world's largest low-voltage battery manufacturer, and early work on hybrid vehicle launches at Ford Motor Company. He has served on COnovate's Board of Directors since 2022, so this is a leadership evolution built on deep familiarity with our technology and team.

Alongside this appointment, COnovate founder Carol Hirschmugl transitions to Chief Technology Officer, where she will focus full time on materials innovation, process development, and our technical roadmap. Carol has led COnovate since 2016, and her continued involvement as CTO ensures that the science driving eCOphite™ remains in expert hands.

eCOphite anodes, derived from renewable, U.S.-sourced feedstocks and based on graphene monoxide (GmO), are designed to serve as a full anode replacement or performance-enhancing additive in lithium-ion batteries, with particular promise in LFP applications.

This transition positions COnovate to accelerate commercialization and scale domestic production of next-generation anode materials at a critical moment for the U.S. battery supply chain.

View David Gonzalez’s profile
David Gonzalez

  • 2nd

Co-Founder & CEO at Arbol | Transforming Student Financial Services for Higher Education

3mo • 

The students who don't enroll, and the ones who don't finish, are the ones that keep financial aid directors up at night.

Not the ones who said no. The ones who wanted to say yes but could not figure out how to pay for it in time. And the ones who started but hit a financial wall mid-semester and quietly disappeared.

We heard this from the very first institution we talked to. Costs are rising. Federal aid covers less of the real bill. Institutional aid is maxing out. Families are left piecing together multiple funding sources, navigating shifting federal policy, and going back and forth with financial aid offices already stretched thin. What should take moments drags on for days or weeks.

And when families cannot find clarity fast enough, yield suffers, melt increases, and retention becomes harder to protect.

That is the problem we started Arbol to solve. Students and families get a clear picture of how to pay for school, fast. Financial aid teams get meaningful relief. And institutions protect the enrollment they worked hard to earn, from first deposit through graduation.

But honestly, the part that gets me most is watching a student figure out their path forward and enroll. Or seeing someone who was on the edge of leaving find a way to stay and finish. That is why we do this work.

We did not build this alone. We built it alongside the colleges and universities who knew this problem best, and we now have strong traction inside the UNC system, SUNY, and the Association of Jesuit Colleges and Universities.

As covered by Series B(uffalo), Marquette University and UNC Charlotte just joined that network.

We are looking forward to continuing to expand this impact. If your institution is carrying this problem, we would love to connect.
