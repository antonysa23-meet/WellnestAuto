About
Just bought a college campus in Upstate NY with some amazing partners. We're turning it into a vibrant economic innovation and lifestyle district. 

I'm also a General Partner of W Fund, a VC investing into early-stage tech startups led by incredible founders, and CEO of SWITCH, a global platform shaping the future of our innovation economy, creating a pipeline of resilient startup leaders and angel investors. 

I've lived in the tech startup world for almost 20 years, and have played an active leadership role in the women in tech and entrepreneurship ecosystem for 15 of them, having led a global, 60+ chapter organization focused on the issue as President for several years prior to coming to SWITCH in 2016.

The 2+ decade combination of operational experience in startups and deep expertise in gender and representation in the ecosystem has found me in the position of an expert and leader on the creation and development of a more equitable future for our innovation economy.

I served as an EIR/Mentor for Techstars for over a decade, and have held prior roles as CMO of untapt, an AI-driven tech talent marketplace based in NYC, COO of Atribute, an EdTech startup, and Adjunct Professor at Syracuse University.

I've keynoted, spoken at and been featured in 100s of places, including SXSW, Collision Conference, Fast Company, Rally Conference, Fifteen Seconds, UNLEASH, Microsoft, IBM, MSNBC, Techcrunch, IBT, the BBC and Al Jazeera. I've authored a chapter in Digital Activism Decoded, entitled "Economic and Social Factors: The Digital (Activism) Divide), and have spent several years focused on issues around digital activism, social mobilization and the use of digital tools at all levels of society.

I hold BAs in Political Science and African American History from the University of Rochester, an MBA in Entrepreneurship and Marketing from Goizueta Business School at Emory University and an MA in International Relations from the Fletcher School at Tufts University.

When I'm not doing all *that*, I'm a very non-professional (but adventurous!) athlete, an equally as non-professional musician, can be found outdoors in any weather — skiing, biking, hiking, sailing — loves a good meal with bubbly, craft beer and good friends, hilariousness and life. My husband is a startup-web-developer-turned-beer-guy and we live on a hops farm in Upstate New York with our two boys.

I hate the phrase "remote work. It's completely outdated.

It means you're DISTANT and NOT CENTRAL, you're OUT OF TOUCH and DISCONNECTED. 

It says you're NOT where you're SUPPOSED TO BE.

This isn't 2026 thinking, at all. It's an insanely limiting mindset.

I had the pleasure of kicking off Startup Maine and addressed exactly this: building an ecosystem outside a major hub - using our work redeveloping a former college campus into a vibrant, innovation-forward "town square" - and offered a complete reframe to this whole concept.

My approach: It's ANCHORED and UNBOUND.

Anchored is strong, and it says that you chose to be there.
Unbound is the refusal to me limited by where you are.

A lot of it rests on what catalyzed remote work in the first place - digital/virtual, lean into your network, etc - but it pushes us to be more intentional with those opportunities.

It's about BRINGING THE WORLD TO YOU.

This is the way we need to think about it in 2026 and beyond. Would love to hear your thoughts.
