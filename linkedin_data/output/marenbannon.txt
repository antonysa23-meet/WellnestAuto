About
Maren Bannon is Co-Founder and Managing Partner at January Ventures, a pre-seed firm focused on B2B startups that are digitally transforming traditional industries, including work, health and climate. January Ventures has invested in 60 companies to date, and closed Fund II in March 2022.

Maren is an engineer turned operator and entrepreneur who saw a huge problem & inefficiency in the market: the tech ecosystem is becoming increasingly diverse and distributed, yet the network driven nature of VC causes it to miss many huge opportunities. Before co-founding January Ventures, Maren spent over 15 years in operating roles in biotech, pharma, semiconductors and digital health with roles at Genentech, Roche, Applied Materials and various startups. Her operating roles include product management, sales, marketing, strategy and pricing. She also cofounded LittleLane, a booking and lead generation tool for SMBs.

Maren is the mother to three young daughters, and she feels energized each day by creating a different world for them. She has an MBA from Stanford and an Engineering degree from Dartmouth. Maren is a Contributor at Forbes and The Financial Times, and she is a mentor at StartX (Stanford's accelerator program) and TechStars London.

Loved spending time at the Precursor Ventures Pre-seed Bootcamp in London and speaking on panel with Ashtan Jordan and Ophelia C.. 

Here were some of our tips for European founders who want to raise funding from the US:

✈️  You don't need to move to SF, but spend time there in person
🌆 Join founder communities - they are often the best channel for intros
🏠 You don't need to live in a hacker house to break into the SF scene(!)

🖊️ Consider flipping to Delaware C (the bar is higher if you're in the international bucket)
🥇 AI makes it easier than ever to build - customer relationships are gold

The startup zeitgeist has swung hard recently towards younger founders, especially in AI. Recent data from Antler showed that AI unicorn founders are getting younger: the average age fell from 40 in 2020 to 29 in 2024. YC’s median founder age dropped from 30 in 2022 to 24 in summer 2025.

I’m seeing more and more initiatives focused on younger founders, like Tech Nation’s new Founders of the Future initiative, Harry’s Stebbing’s Project Europe and YC's Early Decision.

My contrarian take: vertical AI rewards industry expertise. It is one place where operating experience matters.

The founders with 5-10+ years in the field often have the edge in complex, regulated industries like healthcare, supply chain, manufacturing and financial services because they understand the customer, the workflow and the GTM motion from the inside. As AI tools become more widely accessible, it won’t just be about raw technical fluency but also about unique insights into the customer, workflow and regulatory context.

Some news: We've raised $9.3M to power a new infrastructure for preventive and personalized care. Vibrant Practice is now Ultralight, the AI-native operating system for modern medicine. 

This round is led by The General Partnership with Wisdom Ventures, Anthemis Group, Emerson Collective, GSBackers, and angels including Jonathan Swerdlin and former Surgeon General Vivek Murthy.

Patients are spending more on their own health than ever before. The wellness economy hit $6.8 trillion last year. DPC practices tripled among family physicians in a single year. And for the first time, DPC memberships are HSA-eligible. But healthcare still runs on a one-size-fits-all model: a snapshot once a year, a fifteen-minute visit, and care that feels cookie-cutter, not personalized. Patients are voting with their wallets for root-cause care, prevention, and physicians who actually know them. The demand is here. The infrastructure is what's missing.

Ultralight stands for what the technology in healthcare should feel like. Right now, most clinicians are stitching together five disconnected platforms just to see a single patient. Technology feels heavy and cumbersome.

The tech should work in the background. Patients should feel seen. Clinicians can feel present. And they shouldn't be spending their evenings on admin and charting, creating worn-out keyboards in record time.

We're live across 75 clinics, supporting hundreds of physicians and thousands of patients. We haven’t done any paid acquisition yet, and have thousands of clinics interested. 60% of our pipeline has already or will replace their existing systems entirely. Clinicians on Ultralight are saving hours every week, reclaiming their evenings, and telling us something we never get tired of hearing: that they're enjoying practicing medicine again. 

We are honored to be building alongside brilliant clinical minds like Jordan Shlain, MD, Brian Hollett, MD, MBA, Alexis Gonzales and Dr George Papanicolaou on our Clinical Advisory Board.

I'm grateful that so many of our investors are also friends, people who watched this take shape from the earliest days and chose to back it because they believe in the future we see. 

I'm so incredibly proud of our team. Pedro and I started this company because we believed clinicians and patients deserve better, and the people who've joined us deeply believe in this mission.

We're hiring across engineering and GTM. If you want to build the infrastructure for the future of medicine, join us at Ultralight.

Special thanks to Brock E.W. Turner Axios for covering our story (link in comments).
