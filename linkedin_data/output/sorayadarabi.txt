About
Soraya is a Founder and Managing Partner of TMV – a global early-stage venture firm with 230 million in AUM across TMV Funds I-III and TMV: Lifecycles. TMV backs innovation reshaping legacy industries. She built the firm to enable breakthrough entrepreneurs with the capital and go-to-market expertise required to reach a major inflection point of growth.  

Soraya has invested into over 100 startups over her career, including: Anthropic, AMI Labs, Chronicle Bio, Lovable, FIGS, Novellia, NIL Club, Cityblock, Resultid, Weave Bio, Roon, Slingshot AI, Ridwell, Casper, Hungryroot, Mavely, Topline Pro, Smalls, Ours Privacy, Trackstar, Nodal, Gimlet Media, Yield. Pre-IPO, Cloudflare. 

She began her career at The New York Times, where she positioned the global news leader on social networks such as Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, partnered with startups large and small, and established award-winning campaigns.  She went on to co-found the application Foodspotting, named by Apple and Wired Magazine as an “App of the Year," later acquired by OpenTable, then Priceline. 

She graduated with high honors from Georgetown University and completed the Global Leadership and Public Policy for the 21st Century program at the Harvard Kennedy School. 

Soraya currently serves on the Board of Directors for Kennedy Human Rights, Bridge, Resultid AI, Tali AI. She lives in New York with her family.

Topline Pro is an interesting case study in modern vertical SaaS: instead of pitching home services contractors yet another CRM, they hand them a... 

→ Working website
→ Google Business presence
→ a reputation engine on day one

The wedge is a deliverable, not a dashboard. That deliverable buys them trust (and a payment relationship) with an audience that historically refuses to self-serve software, and it quietly becomes the system of record for every job, review, and lead that follows.

A home services contractor holding a phone with his Topline Pro-style dashboard.

The wedge ships in the customer's hand on day one, not after a 30-day onboarding.

From there, the loop compounds: 
AI drafts the marketing copy, automates review requests, and turns every closed job into SEO-ranked proof, so the same software that looks like a website is really a flywheel that lowers customer acquisition cost over time. 

For vertical founders, the lesson isn't "add AI", it's that AI is most defensible when it's buried inside a workflow your customer was already paying someone else (an agency, a marketer, a cousin) to do badly.… more

Today, Novellia announced an $18M Series A led by our friends at Spark Capital. At TMV, we have been proud investors for some time, due to a shared belief that the future of healthcare begins with personalized data.

Shashi built Novellia from a simple but urgent observation: patients managing serious diagnoses like cancer and rare disease are forced to act as their own project managers. Think: physical binders. Multiple portals. Endless browser tabs. Chronic illness demands extraordinary bandwidth, and the healthcare system did little to help.  

So he created the infrastructure to change that.

Novellia patients unify up to 20 years of health history in seconds, free of charge, right on their phone. One organized home for records, symptoms, and care. They walk into every appointment as an informed advocate for themselves.

And the infrastructure goes further. Research has historically been assembled from fragmented data, rarely from a patient who actively chose to contribute their complete story. 

Novellia is the first platform where that choice is the starting point. When patients authorize sharing their de-identified, anonymized data, scientists gain the clinical depth required to build the next generation of medicines. 

Top life science companies are already working with Novellia to leverage this powerful RWE (real world evidence) data to offer better treatments. 

This raise fuels the mission Shashi has been relentlessly building toward: peace of mind for patients, and a foundation for breakthroughs that benefit everyone.

Just spoke to Ben Thompson on BBC Business on behalf of Ada Ventures about the Anthropic SEC filing and what it means for the tech industry and for early stage VCs. I only had 3 minutes so some further thoughts here (and no, I am not smiling because I am an investor in Anthropic - sadly! And this is not investment advice)..  

🥇 1. Anthropic filing with the SEC first ahead of Open AI is significant. The large Frontier Labs that IPOs first may soak up investor demand - and they are certainly likely to set the terms on valuation for whoever comes next.

📈 2. It is hard to process how incredible it is that this company is only ~5 years old and is at a reported $47bn revenue runrate, up from reportedly $10bn last year and (reportedly) targeting a $1tr+ valuation on it's debut. The growth and scale is staggering. 

💰 3. If it happens as forecasted this could be one of the largest IPOs of all time - after Saudi Aramco and SpaceX. 

⚖️ 4. If the IPO goes well, this is both a good signal and a threat to early stage AI companies - the threat is that Anthropic and Open AI become more and more embedded across more aspects of businesses and it is harder to carve out a niche, the positive is that the market believes in the durability of the revenues of AI-first companies and tolerates the large losses. 

🌍 5. The structure of Anthropic is highly unusual and exciting for me to see as an Impact Investor. They are a Public Benefit Corporation which is overseen by  an independent Long-Term Benefit Trust (LTBT). This design legally empowers the company’s board to prioritise AI safety and societal benefit alongside shareholder returns, acting as a safeguard to prevent short-term profit motives from overriding ethical or safety standards. 

🇬🇧 👩‍💻 6. Jack Clark, one of the co-founders of Anthropic is British (and an English Literature grad like me!). Anthropic also have a female co-founder, Daniela Amodei. It is very interesting to me that this team has diversity embedded from the start and has been so successful, despite starting later than Open AI. Inclusive Alpha at work. 

🇬🇧 🇪🇺 7. The question for the UK and Europe is how we supercharge our own and keep them building here. Yesterday, founders in our portfolio like Jack & Jill, MatNex and Lifted, and many others, joined Balderton, a European VC, in their Built in Europe campaign to send exactly that message. The UK and Europe has the talent. Our job is to find them and back them, so they can build category-defining companies that compete globally. I'm really pleased that Suzanne Ashman, Pippy James, Matt Clifford, James Wise, Suranga Chandratillake, Alice Bentinck and so many more are working to foster the European and UK innovation ecosystem through Sovereign AI, EF, ARIA and much more.

TMV launches $200m maritime fund backed by ABS

Early-stage venture firm TMV has announced the launch of TMV Logistics, a US$200 million venture fund dedicated to maritime and logistics innovation and safety anchored by commitments from the American Bureau of Shipping (ABS) and Prologis Ventures, the investment arm of logistics real estate company Prologis.

“We’re in the foothills of a multi-decade rebuild of maritime and industrial infrastructure,” said Marina Hadjipateras, Co-Founder and a Managing Partner at TMV.

The fund is supported by an advisory network including representatives from ConGlobal, Fenix Marine Services, A.P. Moller - Maersk, Flexport, Dorian LPG, and the defence innovation ecosystem.

“With a global footprint across logistics real estate, we see firsthand how constraints at ports and along maritime corridors ripple through the entire supply chain,” said William O'Donnell, Managing Director of Global Corporate Development, Growth at Prologis Ventures.

The fund intends to invest across five core technology themes: industrial-grade autonomy, verticalised robotics, operational AI, maritime dual-use technologies, and the energy transition.

"Maritime AI is a once-in-a-generation opportunity, and the companies being built right now will set the standards for the next fifty years," said Soraya Darabi, Co-Founder and a Managing Partner at TMV.

Following its launch, the anchor partners will serve as active strategic collaborators to shape product development and act as design partners and ecosystem integrators for the portfolio companies.

“ABS has long believed that the future of maritime safety and performance will be shaped by closer collaboration with innovators at the frontier of technology. Our partnership with TMV Logistics reflects that conviction,” said John McDonald, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of ABS.

Principal @ TMV Investing in the Future of Maritime & Freight Supply Chains

1mo • 

Yesterday, TMV Logistics officially launched our $200M fund via an exclusive announcement in The Wall Street Journal.

We are a commercial maritime & logistics venture fund, & it is impossible to ignore the momentum behind maritime dual-use technologies.

The proposed FY2027 U.S. naval shipbuilding budget stands at $65.8B within a record proposed $1.5T U.S. defense budget. U.S. naval shipbuilding funding in 2024 was $33.7B, with the proposed 2027 figure representing nearly a 40% increase versus 2026 levels.

This is bipartisan industrial policy. China now possesses the world’s largest navy & controls 60-70% of global shipbuilding capacity.

The U.S. Navy formed the NRCO in August 2025 to streamline acquisition pathways, created robotics & autonomy offices, & expanded commercial engagement through ONR innovation initiatives. The Navy outlined ambitions to deploy thousands of autonomous maritime systems across the Indo-Pacific & grow uncrewed systems to at least 45% of the naval surface fleet by 2045.

American Bureau of Shipping (ABS) partnered with Persona AI to bring humanoid robotics into shipyards while anchoring the TMV Logistics fund. Huntington Ingalls is also partnering with startups across shipyard modernization initiatives.

The UK & Australia are expanding autonomous naval capabilities. The UAE launched the Abu Dhabi Naval Technology Innovation Hub. Indonesia deployed its first domestically built autonomous submarine. India unveiled its Navy Technology Roadmap 2047 focused on autonomy & indigenous maritime modernization.

HD Hyundai, Hanwha, & Fincantieri are investing heavily into AI, robotics, ISR, underwater autonomy, & shipyard technologies. Hanwha Group acquired Philly Shipyard in December 2024 & committed $5B toward expansion & modernization, part of broader South Korean & Japanese commitments expected to total roughly $150B into revitalizing the U.S. shipbuilding industry.

We are also seeing talent from Anduril, SpaceX, Tesla, & Scale AI move into maritime. One example from our portfolio is Quartermaster's recent $43M Series A, led by Neil Sobin, formerly of Scale AI.

Earlier this year, Saronic raised a $1.75B Series D at a $9.25B valuation, the largest maritime venture round to date.

We are not a defense fund. We are a commercial maritime & logistics fund built by investors & operators from the commercial maritime world, alongside advisors from the defense industry.

Bridging technologies from defense adoption into commercial maritime requires customer relationships, operational credibility, regulatory understanding, & industry access.

“Dual-use” has become an increasingly lazy term in venture. Not every defense technology becomes commercially viable, & not every commercial technology translates effectively into defense environments.

At TMV Logistics, we believe maritime autonomy, robotics, ISR, logistics resilience, & industrial modernization represent a multi-decade build cycle still in its earliest innings.… more

1mo • Edited • 

Prologis and the American Bureau of Shipping (ABS) are anchoring a $200 million venture-capital fund for U.S. maritime and logistics startups.

The fund, managed by TMV, will invest in technologies like automation and AI to improve supply chain efficiency and the maritime industrial base.

There are many dynamics driving the need for innovation in the maritime sector - to name a few…

- There is strong bipartisan support in Congress for initiatives that spark U.S. maritime investment given the massive gap between the US and China’s shipbuilding capabilities.

- The U.S. Navy is pushing for the biggest purchase in decades of noncombat ships to boost U.S. shipbuilding.

- Traditional approaches to scaling US shipbuilding are up against a wide array of systemic and technical challenges, all of which demand innovation and new technologies.

- Ports have long been chokepoints in global supply chains and will be even more as trade volatility continues, demanding more innovation in ports and upstream/downstream of ports.

- Clean fuel technology is a steep but necessary hill to climb for the maritime industry.

- The challenges of securing maritime vessels has been highlighted during recent and ongoing conflicts in the Middle East.

This is a massive, multi-decade growth opportunity that demands the right mix of entrepreneurial talent, technology, operating experience and funding.

New Fund Alert 🚀

Thanks to Paul Berger and The Wall Street Journal for featuring the launch of TMV Logistics, our new $200M venture fund dedicated to backing the next generation of founders transforming maritime, logistics, and industrial infrastructure. ⚓

We’re honored to launch the fund alongside strategic anchor partners American Bureau of Shipping (ABS) and Prologis, two global leaders whose technical expertise, operational reach, and longstanding leadership continue to shape the future of maritime and logistics.

We believe maritime and logistics are entering a generational rebuild cycle. Supply chains are being rearchitected in real time as AI, autonomy, robotics, energy transition, and geopolitics reshape the movement of goods, energy, and industrial capacity.

At TMV, we’ve spent years investing at the intersection of legacy industry and emerging technology. What’s changed is not just the scale of innovation, but the breadth of participation behind it. Operators, infrastructure owners, and industrial leaders are engaging more directly with early-stage innovation to help accelerate the technologies transforming the sector.

We’ll be backing founders across maritime, shipbuilding, ports, intermodal logistics, industrial AI, robotics, autonomy, and dual-use technologies from pre-seed through Series A, with the goal of building the next layer of coordination, resilience, safety, and operational capability across the global economy.

Our first investment through the fund was in Quartermaster, a company building real-time ocean intelligence infrastructure and helping define the future of maritime awareness.

More to come!
