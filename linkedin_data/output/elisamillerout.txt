About
Elisa Miller-Out is an experienced tech entrepreneur, investor, board director and community builder. She is managing partner of Chloe Capital, a seed stage VC firm focused on investing with a gender and diversity lens. She's also an Innovation Advisor and serves on the Climate Action Council at NYSERDA. She is an Entrepreneur in Residence at Cornell University and mentors for 76West, StartFast, NEXUS-NY and LaunchNY. Elisa recently had a successful acquisition deal with a division of Singlebrook, a custom software services firm that she co-founded and led as CEO for over 10 years. She is now chair of the board and CFO at Singlebrook. Singlebrook has Fortune 500, higher education and nonprofit clients from all over the world including: Cornell, Yale, Henry Schein, Hitachi, the Sierra Club, the Environmental Defense Fund and more. Elisa has been featured in the New York Times, the Washington Post, USA Today, Forbes and other publications, and she speaks about technology and entrepreneurship at events across the country. 

In addition to serving as chair on the private company board of Singlebrook, the business she co-founded, Elisa has served on several private for profit and non-profit boards including: GatheringUs, Climate Action Council, Women 2.0, StartFast Code, ConvergeUS, CSRwire, Local First Ithaca, NY Society of Association Executives, and Barnard Young Alumnae. She looks forward to bringing her innovation strategy, robust network, finance knowledge and operating experience to other boards.

Prior to founding Singlebrook, Elisa founded two other companies and worked in a variety of industries in NYC. She also founded several networking groups and regularly serves as a mentor and judge for startup accelerators and competitions. She graduated Summa Cum Laude from Barnard College of Columbia University. When she’s not working, Elisa enjoys local foods, Crossfit, swimming in waterfalls, reading and spending time with her husband and two daughters and her big extended family. Learn more: elisamillerout.com.

Sharon Samjitsingh

CEO @ Health Care Originals | AI wearable for chronic respiratory care | Validated outcomes for asthma & COPD. Serial Deep Tech Builder, Growing High-Impact Businesses from Breakthrough Science

1mo • 

Recently CVS announced efforts around agentic twins. I actually think that is an important step forward for the industry because it validates where healthcare is heading, but it also highlights an important distinction.

Most healthcare digital twin approaches today are still built primarily around snapshots: surveys, episodic data, claims history, retrospective analysis, or periodic interactions. And while that can absolutely improve care, the human body is not static; it is continuously signaling.

A true physiological model has to evolve continuously with the person in real life, not only at moments when data is manually collected or retrospectively analyzed.

If you’re familiar with my journey, you know that before HCO, I spent years commercializing technologies in manufacturing, industrial systems, and sustainability. I’ve also spoken before about the parallels I see between value-based care and EPC contracts in industry. Healthcare still largely pays for activity. Industrial systems learned long ago to optimize for outcomes, reliability, uptime, and system performance.

When you work in high-risk industries operating 24/7, with thousands of interconnected signals, you learn something quickly: you cannot manage a complex physical system if you only measure it occasionally.

A chemical plant does not run on quarterly snapshots and self-reporting.

One of the things that struck my Co-Founder Jared Dwarika and me when we started Health Care Originals® was that healthcare still operates very differently. We wait for symptoms to become visible and rely on episodic visits and fragmented data as though they tell us everything about the patient. We ask people to interpret signals their own bodies are generating continuously, but cannot fully perceive.

That disconnect became deeply personal for me living with asthma. As I shared over the past couple weeks, my body was signaling deterioration weeks before exacerbations. Nobody could see it. Including me.

That lens is what we brought to HCO: industrial systems engineering applied to human physiology: continuous sensing; signal interpretation; predictive intervention; and closed-loop response.

The world is suddenly fascinated with the term Physical AI. It's good to see everyone catching up, because that's what we've built.

ADAMM-RSM continuously captures physiological signals in real life. Our respiratory therapists understand each patient’s unique baseline and detect meaningful deviations early, much like operators in a control room monitoring complex systems before failure occurs: not reactive care but continuous system awareness.

Every interaction, signal, and response has contributed to a continuously evolving model of that individual person, grounded in real-world physiological signals.

We now call that HCO’s Living Patient Model, and it forms the basis of our AI, Aidah. Not an LLM wrapper, but a continuously evolving physiological intelligence system grounded in the real world.… more
