Although aviation and shipping move the global economy, they remain among the hardest sectors to decarbonize. They need energy-dense fuels, and e-fuels could be the answer - but only if CO₂ and hydrogen can be produced affordably and at scale.

AiiM Partners Ocean Plus Fund II portfolio company Brineworks tackles this. Its platform, now online in Amsterdam, captures CO₂ from the air and co-produces green hydrogen in a single process, delivering two critical e-fuel feedstocks at once. Its patented electrolyzer avoids costly precious metals, requires fewer membranes, and operates flexibly on intermittent renewable power without degrading; significantly lowering the feedstock cost for e-fuels.

Congratulations to Gudfinnur Sveinsson, Joseph Perryman, and the entire Brineworks team.

AiiM portfolio company Lyten has completed its acquisition of Northvolt's Swedish assets (~$5B): 16 GWh of existing manufacturing capacity, 160+ hectares of industrial infrastructure, and the largest and among the most advanced battery R&D centers

Lyten is establishing the Lyten Industrial Hub in Skellefteå — co-locating battery manufacturing, energy storage, and AI data centers on clean Nordic hydropower. EdgeConneX plans to acquire a data center site from Lyten with potential to scale to ~1 GW.

We backed Lyten five years ago on the conviction that lithium-sulfur was a platform shift, not an incremental improvement, while reducing reliance on cobalt, nickel, and graphite. With 550+ patents  and expanding capacity across two continents, Lyten is scaling where it matters.

Congratulations to Dan Cook, Lars Herlitz, Keith Norman, and the entire Lyten team!… more

SAN JOSE, Calif., and STOCKHOLM, Sweden – February 27, 2026 – Lyten, a global leader in lithium-sulfur batteries and energy storage, announced today that it has completed the acquisition of Northvolt Ett and Ett Expansion (Skellefteå, Sweden) and Northvolt Labs (Västerås, Sweden). 

The Northvolt Sweden acquisition includes 16 GWh of existing battery manufacturing capacity, more than 160 hectares of land, infrastructure and buildings to support expanded manufacturing and industrial activities, and the largest and most advanced battery research and development center in Europe.
