As we welcome the biggest tech IPO ever, SpaceX, a timely conversation with Ajay Agarwal, Partner at Bain Capital Ventures (BCV) Capital Ventures who has been investing in Robotics and Industrials for 25+ years. It was tremendous to sit down with Ajay for this episode of #PostMoneyPodcast to dive into what is ahead  as AI and venture capital move aggressively into all sectors from Space, to defense, construction, healthcare, public education, you name it.
Inside the episode:

-Where BCV is investing today and how the synergies with Bain Capital continue to drive value. 

-Building high value and durable companies takes not just time, founder vision & stamina (SpaceX founded 2002) but also investors that can hang in there through the downs. We unpack what that conversation looks like inside a VC partnership when other VCs may not be reinforcing your high conviction investment with their signals.

-Why Industrials/Physical is seeing a renaissance: push for onshoring, securing critical supply chain, breakthroughs in AI models, an order of magnitude decline in HW costs , but most importantly, the best founders now want to be in the physical world.  

-While “Physical” can sound new and exciting, these are historically low margin, capital intensive industries that have relied on debt capital, and therefore cash-flows.  We dive into why equity is rushing into these industries, the opportunity and caution for founders and VCs.

- Robotics and “Phyiscal” are interchangeable.  It’s hi tech’s wedge into traditional physical industries.  We discuss several recent BCV investments that are fully verticalized: robotics as a wedge to deliver a full service from construction to automotive assembly.  The key for these companies to succeed beyond the initial hardware: proprietary data, much harder to access in the physical world and how to get it.

- Why SaaS companies need to play offense with AI if they are to make it and how culture may be their biggest obstacle.

-Looking to the future: using the power of AI to reinvent public education, life sciences and healthcare.

“Think big, raise massive capital, and attain multi-billion-dollar outcomes” is the message to tech founders. The data however is stark: 99.99% will not IPO, 0.01% may see an M&A, and success will take a decade or more.  

Private markets have long suffered from the inefficiencies of opaqueness.  With over 60,000 tech companies now managing their cap tables on Carta, the Company sits on a treasure trove of private markets data that can bring long overdue transparency. My guest in this episode of #PostMoneyPodcast is Peter Walker, Head of Data Insights at Carta.  Peter is a one-man megaphone in our industry putting out pithy slides that bust many of our bombastic myths. It was an absolute pleasure to sit down with Peter to walk through some of his most recent analysis of private company valuations, secondaries, IPO and M&A data.  WE KILL MANY MYTHS – so tune in 😊!

Some topics inside the episode:
·       The real fundraising benchmarks founders need to hit in 2026
·       Why “zero to $100M in a year” is distorting startup expectations
·       The stark financing reality facing SaaS companies 
·       Why down rounds are far more common than founders think
·       The truth about unicorns, secondary sales, and tender offers
·       IPO and M&A data going back to 2001 
·       The dilution math every founder should understand at inception

Finally, memo to Henry Ward and the Carta PM team, how about a structured data product on private market valuations & exit data that Carta and non-Carta users can subscribe to? Such a product can bring massive efficiencies to the private markets enabling founders and VCs to take data driven rational decisions, broaden gains distributions, and expand the market by enabling much bigger capital pools to join.

For VCs, the bet is no longer software but entirely new forms of intelligence. Raviraj Jain from Lightspeed explains why some of Silicon Valley’s biggest capital pools are shifting to self-learning systems, humanoids, fusion energy, and frontier AI that could redefine how humans work, manufacture, make scientific discoveries, and generate power over the next decade.
In this episode of #PostMoneyPodcast, we break down why many investors now believe the next leap in AI will happen outside the digital world, in machines that can operate in physical environments, understand motion, adapt to unfamiliar situations, and interact with the real world autonomously. Foundation models, reinforcement learning, and multimodal systems are advancing faster than most people realize, fully functioning humanoids may arrive state side soon. We get into the hidden constraints behind the AI boom. As data centers become one of the fastest-growing consumers of global energy, technologies once considered experimental, including nuclear fusion, are becoming venture attractive. The race today is no longer whether AI will change industries but who will move fast enough to rebuild them.
Inside the episode:
·       The rationale behind VCs funding NeoLabs
