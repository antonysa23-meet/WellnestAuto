In this out-take from a recent Valor VC LP meeting, I provided some context on the very real schism in VC over AI. It isn't "big vs. small" or "legacy vs. emerging." It's maximization vs. optimization.  AI is accelerating both paths—toward very different outcomes.

🔳 Maximizing #VCs answer one question: 
How many dollars do you need to be powerful?
→ Capital concentrated in $100M+ rounds, regardless of the letter on the check → Polished, homogenized LP experience—annual meetings where the photos are curated more carefully than the content 
→ AI deployed to automate the gleam: marketing, financial engineering, showmanship 
→ The general thrust to LPs: write bigger checks. Competition via inflation.

🟩 Optimizing #VCs answer a different question: 
How powerful can each LP dollar be?
This is Valor's path. We don't win by writing bigger checks or putting more stars on stage. We seek alpha by knowing more sooner and acting on it in tight partnership with founders.

▪️ How we're using AI differently:
→ Informing LPs of moments to support the portfolio—not just moments to deploy more capital 
→ Augmenting the human intelligence of the founder-LP relationship, not the capital transfer efficiency 
→ Training AI on sustainable business growth as the optimization target, not maximum valuation

▪️ Why this matters for returns:
LPs still feel the markdown pain of 2021-22 overexuberance. Capital retreated to large legacy firms with perceived structural safety. But perceived safety and actual alpha are different things.

True portfolio alignment demands infrastructure that doesn't exist at scale in VC yet—letting partners know about portfolio health, momentum, risk, and opportunity ahead of reporting cycles, on their own timelines.

That's what we're building at Valor. In a few days, I'm looking forward to posting a deep dive on some of our LP-facing technology. 

If you know an RIA in the South that thinks similarly, I'd love to be connected for Valor's private capital listening tour in Q2.

Day 2,904:  What if the CRM ran on autopilot too?

One of the most interesting lessons from the last several years is that prospects don’t wake up one morning and magically become opportunities.

But sales reps love waking up to fully baked opportunities they've never talked to before. Who wouldn't?

A fully sourced, verified, engaged, and qualified opportunity. Created in the background. Delivered on their behalf. Ready to meet and convert. 

Pure prospecting magic.

The paradox is that the magic only works when nobody has to think about how much work went into creating it.

Before an opportunity exists:

- A market has to be mapped.

- Accounts have to be identified.

- Contacts have to be discovered.

- Messages have to be delivered.

- Conversations have to begin.

Interest has to develop. Only then does something eventually become pipeline.

For years, one of the best parts of Sailes was that none of this was visible.

The Sailebot worked quietly in the background and a rep would suddenly receive an opportunity that appeared to come out of nowhere.

Customers loved the outcome.

What they didn't love was not knowing what was happening in between.

When leads were flowing, everything was great.

When they weren't, customers had no way to see the work being done on their behalf.

That realization changed how we think about autonomous software.

The goal isn't to make users operate the system. It's to help them believe in it.

That's why we released the autonomous Deal Board this week.

Not to create another CRM.
Not to create another dashboard that sales teams feel obligated to manage.

Instead, to make pipeline formation visible.

The Deal Board updates itself as accounts move from sourced to qualified to opportunity.

To show markets being mapped.

Accounts being engaged.

Opportunities developing.

Pipeline growing.

And eventually revenue being created.

The system still works in the background. 

The difference is that now customers can watch movement become momentum, momentum become pipeline, and pipeline become revenue.

Pipeline exists before Salesforce. Now you can watch it form.… more
