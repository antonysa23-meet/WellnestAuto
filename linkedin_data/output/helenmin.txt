Reshared by Helen Min


I've been quietly building something, and today I'm taking the cover off.

Eight years ago, I made my first angel investment while running marketing at Plaid. For eight years, I've been backing exceptional founders from my network (I was also an early marketing leader at Facebook, Dropbox, Quora, and AngelList) at the earliest stages, writing checks and helping them sharpen their stories.

That's meaningful—and it's built a real track record—but I kept coming back to something: if you have real access to great founders before they become consensus bets, and you happen to be a storyteller, the most valuable thing you can do is to back them before the market catches on, and quietly help them look inevitable by the time it does.

That's Articulate. A new venture capital firm backing technical founders "pre-pitch deck."

I moved to San Francisco 20 years ago for a job in advertising, and a few years in, I got to work on a Super Bowl commercial. So as a nod to that beginning, I made a 60-second commercial about Articulate. I hope you enjoy it.

View Syed Ali’s profile
Syed Ali

  • 2nd

Co-Founder & CEO at Pie

4d • Edited • 

Today, Akhil Mantripragada and I are announcing Pie’s $19.5M Series A, led by Lightspeed, bringing total funding to $23.7M, with participation from Capital One Ventures, SciFi VC, F-Prime, Commerce Ventures, WEX Venture Capital, Restive Ventures, Cambrian and our existing investors.

Ask any small business owner what they want most, and the answer is always the same: More customers. More people are walking through the door. More phones ringing. More bookings. Nothing is more important.

And yet, it’s the one part of running a business that owners and entrepreneurs get the least help with. Small businesses spend roughly $640 billion a year trying to grow, and a lot of it is wasted because today’s tools were never built to work for them or with each other. The POS software a business pays for is, all the while, storing valuable data that can be leveraged to power a growth engine (or 🚀). 

Akhil and I built Pie to address that. We want to increase the share of the Pie for every small business.  

It places a small business right where it needs to be and in front of the customers when they are searching for it. And when those customers reach out, it answers. 

Pie has brought 3 agents to market for the local businesses: 

– Growth: takes all of the POS & CRM data and creates content to find local customers across multiple platforms like Google Maps, Meta, Yelp, & Nextdoor, always learning and optimizing for the best performance. 

– AI Search: takes those profiles and optimizes them for local LLM search (asking ChatGPT or Gemini where to take your car in for repair, or which hair salon in your area you should visit for a new look). 

– Front Desk: we have all the context needed to give that specific business a voice agent for answering calls, taking bookings, responding to customer questions, and ensuring it never misses a sale. We’re proud to have steered over 100,000+ calls to local businesses in the last 10 months. 

It’s been a sprint to get Pie to where it is today in less than a year: thousands of customers, major platform partnerships, an office in NYC, and an amazing team that understands the small business industry and is eager to serve them.  

With our Series A and launch, we’re excited to add fuel to the fire! If you’re a platform, franchise, or local business and want to understand how we can help drive incremental revenue for your business, feel free to reach out! 

P.S. We’re hiring across multiple teams. If you know great people, my inbox is open.

Last June, I launched Frost LC A Law Corporation. In the year since, I haven’t announced it or marketed much. I just got to work on building a long-term foundation and delivering for clients.

A year later, I’m proud of the results:

- An incredible roster of clients, both individual and institutional
- A diverse practice, representing some clients as plaintiffs, some as defendants, some as corporate counsel, and many in the messy prelitigation phase of disputes where accusations are flying in both directions
- For some representations, reaching practical solutions tailored to my clients’ goals
- For other representations, advocating zealously in the biggest dispute that the client has ever faced
- On the plaintiff side, multiple seven-figure resolutions
- On the defense side, several favorable settlements
- On the corporate side, practical solutions and advice to help individuals and startups move forward

So, after a year of hard but quiet work, it was time to say something. And what I want to say is, “thank you.”

I want to thank my family and friends who have supported me through this journey.

I want to thank those at my former firm Lewis & Llewellyn LLP for their continued support and friendship.  It’s one of the best litigation boutiques in California.  I’m lucky to have worked with and learned from Marc Lewis, Paul Llewellyn, and many other former colleagues for almost eight years.  And I’m grateful they were enthusiastic when I first told them I wanted to start my own firm.

I want to thank Judge Joseph C. Spero, who gave me an opportunity to work with him and his wonderful clerks for a year.  I learned so much from them about practice—how to do it well, and how to do it with the highest degree of integrity.

I want to thank those at my earlier career stops, the California Department of Justice, the Superior Court of California, County of Kern, and my many clinics and externships while at the University of Minnesota Law School ranging from the U.S. Attorney’s Office in the District of Minnesota to the nonprofit Northside Residents Redevelopment Council (NRRC).

And I want to thank my friends at BakerHostetler for encouraging me to go to law school when I was a member of the LA Office staff.

Many of my career stops were incredible experiences. Others were tough life lessons.  But all were formative, making me uniquely suited to launch this firm and deliver results for my clients.

Now, I focus on commercial litigation for people and companies in the tech ecosystem: founders, executives, investors, venture funds, startups, and publicly traded companies.  I also provide fractional services for companies that need experienced litigation oversight or practical commercial advice and services without committing to a full-time in-house counsel.

Today, we’re launching Town: the AI assistant that learns you.

Right now, getting real value from AI often means learning how to prompt, configuring systems, building workflows, and managing agents.

We think that’s backwards.

AI shouldn't require people to become operators. It should just know you.

The product is simple: a personalized AI assistant, your Townie, that understands your world and helps you complete tasks and stay organized, becoming more useful the longer you work together.

Town connects across your inbox, calendar, Slack, docs, messages, and workflows to understand how you actually work, then starts doing the work with you.
