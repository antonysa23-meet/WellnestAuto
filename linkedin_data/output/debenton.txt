Deborah Benton is sufficiently skeptical of celebrity brands. She's also the investor behind Katy Perry's De Soi, Gal Gadot's Goodles, and Molly Sims' YSE Beauty.

She just joined me on the HotStart VC Podcast to tell the full story.

Deborah is the founder and Managing Partner of Willow Growth Partners, an emerging growth consumer fund focused on better-for-you brands across food, beauty, and personal care. Celebrity and creator-founded brands were never the core thesis, but several have cleared her bar.

Here is why she is skeptical. Most celebrity brands fail not because the product is bad or the celebrity is wrong for the category. They fail because the hard conversations never happen upfront. What is the celebrity's role? What does success mean for them? How does this fit into the rest of their obligations? Are expectations aligned between the talent and the operating team? When those questions go unanswered, the brand falls apart. And Deborah has seen it happen too many times.

She breaks down why massive celebrities like Katy Perry are better for awareness while smaller creators like Amber Fillerup are better for conversion, why she passes on pre-launch celebrity brands raising at $50 to $100 million valuations, and why a creator can move a deal from yes to no just as easily as they can move it from no to yes.

Her advice for investors evaluating celebrity brands: be very disciplined and diligence the relationship. The massive success stories are very, very few. What we don't see are the many, many brands that quietly didn't work.

We are proud to share that KilgourMD has closed its Series A, led by Prelude Growth Partners, and with continued participation from Willow Growth Partners.

Why Willow Growth Partners doubled down and continued to invest:

We first partnered with KilgourMD because we believed James Kilgour, MD FAAD was building something both deeply needed and highly differentiated. As a founder, James is a rare gem: he combines exceptional clinical credibility as an Oxford- and Stanford-trained, board-certified dermatologist with a clear understanding of an underserved problem, a personal connection to consumers, and a disciplined vision for how to solve it. As a practicing dermatologist, James crafted a brand based on science and clinical studies, and one that speaks to patients with deep understanding, credibility, warmth, and empathy.

What particularly stood out to us was his view that scalp health is not adjacent to hair regrowth. It is foundational to it.

In a category long defined by compromise, harsh side effects, limited efficacy, and little real innovation, KilgourMD is taking a fundamentally different approach: clinically proven, science-backed products designed to address scalp aging and hair thinning at the root. We believe that is where the future of the category is headed.

The company’s extraordinary momentum reflects the strength of that thesis as well as the efficacy of the brand’s products. KilgourMD is pairing meaningful clinical validation with strong consumer resonance, proving that efficacy, trust, and brand love can and should coexist. James and the team are not simply building a product line. They are helping define a new standard in scalp health and hair regrowth, with empathy and humanity, especially important during times when patients feel most vulnerable and insecure. 

We are thrilled to continue supporting KilgourMD in this next chapter and excited to welcome our friends at Prelude Growth Partners to the partnership.

Congratulations to James and the entire KilgourMD team on this incredible milestone!
