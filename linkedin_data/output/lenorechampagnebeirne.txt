Post

I used to think agency was mostly about momentum. Clarity, conviction, the ability to move forward.

Building Bright and co-creating Asteri have taught me it's also something deeper.

Agency is the ability to stay rooted in your principles while allowing their expression to evolve. Knowing when to act, and also when to pause long enough to listen. Learning to trust yourself, other people, and a future that will never fully reveal itself in advance.

Last week, we gathered in Ojai for the '5th Annual Asteri Women's Wealth + Innovation Summit'. The theme was Agency, and it felt deeply personal.

After twelve years at Bright, the through-line has never been a single strategy. It's been a set of commitments: to human potential, to capital that builds rather than extracts, to leadership grounded in responsibility rather than performance.

Those commitments have stayed steady. But their expressions have surprised me.

I didn't set out to build a women's wealth and leadership community. And yet, Asteri emerged because those principles met the vision, belief, and gifts of other people – Maia’s vision for next-generation leadership, @Lili’s belief in what women can do in financial markets, Dani’s excellence in execution, and Aisha’s ability to translate vision into reality.

That is one of agency's quieter lessons: your principles may lead you somewhere your personality never would have planned. There is humility in that, and also freedom.

Coming back to Ojai – where, incidentally, the Bright team first sat with the question of whether we could create a venture fund that helped build the world we wanted to see – felt like a continuation of that same practice. 

Remembering that agency isn't about controlling everything, especially in a world of uncertainty. It's about taking stock of what is true, what we have, what we can shape, and who we can trust to build alongside us.

Again and again, I've seen what becomes possible when women have the space to clarify what they believe and the community to help them act on it. An inkling becomes a thesis, a question turns into a commitment, and a timely conversation leads to moving capital more with greater intention.

Five years in, I'm deeply grateful for every person who has helped shape Asteri into what it is becoming. To everyone who gathered with us in Ojai: thank you for your presence, your candor, your ambition, your care, and your trust.

The future will never fully reveal itself in advance. But we can practice becoming the kind of people who are ready to meet it, and we can do that together.

Post

As I plan for the year ahead, I’ve been reflecting on a few threads that shaped my work and conviction in 2025.

This last year brought many meaningful discussions - from speaking at the Stanford Responsible AI Summit and the Women’s Venture Summit, to the BLK VC’s Inaugural “Call to Capital” Gala and featured thought leadership in VC Journal – spaces where I had the opportunity to share what we’re building at Bright, listen deeply to others, and sharpen my own thinking in the process. 

It was also a year of continued momentum as we advanced the work of raising another fund. The process has been both grounding and clarifying. A reminder that the strongest alignment comes not from speed, but from shared values, trust, and long-term perspective.

I’m incredibly proud of our portfolio companies for not only navigating an unpredictable year, but growing through it, with Forerunner, Mae, and Aware Health raising a Series A and B; ryse and Minerra Health rapidly increasing revenue; Tandem, Nav.it and Options MD getting acquired, and exciting headway and recognition for Ohai.ai and others.

More than anything, last year brought a deeper conviction in what we believe and what we’re building:
- Investing in human potential means investing in the leaders, not just the innovations, that are shaping and building our future. 
-Reimagining institutions and systems change are shaped through bold vision and deep collaboration.
- And capital, when stewarded with clarity of purpose, can be a powerful force for generative change.

I’m grateful to everyone who’s been part of these conversations and this journey - our founders, investors, partners, and the Bright team. 

Thank you for the trust, the questions, and the shared commitment to reimagining what’s possible. Looking ahead with a great sense of appreciation and deepened commitment to my life’s work: 

Investing in human potential and building a brighter future. ✨

Agency is not the absence of uncertainty – it’s how we move through it. Recognizing what we do hold, what we can shape, and where we choose to carve out a path forward.

This year marked five years of the Asteri Women in Wealth Collective. We brought our community together for our "Annual Women’s Wealth + Innovation Summit" at the Ojai Valley Inn and it was a deeply meaningful few days.

The theme, Agency, was especially timely. 

The economic headwinds are real.
The political landscape is turbulent.
The social fabric feels fragile.

And for many leaders, those forces do not stay abstract. They land in our businesses, our families, our communities, and our bodies.

So this year, we returned to a grounding question: What can we shape from here – both individually and collectively?

Over three days, we explored the macro forces shaping the world around us, from the economy and women’s wealth to climate, sustainability, and the future of capital.

We moved inward through conversations on intention, influence, abundance, and leadership.

And we closed with practical tools for directing portfolios, philanthropy, and investment decisions with greater clarity and purpose.

Thank you to the incredible speakers who shared their insights, expertise and personal journeys: Analise Roland, Ebony Bloom, Jade Chatham, Jenifer Sapel, CPWA®, Kyla Scanlon, @Kasey Crown, Kate Nevin, Jessica M., Nicola B., Sarah Angello, Sheila Klehm. 

Thank you to our sponsors and partners who helped make this gathering possible: MALIAM, UBS, Apollo Global Management, Inc., and Novilla Pharmaceuticals. 

We are truly grateful for this community and to everyone who made this year’s Summit such a meaningful reminder of what becomes possible when women come together to reclaim agency, deepen alignment, and move forward – together.

Accelerators in the venture landscape have proved that structured founder support works. However they have a limit, naturally. 

After accelerator programs end, founders are often left facing the toughest stretch of venture building alone, with no one guiding their decisions or supporting their ongoing resilience. 

Studies show that founders who lose access to structured mentoring see a drop in decision quality and slower operational progress.

At Bright Ventures, coaching is embedded into every stage of our investment and portfolio management process. It’s not episodic or reactive. 

It evolves alongside the founder and the company, adapting as the challenges change. That means ongoing, personalized guidance that is measurable and strategic.

Founder support and resilience translates to real results: clearer decisions, faster pivots, and growth that holds over time.

This is how we think about long-term value creation: by building leadership capacity as durable infrastructure, not temporary support.… more
