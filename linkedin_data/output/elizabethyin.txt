"It's not your job to know the market. You're not the one selling into it."

That line from Elizabeth Yin reframed how Cheryl and I think about investing across borders.

Hustle Fund has done something almost no other Bay Area fund has done well through the cycles: invest globally, at pre-seed, and keep doing it.

So I asked her the obvious question: how do you back founders in markets you're not physically in?

When the investors you're pitching are overseas, they need to understand the local dynamics before any of your numbers make sense to them.

The founders who are most successful at raising international capital are the ones who do that education work upfront, and transfer context as part of the pitch and diligence process.

If you're preparing to raise from investors outside of your market, don't take this piece for granted.
