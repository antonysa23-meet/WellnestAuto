MIT CSAIL Director, Co-Founder, Leader, Professor, Author, Entrepreneur, Inventor, Board Member

2mo • Edited • 

The first-ever MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL) Agentic AI Hackathon was a wonderful display of creativity, technical ambition, and what can happen when extraordinary talent is given a weekend and a meaningful challenge.

Over the course of the weekend, 24 teams built agentic systems that ranged from novel data-generation systems for humanoid robots to browsing support for visually impaired users, an AI running coach, an agent that finds mistakes in papers, an agent that helps you stay focused, an agent that automates workflows, an agent that optimizes token utilization, and more. The range of ideas was remarkable, and so was the level of execution.

What made the event especially exciting was how grounded the projects were in real utility. The guiding questions were simple: Does it work? Is the task worth doing? Would you use it again next week? Many of the demos answered all three with a strong yes.

Congratulations to all of the teams. The talent on display was truly extraordinary. It was inspiring to see how much could be built in such a short time, and how many of the projects felt less like demos and more like the beginning of startups and of tools people will actually want to use.

Many thanks to our sponsors Dell Technologies, Delta Electronics, Microsoft, Glasswing Ventures, Link Ventures, JPMorganChase, Google, Jane Street, and OpenRouter for making this event possible.

We are also very grateful to the many advisors who joined the demos and discussions, including John Werner, Frazer Anderson, Daniel Duecker, Rachel Shalom, Ali Mahmoud, Chareeda R., Millie Liu, Rob Lincourt, Adel Hanna, and Rudina Seseri.

A wonderful weekend of creativity, technical ambition, and real progress at MIT CSAIL. The future of agentic AI looks bright and apparently does not sleep much.… more

View company: BIOKET Bioeconomy Key Enabling Technologies platform
BIOKET Bioeconomy Key Enabling Technologies platform

7mo • 

🔬 Enzymit and Royal Cosun announce a major milestone in sustainable biomanufacturing: the world's first successful pilot-scale production of hyaluronic acid (HA) using an enzyme-only, cell-free process at 200-liter scale.

The breakthrough campaign produced multi-kilogram quantities of high-purity HA, demonstrating superior process efficiency and product consistency compared to traditional fermentation methods.

Key highlights:

🧬 First cell-free process enabling precise control over HA molecular size during production
📏 Produces HA in defined sizes from ~10 kDa to ~4 MDa with narrow size distribution
🤖 Powered by AI-designed enzymes, eliminating biological bottlenecks of cell-based methods
⚡ Faster, cleaner, and more efficient than conventional fermentation
💰 Addresses growing market valued at $9.43B (2025), projected to reach $15.94B by 2035
🎯 Applications span dermal fillers, wound healing, drug delivery, and tissue engineering

The technology demonstrates Enzymit's cell-free biomanufacturing platform's ability to synthesize complex bioproducts without living cells, marrying the best of biology and chemical engineering. By replacing cell-based methods with enzyme-driven synthesis, the approach removes traditional biological constraints and enables precision manufacturing of biomolecules.

The successful scale-up was achieved in partnership with Cosun, an agricultural cooperative and leader in sustainable, circular production of plant-based ingredients, utilizing existing manufacturing assets to bring this innovative cell-free technology to industrial reality.
