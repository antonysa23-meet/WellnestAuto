About
Dayna Grayson is Cofounder and Managing Partner of Construct Capital, an early stage venture firm that invests in founders who are recreating the most foundational industries of our economy that comprise over half of the US GDP with software and technical architecture. The Construct portfolio spans sectors including manufacturing, supply chain, critical infrastructure, energy and physical AI that impacts all of these industrial sectors. Some investments include Hadrian, Copia, Veho, Vammo, AMCA, Stacked Energy, and many others.

Dayna was one of the first venture capitalists to lean into transformation of industrial sectors during her tenure as a partner at NEA (2012-2020) where she led investments in companies such as Desktop Metal (2015 - 2025; board member), Tulip (2017 - 2020; board member), Onshape (acqd by PTC; 2013-2019 board member), and Framebridge (acqd by Graham Holdings; 2014-2020 board member) as well as companies such as Guideline (acqd by Gusto), Neuralmagic (acqd by Redhat), and Formlabs. 

Dayna started her career in product and entrepreneurial roles after helping to found a company in college focused on eye gaze responsiveness to control computer interfaces. She then led product development and design efforts at Blackbaud [BLKB], the leading global provider of software to nonprofit organizations, as the company grew to over $130 million in revenue and completed a successful public offering.  She is a graduate of the University of Virginia (Systems Engineering) and Harvard Business School, where she has served as a venture partner.

We are so grateful for our limited partners, many who have been with us since Fund I, and the founders who have partnered with us at Construct. With our freshly closed $300M Fund III, we are doubling down on our focus, leaning into our pairing of a boutique style and commercial mindset, and emphasizing the gap in the market for true early stage funds. 
1. Doubling down (tripling down?) on our focus on rebuilding the Foundational Industries of our economy. After growing lockstep with the rest of the economy, the industrial sectors diverged 20+ years ago, after the dot com crash. While the rest of the economy recovered in domestic productivity, these sectors leaned into labor arbitrage and other forms of off-shore cost savings. The result: these sectors completely missed the capabilities of the internet, cloud, mobile, and big data tech. But they must catch up and in doing so, there is a huge opportunity to build tech-first industrial companies capable of reshaping our economy AND our public markets. 
2. Leaning into our boutique style with a commercial mindset. We invest in less than two dozen companies per fund which makes us different from the seed funds and multi stage firms who have large portfolios. We strive to give dedicated attention especially in the early days at critical moments where small decisions can drive long term success.  We aren’t just focused on getting our companies to the next round - we have the experience to support founders through the full journey of company building.
3. Rejuvenating an early stage approach to venture partnerships. We set the target and the hard cap of our fund at $300M in size because we believe this is the best size for our early stage investing strategy. We believe there’s a critical moment to return to true early stage fund vehicles that can allocate time, full team support and proper check sizes to their investments and we are so thankful for all of those who wanted to join us on this mission. 

****Finally, I cannot say enough about the incredible team we have built. From those with us from Day 0 to the impressive investors who have joined us recently, we are so proud of our team who is rife with investing, operating and entrepreneurial experience. We craft our firm to have deep appreciation for the hard work our founders do and everyone we bring on has the drive to do the hard work for our companies. The internal team (as much as our external founder teams) are what make me excited to come to work every day! *****

The hardest energy infrastructure problem isn't the one everyone is talking about.

AI data centers are straining the grid, but the structural failure runs deeper. Pacific Island nations spend billions annually on diesel with no alternative. Naval installations in the Pacific face real supply chain vulnerability. Coastal desalination plants have nowhere to turn as water scarcity accelerates.

This is the problem Endurance Energy was founded to solve — and it's why we are proud to invest in their $54M Series A alongside Founders Fund Riot Ventures and Point72

Most of the planet's accessible geothermal energy sits under the ocean. Endurance drills shallow wells along mid-ocean ridges — 5x shallower than traditional enhanced geothermal — to access superheated water up to 450°C, spinning turbines in modular, factory-built generator units on the seafloor. Firm, clean power delivered to shore via submarine cable. At gigawatt scale, this is potentially the cheapest and most reliable clean energy on Earth.

Andrew Redd led critical systems on Starship at SpaceX before founding Endurance, and the team has just deployed its first hardware to the seafloor in August 2025. The physics are proven. What comes next is deployment. Onward.

For decades the conversation around AI infrastructure has centered on models, chips, and software. Increasingly, the bottleneck is something far more fundamental and essential: energy.

Earth’s existing power grid was not designed for the pace and scale of the current AI era. Across major markets, data centers are already facing years-long waits for grid access as demand for compute accelerates faster than existing energy systems can keep up.

Today, Cowboy Space Corporation (formerly Aetherflux) announced its $275M Series B to build orbital infrastructure for the AI era. We are honored to participate in this round alongside an incredible group of co-investors and proud that we have been able to support this incredible team since galvanizing and investing in their Series A last year. 

From the beginning, we believed Baiju Bhatt and team were pursuing something much bigger than a traditional space company. They are rethinking the relationship between energy, compute, and space infrastructure.

What makes this especially compelling to us is that Cowboy is not treating launch vehicles and satellites as separate systems. Their approach rethinks the stack: the rocket’s upper stage becomes the data center itself, powered by continuous solar energy in Low Earth Orbit.

This is the kind of “physical AI” infrastructure we believe will define the next decade of ambitious engineering challenges that drive the rebuilding of our most Foundational Industries in the US - those that sit at the intersection of energy, manufacturing, compute, and national competitiveness.

At Construct Capital, we’ve been investing from the beginning behind the notion that whether it’s factories, supply chains, transportation networks, or now the power grid itself, transformative companies are built by founders willing to rethink how complex systems should be purpose built, from the ground up.
Earth’s grid can’t keep pace with AI. So the team at CSC is building a new one.

Excited to support their efforts!
