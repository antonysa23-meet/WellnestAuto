View Seyed H. Madaeni, Ph.D.’s profile
Seyed H. Madaeni, Ph.D.

  • 3rd+

Co-founder & CEO at Verse Inc.

2w • Edited • 

The race to AI supremacy is no longer just a race for chips. It is a race for power.

Every AI model, every gigascale data center, and every query we run depends on electricity. A single ChatGPT query can require many times more energy than a standard Google search. At global scale, this surge in demand is pushing aging energy infrastructure to its breaking point.

The most advanced software systems ever built are now constrained by one of the oldest physical challenges in the world: access to the grid. Miss your interconnection window in tight markets like PJM, and your data center could wait years — in some cases close to a decade — to get online. Developers are losing time they simply do not have.

Today, I am thrilled to announce that Verse has raised an oversubscribed $54M Series B led by Bessemer Venture Partners, with participation from GV (Google Ventures), NVIDIA, and Norrsken VC.

I want to especially thank Lindsey Li and David Cowan at Bessemer for believing in our vision and in the scale of the problem we are working to solve. From our earliest conversations, they understood that the next great infrastructure bottleneck for AI would not only be compute. It would be power.

But software alone cannot plug directly into the dirt. To truly solve the speed-to-power crisis, we are launching Dispatch Intelligence™ alongside a major new strategic collaboration with Calibrant Energy. Thank you Philip Martin for all the strategic thinking and vision.

Most legacy approaches force data centers to make a painful choice: curtail or shift compute workloads to accommodate the grid. We believe that is a false compromise.

Dispatch Intelligence takes a fundamentally different path. With Calibrant, we are turning that path into a full-stack solution. By intelligently orchestrating physical, behind-the-meter energy assets right where compute happens, we can present a flexible load to the utility without impacting performance.

Data center operators can run at full capacity, bypass multi-year utility bottlenecks, and bring capacity online years faster. We are enabling a new model: power delivered on-site and on-demand, matching energy infrastructure to the speed of AI innovation without slowing down a single GPU.

When Matt Penfold and I co-founded Verse, we bet on a future where software-driven, distributed energy resources would define grid resilience. That future arrived much faster than anyone anticipated.

We are here to make sure the AI era is not left waiting on electrons.

Philip Martin Abhijoy Mitra Roni Hiranand Michael McBride Amit Mukherjee Agate S. Freimane Cody Simms Leshika Samarasinghe Brad Armstrong

Michael Manapat

  • 3rd+

Co-Founder at Rowspace

4mo • Edited • 

I've gotten to spend time over the past year with some of the most forward-thinking investors in the world, learning where AI is meeting their needs and where it isn’t.

All of these firms know they need to drive AI transformation on a fast timeline. But, as one put it bluntly: “All these AI tools are only as good as the data you feed them, and ours is a mess.”

This may be true, but their data also represents decades spent building proprietary knowledge that codifies how they think, operate, and win. It just happens to be scattered and fragmented across many systems. The real challenge is scaling this edge to make faster, sharper decisions. There's no quick-win alpha.

That's why we built Rowspace—launching today with $50M from Sequoia Capital, Emergence Capital, Stripe, Conviction, Basis Set, Twine Ventures, and many others.

What we do:
-Map firm data comprehensively—memos, decks, models, ledgers, positions, etc. from systems like Salesforce, DealCloud, Box, Egnyte, SharePoint, Everest, Dynamo, Snowflake, PowerBI, and more. We do this in our customers’ environments for maximum data security.
-Model how they actually work—reconciling conflicts, deciding what to trust, what to prioritize, what to ignore. We extract meaning and make it available on demand. (It's one thing to recall an answer from a database. It's another to know which addbacks apply to an EBITDA calculation based on a specific credit covenant.)
-Push that intelligence into the tools where decisions happen—chat, real-time dashboards, Excel, Teams, wherever you already work—with full traceability down to the calculation.

We're already working with some of the most storied firms in the industry: a global growth pioneer with 50 years of history, a crossover fund invested in the most notable tech IPOs of the last decade, and one of the leading credit originators in the world. They chose Rowspace for the depth, nuance, and specificity we’re able to deliver.

Try Rowspace if your firm wants to accelerate with AI in ways you can trust.
Or come build with us if you want to build at this level of depth for customers shaping our economy.… more

CEO & Founder at Alaffia Health | Forbes 30 Under 30

5mo • 

Six years ago, I left my job at Goldman Sachs and turned down a private equity job I had worked toward for years, often through 80hr+ weeks. While I was excited to invest in businesses that were transforming industries, I realized that my real passion was creating impact from the driver’s seat in a space I cared deeply about. So Adun, Oyinade, and I had an idea, which we put into a simple framework: healthcare + AI (before it was cool) = potential for outsized & positive impact for everyone. 

Growing up, we watched our parents build a provider revenue cycle management business. We saw firsthand the complexity and inefficiency that healthcare organizations (providers, payers, and vendors alike) navigate every day, particularly due to limited innovation and tech adoption. That exposure stayed with us, so we got to work.

We built the first version of Alaffia, raised our initial round of funding, and hired our first team members. There have been many long days/nights since, and many moments when things didn’t go as planned. But staying resilient and keeping our mission front and center is what kept us going until today. 

That mission: reduce billing errors, waste, and administrative burden to directly and quantifiably reduce healthcare costs for all.

We've come a long way since embarking on that mission. Today, I'm incredibly proud to announce that we've raised $55M in Series B funding led by Transformation Capital, bringing our total capital raised to $73M.

This funding is a testament to the passion and dedication of the entire Alaffia Health team and to everything we've accomplished together. With over $120M in savings delivered for our health plan partners so far, I'm more bullish on our thesis than ever and more confident in what we’re building.

I feel so fortunate to be sharing this journey with Transformation Capital and all our incredible investors who are doubling down in this round, including FirstMark, Tau Ventures, and Twine Ventures. 

I'm incredibly grateful for the entire Alaffia team, including so many of us who are still here since we got started. It’s a new day for affordability in healthcare, and we’re just getting started. Stay tuned!
