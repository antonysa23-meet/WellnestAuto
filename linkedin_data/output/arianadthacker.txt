We just did the most software thing a healthcare company can do ⬇️ 



This weekend, we started giving patients the first month of membership for free (all they pay for is the cost of their meds).

When we looked at our internal data, 2 things jumped out:

1) Retention is great. Patients stay because they love our care, and our NPS is through the roof. People are getting meaningful improvements they've been waiting years for.

2) From a symptoms perspective, that progress happens fast, with 95% of our patients seeing symptom reduction within the first 30 days of their membership. That progress continues over time. 

The most difficult thing for patients in the new market that are not used to addressing the root cause is to get them in.

And the whole thing made sense. Our typical patient has been sick for years, they've paid for doctors who shrugged, supplements that did nothing, answers that went nowhere... And they've even normalized their chronic symptoms. 

Then we show up with a new idea, that mold could be the root cause keeping them inflamed, and we ask them to pay up front to find out if we're right. 

We've all been trained to treat health symptom by symptom, so it takes time to even accept that mold could be the root cause keeping you inflamed.

To that brain, the money is a real loss, happening right now and feeling better is a maybe somewhere down the line.

That's not a pricing problem, as you can't discount your way out of a fear. The only way to beat it is to remove the thing the fear is attached to.

So we did what the best software companies have done for years: 

Give people the product first and let them taste it before they bet anything on it. A free trial only works if you know people stay once they're in, and we know they do.

If you're in a state where we're active and you qualify for care, your first month of membership is free. We believe in the care enough to let it prove itself to you.

2w • Edited • 

After my own experience with mold exposure, I have spent years fighting for a moment like this...

The MOLD Act is now in the Senate NDAA!

As Director of Public Policy at Change the Air Foundation, I had the honor to help lead the advocacy effort that drove this legislation forward alongside incredible military families and allied organizations.

The MOLD Act establishes enforceable health and safety housing standards for privatized military housing, mandates independent inspections by certified third parties, requires ANSI/IICRC S520 mold remediation, and gives military families the direct power to withhold housing payments when their unit fails inspection. If a housing contractor won’t fix the problem, the taxpayer money stops. That is a level of accountability that has never existed in military housing before.

This is the result of years of work by military families who were courageous enough to step forward to share their stories, allied partners who also helped amplify their voices, and a small but passionate team at Change the Air who refused to quit.

Thank you Senator Richard Blumenthal for championing this important legislation. While the fight is not over, today marks an important milestone to getting this passed into law.

Military families defend our nation every day. They deserve to be defended in their own homes. 

We're here to make sure that they are.

Military Officers Association of America (MOAA) IICRC Stephanie Rose Robbie Bradshaw, Esq. National Military Family Association

Yesterday was a big day for healthy building innovation! Alongside a packed house of healthy building experts, leaders and advocates, it was inspiring to be on hand for the Advanced Research Projects Agency for Health (ARPA-H)’s Healthy Buildings Demo Day. I came away more convinced than ever that we are witnessing what may be the world's most visionary R&D effort on healthy indoor air.

For decades, indoor air quality has remained largely invisible despite its profound impact on human health. ARPA-H’s BREATHE and PRO-MICROBE programs are helping change that by rapidly accelerating breakthrough technologies that can make indoor air visible, actionable and scalable across our schools, hospitals, homes and other critical spaces.

It was a special moment, too, when leaders from the Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America, IFMA and the International WELL Building Institute (IWBI) presented ARPA-H with a Leadership in Innovation Award for its groundbreaking work through BREATHE and PRO-MICROBE, which together reflect a groundbreaking effort to revolutionize how indoor environments are understood, monitored and optimized to protect and enhance human health.  A special thank you again to ARPA-H Director Alicia Jackson, BREATHE Program Manager Jessica Green, and the four extraordinary innovator teams led by Poppy, Mayo Clinic, Virginia Tech, and SafeTraces, Inc. for their vision, leadership and progress. Less than a year into these efforts, the momentum is remarkable.
