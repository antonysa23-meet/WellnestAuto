Low-cost technology is rewriting the rules of warfare in real time, but the most sophisticated software in the world is useless without a secure physical foundation.

I joined Jack Otter on the Barron's Roundtable this weekend to discuss why national security innovation is only as durable as the materials that make it possible. Right now, we are facing a hard truth: China still holds the midstream chokepoint on critical minerals. More than a trade dispute, this is a huge strategic vulnerability.

Further to the discussion is why "Industrial Diplomacy" is the new frontline. Initiatives like Pax Silica and Project VAULT are the market-defining catalysts that allow the private sector to finally de-risk from Chinese price-dumping. If we can’t process what we mine, we can’t surge production of the systems that matter most when it matters most.

From Direct Kinetic Solutions, Everlasting Power’ 20-year batteries to Beamlink’s rapid-deploy connectivity, the technology is moving faster than the legacy supply chain can keep up. Closing that gap is both an investment opportunity and a national security mandate.

A huge thank you to Jack Otter, Beth Finegan, and Sarah Schornstein. The Barron's Roundtable team is exceptional, and I left deeply impressed by the depth of the conversation they’re driving.

The "Silent" Rail Fracture – Closing the Detection Gap 🛑🛤️

The preliminary CIAF update on the Adamuz derailment highlights a technically uncomfortable reality for high-speed rail:
There are indications consistent with a rail fracture existing before the derailment, and several trains passed over the location with wheel damage compatible with impacts in that area. While this hypothesis remains preliminary and subject to further analysis, it points to a critical challenge.

This does not mean that signaling failed. Public information does not yet detail the exact behavior of the track circuits, or other detection systems. What it does reveal, however, is a practical safety gap that deserves attention: 
👉 #Occupancy detection alone does not guarantee structural awareness of the rail : A partially fractured or electrically bridged rail may continue to be detected as occupied  while its mechanical integrity is already compromised.

So how do we close that detection gap? Four pragmatic lines of action:

1. #Monitoring: As discussed last week, we currently lack SIL4-certified broken rail detection systems. However, waiting for a SIL4 solution before acting is a missed opportunity. A Non-Vital monitoring layer (like Fiber Optic DAS, Acoustic Monitoring or vibration-based solutions) can provide a critical early-warning layer, detecting abnormal structural behaviour and localizing it in near real time.

2. From Detection to #Operational Mitigation: Even without automatic intervention in the interlocking logic, real-time alerts enable immediate Operational Procedures:
 -) Emergency Speed Restrictions (TSR): Reducing speed in the suspect section significantly lowers the dynamic impact on the fracture.
 -) Driver Advisories: Alerting drivers to "Proceed with Caution" and report any unusual shocks or noise.
 -) Urgent Visual Inspection: Dispatching maintenance teams to the exact location provided by the broken rail detection monitoring system.

3. Wheel Impact Load Detectors (#WILD) These trackside sensors measure the vertical force of every wheel. In Adamuz, three trains sustained notches or flat spots before the derailment. A WILD system could have flagged these impact signatures, alerting subsequent trains before reaching the site. While it seems that the Madrid-Sevilla line is equipped with WILD, their specific location and operative status during the event are part of the investigation.

4 The Role of Rolling Stock: Modern On-Board Monitoring (#OBM) can detect abnormal vertical accelerations and impact events at wheelset level. If the first train experiencing an abnormal impact reports it in real time, following trains could operate under restriction or be stopped until inspection confirms track integrity.

#Key reflecion for today post: In the era of High-Speed Rail, Occupancy Detection is no longer sufficient on its own. We need Rail Structural Health Awareness.

Everyone's fighting over gigawatt data centers. The real AI infrastructure play is sitting empty in your city.

That old warehouse with 5 MW of power? It's worth 10x what it was three years ago. And nobody's paying attention yet.

-The Hidden AI Gold Rush-

While Meta builds $29B facilities for training models, the actual AI revolution needs something different: distributed compute for real-world applications.

Self-driving cars can't wait for data to travel to Virginia and back. Drones need sub-10ms response times. Manufacturing robots require local processing to avoid catastrophic delays.

These applications don't need 500 MW campuses. They need 1-10 MW facilities within 50 miles of where they operate.

-Why These Sites Are Suddenly Valuable-

Power availability in urban areas is the constraint. New utility connections take 2-4 years. But existing sites? Already connected.

An old warehouse with 5 MW can become an edge data center in 9 months. Try getting that power allocation new - you'll wait until 2029.

The economics:
- Acquisition: $5-15M
- Conversion: $10-20M  
- Value post-conversion: $50-80M
- Timeline: 9-12 months vs 3-4 years

-Who's Already Moving-

Vapor IO is dropping micro data centers at cell towers - supporting autonomous vehicle corridors. Partnering with Hangar for drone operations.

EdgeConneX raised $1.9B to convert suburban facilities. They're buying existing industrial sites with power in place.

DataBank is turning dead office buildings into edge facilities. 2-10 MW conversions where nobody's looking.

Locus Robotics needs edge compute for warehouse robots. Every fulfillment center running AMRs needs local processing.

-The Use Cases Driving Demand-

Autonomous Vehicles: Waymo and Cruise need compute every 10-15 miles. Can't process in the cloud at 45mph.

Drone Delivery: Amazon Prime Air, Zipline require local processing for collision avoidance.

Smart Manufacturing: Boston Dynamics robots need sub-5ms latency. Compute must be within 10 miles.

Healthcare: Surgical robots can't risk network delays. Hospitals are building their own edge facilities.

-What Makes a Site Valuable-

Power: 1-10 MW available capacity
Location: Within 50 miles of population centers
Fiber: Proximity to major routes
Zoning: Industrial/flex allowing data center use
Loading: Existing docks for equipment delivery

-The Window Is Closing-

We're seeing 3-5x appreciation on these sites. A client bought a 3 MW Dallas building for $8M in 2022. Current offers exceed $25M.

This arbitrage won't last. Once institutional capital realizes edge is where AI deployment happens - not training - these sites disappear.

Smart money is quietly accumulating these properties. Not for industrial use. For the infrastructure that makes autonomous systems work.

The hyperscalers are building AI brains. These edge sites? They're the nervous system making AI useful.
