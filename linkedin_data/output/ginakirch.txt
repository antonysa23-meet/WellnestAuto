The "Nylon 6,6 Gap" has long been the Achilles' heel of textile circularity. While Nylon 6 has seen recycling success, its more durable cousin, Nylon 6,6, has seen less than 1% recycling rates—until now.

View company: Epoch Biodesign
Epoch Biodesign

 

3mo • 

We’re announcing that we have closed a $12M strategic funding round, bringing total capital raised to over $50M. 

The round includes investment from lululemon, KOMPAS VC, Happiness Capital | Certified B Corp, Extantia, Leitmotif, and others.

The funds will accelerate our commercialisation strategy across apparel, automotive and industrial markets, underpinned by the completion of our demonstration nylon 6,6 biorecycling facility, which is under construction in London.

Our process deconstructs end-of-life nylon materials back to their chemical building blocks, using AI-engineered enzymes. The resulting monomers are used to produce virgin-quality nylon 6,6, without the need for petrochemical feedstock. This is true circularity, built for scale.

This announcement follows the signing of an MoU with INVISTA and comes as we continue the journey from multi-tonne to multi-kilotonne production.

Nylon 6,6 is a critical material across some of the world's most demanding industries. Circular production of that material, at quality and scale, is what we are here to deliver.

Check out the feature from Tim De Chant at TechCrunch linked in the comments below.
