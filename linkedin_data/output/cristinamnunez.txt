About
Cristina is the co-founder of True Beauty Ventures, an emerging growth fund dedicated to investing in and partnering with brands exclusively in beauty and wellness. She has spent her entire career in consumer products and has extensive experience as both an investor and operator. Cristina leverages her expertise and network in the beauty and wellness sector to identify, evaluate, and help founders and companies achieve their strategic, operational, and financial goals and help them navigate the challenges and opportunities of scaling their businesses.

We just launched our Fund III raise at True Beauty Ventures.

Six years ago, we were told our strategy was too narrow. Too niche. Not enough shots on goal.  What we have learned since then is the opposite.  Focus expands what you see. It creates repetition, pattern recognition, and ultimately the ability to distinguish what actually matters.

We do not win by seeing more. We win by recognizing what is different.
That has allowed us to build incredible deal flow, generate early DPI, and partner with brands before they become obvious.

At the same time, beauty, health, and wellness are converging, both from a consumer and exit perspective. The acquisition of Grüns by Unilever is another recent example of that shift.

As we launch Fund III, we are not a startup anymore. We have spent six years building the infrastructure, the network, and the brand to compete at a higher level.

I just published a new piece on Substack: The Forever Brand Paradox.
There’s a tension I see across almost every beauty and wellness brand right now, the balance between growth and longevity.

Retailers want newness. Consumers expect it. Brands feel pressure to keep launching. But do customers actually want more, or do they just want something that works?

In the piece, I explore why growth still matters, but the brands that actually last are the ones building around behavior, not just moments. 

Fast growth can signal momentum. It can also mask fragility. The difference comes down to whether a brand earns a place in someone’s life.

Would love to hear how others are thinking about this balance.

Building a consumer brand today requires more than a great product and a compelling story. Founders are navigating an increasingly competitive landscape where investors are evaluating not just the vision, but the signals that suggest a brand can truly scale. From early traction and customer loyalty to the structure of a founder’s first investor relationships, the earliest decisions can shape the trajectory of the entire company.

In this LinkedIn Live conversation, we sit down with Cristina Nuñez, cofounder of True Beauty Ventures, to unpack what investors are actually looking for when evaluating early-stage consumer brands. Drawing from her experience both investing in and operating within consumer companies, Cristina will share how investors assess traction before scale, the metrics that matter most in the 0-1 stage, and the signals that indicate real product-market fit.

Together, we’ll explore what makes a pitch stand out in today’s fundraising environment, how founders should think about assembling their earliest investors and advisors, and how to communicate the right data when your brand is still building momentum. The conversation will also dig into the founder–investor relationship itself, including how founders can choose capital strategically and build partnerships that support long-term growth.

This session is designed for founders across all consumer categories, including beauty, fashion, food and beverage, wellness, and lifestyle brands. Whether you are preparing to raise your first round, refining your metrics, or simply trying to understand how investors evaluate early-stage companies, this conversation will offer a clearer lens into what actually moves the needle.

About Cristina Nuñez

In 2020, Cristina co-founded True Beauty Ventures, a sector specialized investment firm focused on beauty, health, and wellness. She places an extra emphasis on brand strategy, portfolio support, marketing, and ecosystem development, while serving as a primary partner to founders on everything from product architecture to retail strategy, organizational development, and financial planning. She is also a prominent voice in the industry, regularly featured in WWD, Business of Fashion, BeautyMatter, Beauty Independent, Vogue Business, and Fashionista, and speaks frequently on topics ranging from category trends to founder resilience. She writes a widely read Substack newsletter called Beauty Is My Business, hosts mentorship sessions for emerging female talent, and champions access and education across the beauty and venture ecosystem.… more
