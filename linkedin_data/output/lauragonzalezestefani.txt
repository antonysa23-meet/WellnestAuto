About
Built TheVentureCity in 2017 to be the fund I wished existed when I was an operator.
Today: $145M+ AUM, 120+ portfolio companies across 14 countries, and a community of 500+ co-investors and founders that runs deeper than any term sheet. We're product-led, data-driven, and unapologetically unconventional.
We invest from pre-seed to Series B in product-led companies, mainly in the US and Europe, also in Latin America. Sectors we love: fintech, bioscience/healthtech, mobility & logistics, cybersecurity, senergy.
Before TVC: Early Days Facebook employee! In the International Growth from Silicon Valley. Before that, eBay, Siemens, Ogilvy. Co-founded Esplaya.com in 2000. I lead with what I learned shipping product, not from a finance textbook.
What makes us different: we're not a fund that also has a community. We had  a community before we launched our fund. The annual Summit, the Clandestinos, the founder gatherings, the 500+ co-investor network, that's the real asset. The fund is the tool we use to invest in it.
Proprietary tools we built: GrowthScanner.com, TheWatchList.AI, AHA (goaha.io). We invest like operators because we are operators.
Henry Crown Fellow (Aspen Institute, 2022). Member of the Investment Committee at the European Innovation Council Fund (2019–2022). Actually Board member at CaixaBank Payments & Consumer. Consejera at CDTI Innovación (Spain). Speaker at SuperReturn, MWC, and other places where ''serious'' people gather.

Mother of three. Optimist. Executor at heart. Builder. Limits breaker. In that order, but all at once.

Chile caught me before I fully understood why.

That's rare. Most ecosystems announce their potential loudly. Chile just builds.

Yesterday I joined Endeavor Chile’s  board for 2026-2029, alongside Diego Yarur , Jorge Nazer , and Trinidad Carmona . A group that takes this seriously.

And so do I, because I genuinely believe the next wave of world-class tech companies won't come from San Francisco or London. They'll come from places with deep industrial DNA, real problems worth solving, and the talent to do it at scale.

Chile has all three.

The country sits on top of more than half the world's lithium reserves. It produces a third of global copper. Its salmon industry is second only to Norway. It has some of the world's best conditions for solar and wind energy, and a mining sector that reinvents itself faster than  any other on the planet.

That's not a resource story. That's a tech story waiting to happen.

Climate tech, industrial AI, precision aquaculture, critical minerals supply chains, clean energy infrastructure: the founders who will define these categories globally might already be building in Santiago right now.

Endeavor has been betting on Chilean founders for nearly 30 years. I'm joining that table to help push harder.

The future gets built where the opportunities are. Chile has plenty of those. 🇨🇱… more

Congrats Alex Yancher and the Passport team!!!

We’d never seen a team that had built this level of depth: proprietary global shipping network, AI-driven fulfillment systems, in-country operations, and marketplace management across 220+ markets, trusted by 1,000+ merchants worldwide. Full-stack commerce enablement, not another aggregator.

Passport is expected to generate ~$100M in revenues in 2026 — growing faster than Global-e itself. Global-e (1,500+ brands, world’s leading DTC platform) looked at everything in the market and chose Passport. Alex will join their executive team to lead the integrated business. What this means for our Fund II, 35–45% IRR on a 20-month hold. Strong returns.

At TheVentureCity , we are deeply proud and genuinely fortunate that Alex trusted us to be part of this journey.

Congratulations to Ollie Hughes and the entire Count team on launching Count Agent! 

For builders and Data Teams you are going to ❤️ this:

Count has addressed the infrastructure bottleneck that many AI analytics tools face. Typically, data warehouses throttle AI’s speed, but Count has developed a distributed compute layer that intelligently divides work across your warehouse, their servers, and local machines. As a result, 60% of queries now run on users’ browsers, allowing for scalable AI analytics without escalating cloud costs.

The canvas approach is innovative. Unlike traditional chat interfaces that create black boxes, Count’s collaborative canvas enables humans and AI to work together transparently, showcasing the work, iterating in real-time, and producing assets that are editable, auditable, and reusable. This eliminates the need for “trust me” outputs.

Count is enterprise-ready from launch. With Count Metrics (their semantic layer), version control, and enterprise security, this solution can be deployed across entire organizations. Notably, their biggest agent users are already in the C-Suite, demonstrating the potential to expand beyond just data teams.

The technology is so effective that we’ve integrated it throughout our own data stack at TheVentureCity .

The Aspen Institute today announced its 2022 Class of Henry Crown Fellows. Meet my classmates http://as.pn/crown2022 
The Henry Crown Fellowship mobilizes a new breed of leaders, all between the ages
of 30-45, to tackle the world’s most intractable problems. All are proven entrepreneurial leaders, mostly from the private business sector, who have reached a point in their lives where, having
achieved success, they are ready to apply their creative talents and skill sets to building a better society. Henry Crown Fellows spend four weeks over the course of two years in structured retreat – exploring
their leadership, their core values, their vision for a Good Society and their desired legacies. But the Fellowship is not just about reflection. It is also about action: each Fellow launches a leadership venture that will stretch them and have a positive impact on their communities, their country, or the
world.

The Henry Crown Fellowship seeks to develop the next generation of community-spirited leaders, providing them with the tools to meet the challenges of the  21st century. It was established in 1997 to honor the life and career of Chicago industrialist Henry Crown (1896-1990) and was initially funded by the Henry and Gladys Crown Charitable Trust Fund. The program is the flagship Fellowship of the Aspen Institute and the founding Fellowship of the Aspen Global Leadership Network. For more information, visit http://www.aspeninstitute.org/crown.… more
