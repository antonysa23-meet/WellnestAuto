1w • Edited • 

After 11.5 years, I left Airbnb in May.

What an incredible run, and how lucky I am to have been part of the growth of such a special brand. I got to travel the world launching and scaling our Experiences business, then turned around and did the same for Services. What once took 100+ people and 3 years, we condensed into 9 months, thanks to AI and an amazing team.

During my time at Airbnb, I had the first-world problem of a hefty tax bill after the IPO. No one prepared me for that moment. My CPA filed my taxes, and my wealth advisor was focused on diversifying out of my stock over the coming years. It took a host at a community event to open my eyes to the tax benefits of hosting as a W2 employee.

So I bought my dream escape in The Sea Ranch. Tito Pedraza and Tawni Bannister helped me purchase, remodel, and host all in one year. The vehicle that allowed me to defer my taxes into the house is called Cost Segregation.
But the process was painful. Midway through my remodel, my CPA told me to spend more than we initially budgeted to effectively remove my full tax obligation (would have been good to know up front). It took 3 months to finalize the study and the initial report was riddled with errors. It became a mad dash to file in time. Meanwhile, my coworkers and other friends in tech with the same situation had no idea that a ski place in Tahoe or a beach house on the coast could absorb their tax bill.

My accountant was stressed, first trying to do the cost seg in-house, then scrambling to outsource it. Through all of it, I realized there had to be a better way. So I started building Unlevered on the weekends and late into the night, and unintentionally began my entrepreneurial journey with a few folks behind the scenes to support.

In May, right after I left to pursue this full time, Riley Newman and Sara Adler at Wave Capital saw something in me and in the opportunity, and they invested.

So it's official: we've closed a seed round with Wave Capital and launched Unlevered, to help CPAs, RIAs, and Wealth Advisors unlock asset depreciation as a tax lever for high-income earners.

And we're off 🏇... Unlevered is officially live. If you are interested or curious if this makes sense for you, I would welcome introductions to your CPAs, RIAs, and Wealth Advisors to equip them with the platform to make it easier to implement this lever for you and their clients.… more

Cofounder and CEO @ Rejigg

8mo • 

The story of Rejigg doesn’t start with Silicon Valley — it starts with my mom behind the counter of a Baskin Robbins.

Born in post-war Busan as the youngest of five kids, my Mom grew up intoxicated by the opportunity provided by the American dream, accelerated by a growing love of John Denver and all things Americana.

In her 20s, she followed that yearning and immigrated to the US, first landing in the cornfields of northern Iowa. While there, she met my dad and they later settled down in San Jose.

After they had my brother and I, my mom struggled to find employment. Minimal prior work experience and an art degree made it tough.

But she had grit and intelligence and struck out on her own: she piled our family’s life savings into opening an ice cream shop in Santa Clara. You can see her on opening day in the pic below!

Over the next many decades, my mom lived the American Dream - her sweat and tears turned that first shop into an empire of a dozen successful quick service restaurants all across the South Bay.

A few years ago, as she entered her mid-sixties she was ready to retire - but found that this was much more difficult than she expected.

Brokers offered to take her out to free lunches where they told her “We can sell your business for only 10% of its value (hundreds of thousands of dollars). And if you don’t do it with one of us, you won’t be able to do it at all.”

She refused to believe that.

My mom ran the process herself and sold location-by-location to people she knew in the community, mostly other women from our Korean church. 

That’s the process we’re trying to systematize at Rejigg. And we’re trying to make your reach broader than just those in your immediate circles. We want to give owners the tools to run their own process of selling their small business.

If you’ve built a business worth buying, you already have the skills to sell it. You just need to meet the right buyer.

Rejigg was inspired by my mom: a 4’11” force who built the American dream from scratch. Now, we’re building the platform to help every owner do the same. We’ve already helped 25 owners find their exit using our technology and we’re just getting started.
