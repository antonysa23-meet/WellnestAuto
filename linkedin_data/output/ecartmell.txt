About
Emma is a healthcare industry operator, successful investor and sought after Board Member. Most recently, Emma was Managing Partner at Cartmell Ventures where she made 14 digital health investments. Additionally, Emma is an advisor to Morgan Stanley's Expansion Capital Fund and United Health Groups accelerator program. Previously, she served on the board of HIMSS North America and advised the British Government on digital health. Emma was also the founding COO for NantHealth where she oversaw over 30 digital health investments, worked as COO for Abraxis BioScience which was sold to Celgene for over $3B dollars, and served as SAIC's Vice President of business development for the Health Solutions Business Unit and VP of Healthcare Advance Programs.

With over two decades of proven leadership and investment success, Emma values leveraging technology to fix broken workflows in healthcare delivery to create an effortless patient experience, improve health outcomes and lower costs for all.

Healthcare doesn’t have a resource problem. It has a transition problem.

Most outcomes, clinical or social, are determined in the space between organizations that aren’t truly connected.

That’s what USA Health and Feeding the Gulf Coast set out to fix. With Watershed Health, they built a closed-loop model that makes transitions actionable and countable:

- Risk identified in the hospital
- Referrals flow across the network
- Community partners engage
- Outcomes are visible and measurable

This started with food insecurity, but it’s not just about food. It’s about an infrastructure for coordinated action across an ecosystem.

When transitions are connected, interventions work. When they’re not, even the best programs fail.

Because every transition is the same problem, and now it has a scalable solution. Be part of building your connected community - providers join free. www.watershedhealth.com

Today, we’re proud to announce $7M in seed funding to expand access to pediatric developmental care, led by Nina Capital.

One in four children under age 6 are at risk for a developmental delay or disability, but families routinely wait six to nine months or longer for therapy during the most important developmental window of a child's life. Ladder Health helps health systems and pediatric providers close that gap, reducing time-to-care from months to days through a caregiver-activated, virtual-first model that extends expert developmental care into the home, available evenings and weekends. 

Built in collaboration with Boston Children's Hospital, we now partner with more than 80 provider organizations and health systems across the country, helping expand capacity, reduce waitlists, and reach more families without requiring additional physical infrastructure or clinical headcount.

We're especially proud of our work serving Medicaid, rural, and historically underserved communities, where access barriers are often greatest, and the need is most urgent. 

Thank you to Nina Capital for leading and 25m Health for incubating us from day one, along with Mairs & Power Venture Capital, South Dakota First Capital, Hatteras Venture Partners, Create Health Ventures, Jumpstart Capital, White Oak Enterprises, Groove Capital, and 7Rock Ventures.

After three straight weeks of in-person board meetings, I’ve been thinking about the difference between simply showing up and working together to shape strategy and tackle difficult growth issues.

“The best two hours together weren’t even on the board agenda.”

One theme that kept repeating is that the most valuable conversations often aren’t the formal ones. They happen:
- after the meeting
- over dinner
- during coffee
- at the whiteboard

“CEO’s rarely have the harder conversations in the boardroom.”

Financing options, changes to positioning, hiring/staff changes, or simply what to prioritize - those discussions often happen later, when trust has been built and people can speak more candidly.

“Pattern recognition and broad ecosystem perspective are part of the job.”

The best board members do more than provide oversight. They help founders think strategically, navigate difficult decisions, build alignment, and prepare for what’s ahead.

And when you sit across multiple boards, you also start to see patterns emerge across markets, buyers, fundraising environments, and healthcare systems long before they become obvious publicly.

"Healthcare is hard. Building companies in this environment is even harder."

Which is why I continue to believe that immersion, relationships, trust, and strategic partnerships matter... and nothing replaces the value of a good, experienced board.

Thanks for all the tough and brilliant conversations (in and out the board room)! You are all doing an incredible job in this fast-changing world.
