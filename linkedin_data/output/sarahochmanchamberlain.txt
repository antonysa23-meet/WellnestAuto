About
Sara has more than 20 years of focused experience in the energy and environment sector.  She co-founded an early-stage cleantech venture capital firm that pioneered a new model for investing in startups with transformative technology.  She led investments in several high profile companies including e-Zinc, Tank Utility (acq by GNRC), NanoGraf, Azumo, Polystyvert, Volexion, and others.  Prior to Earth Foundry, Sara built a government advisory practice at Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati and helped energy startups raise $300M in non-dilutive funding.  She has also held roles with GE Energy and environmental consulting firm, GaiaTech.  Sara holds an MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business, and an undergraduate degree from Emory University. She is on the Advisory Board for Chain Reaction Innovations, an incubator program at Argonne National Laboratory, and is a member of the Investment Committee for accelerator Clean Energy Trust.  In 2023, she was recognized by Fortune as a "Top 13 Seed Stage Climate Tech VC to Watch"

Well done to Luke Winston-Almanzar, Gabriel Parisi-Amon, and Jake Fesler, founders of Reservoir, and the many others working on similar approaches, such as Cala Systems.

At Harvest, we completely agree that water heaters are an underutilized grid lever - Reservoir Homes' approach is a real step forward in comfort and controllability.

We'd like to add one nuance: water heating is meaningful, but it’s not the biggest residential load. Nationally, space heating accounts for ~42% of household energy, and water heating for ~18% (so ~61% combined). In cold/very cold climates, space + water can be ~70%+. That’s why Harvest, we believe the next leap is co-optimizing heating and hot water, not just making a single appliance “smart.”

That’s exactly what Harvest is built for: an integrated heat-pump + thermal storage + controls layer that shifts up to ~90% of BOTH heating and DHW off-peak while preserving comfort and turning homes into a dispatchable fleet asset, not just a collection of smart devices. With Harvest's fleet of ~530 systems deployed and operating, this represents >5 MW of flexible capacity in Northern California alone—a platform built with automated DR dispatch, TOU/price/emissions-responsive optimization (CTA-2045/EcoPort compatible) and program-ready alignment (incl. CEC/DOE work on grid-flexible buildings). 
But most importantly, our customers average 20-40% utility bill savings, which impact households where it matters most: their budget! 

And this is where it becomes relevant to “speed-to-power” for data centers: Harvest can deliver targeted, feeder-level peak reduction and, more importantly, reshape the load, so utilities and aggregators can count on it 24/7, 365 days per year, helping defer upgrades faster than waiting for new wires and generation. 

Lastly, Harvest believes that addressing "first-cost" sticker shock for consumers upgrading their systems is an important driver of adoption. Offering a thermal storage financing-first model removes 100% of upfront costs for homeowners by bundling equipment and installation into a simple monthly payment, enabling adoption to scale at replacement events without first-cost friction.

Models like Harvest are addressing the unspoken burden of affordability and the use of domestic appliances already in the home as viable grid assets.

PS. A sincere thanks to Jigar Shah for continuing to help drive these early innovations across our ecosystem!
