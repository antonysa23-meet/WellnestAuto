About
I specialize in identifying and investing in high-growth consumer software companies, particularly in consumer AI applications, consumer digital health, personalized medicine, and companies positioned for hypergrowth due to an emerging consumer trend. With over a decade in venture capital, I have invested in companies like Carrot Fertility, Daybreak Health, Hello Heart, Embark Trucks, Medeloop, and Wildtype.

In addition to my work at Maven Ventures, I am a Lecturer in Management at the Stanford Graduate School of Business. For the MBA elective course Startup Garage, I help Stanford students turn their early ideas into real businesses. 

I love working with founders who are bold and incredibly ambitious to build an iconic company, especially those driven by their own purpose who strive to make a positive impact on the world. 

I have been supporting and cheering on founders for over 15 years and am so grateful to be part of their journeys. I'm always on the lookout for the next big consumer trend that can unlock hypergrowth and build an iconic consumer company!

Founders are heading into Q1 board meetings, and many of them are about to walk into rooms that could work so much harder for them.

I'm sharing the B-O-A-R-D boardroom checklist to help investors prepare for productive and effective board meetings and to guide founders in getting the most out of their cap table. 

B: Build the relationship before the meeting
O: Outline expectations. What makes a successful board meeting?
A: Advance preparation: preview materials, send questions ahead, help founders get what they need. 
R: Reporting consistency: same structure, every time, with honest hits and misses.
D: Deliver feedback. Debrief after the meeting. 

Successful board meetings that deliver what the founder needs to run the company require intention from both sides of the table.

If you're a founder who wants this level of involvement from your investors, ask for their support and active engagement to help you succeed. … more

OpenAI, Google, Anthropic and Perplexity are eating the surface area of consumer AI.

Ideas that looked like good startup opportunities are getting absorbed into the existing players faster than ever before. It's real, and founders building in consumer AI can't ignore it. 

And yet, being close to the ground in consumer also yields new, novel, and exciting insights just as fast. I still deeply believe there will be massive successful companies started in the space. We're still so early.

Here are some areas that I think are interesting right now:

🤓 AI-enabled services. Offline, physical consumer problems that aren't going to be solved on a computer alone. AI doesn't replace the human experience, it makes it better, faster, cheaper, or more accessible.

🔩 Infrastructure for consumer AI agents. What will the internet look like when consumers have AI agents acting on their behalf? I'm especially interested in experienced teams with a genuinely novel insight about the category... it could be B2C, B2B2C, or infrastructure for consumer agents. 

❤️ Consumer AI meets health. We've already made some investments here and I am sure we will do more. How can the power of and access to AI technology extend human lifespan or save lives? I'm exploring everything from longevity to  synthetic biology.

💎 Luxury for the 99%. AI making things that were previously exclusive radically accessible.

Here's your friendly reminder that if you're building something slightly crazy (and I mean that as a compliment) that will change life for millions of consumers, I'm excited to learn more!… more

It’s Demo Day season!

Big swings. Fresh ideas. Founders building things that feel slightly bananas and just might be massive.

We’ve already made two new investments to start 2026 (both at the intersection of AI + health), and I'm actively looking for more. The top recurring theme for our investments this year is being bold: ideas that can be truly huge and majorly impactful for consumers if they succeed, and backing founders just crazy enough to try.

A few areas of excitement right now:
🏋️ Massive ideas with hypergrowth potential: the kind that, if they work, reshape daily life for millions (or billions)
💵 Consumer AI products that are novel and provide real, instant value
🩺 AI-driven health & longevity that integrates seamlessly into everyday life
📺 AI-powered entertainment and social platforms unlocking new forms of creativity and connection
🛥️ “Luxury for the 99%” AI making things that were previously exclusive radically accessible
💃 AI that’s simply fun: products that bring joy, delight, and surprise

If you’re a technical founder building for consumers, especially if people think your idea is a little crazy...we should talk!

I'd love to learn about what you’re building. 🚀
