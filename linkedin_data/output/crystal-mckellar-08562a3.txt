View company: Defense Innovation Unit (DIU)
Defense Innovation Unit (DIU)

 

5mo • Edited • 

“Shadow that vessel at twenty kilometers and avoid radar exposure.” 

Commanders are used to giving orders such as this – and having their teams understand the intent and execute appropriately. As autonomous vehicles are integrated into the Joint Force, humans need to be able to work the way they already command: expressing desired effects, constraints, timing, and priorities, not clicking through menus or programming behaviors.

The United States Department of War needs an operationally viable Autonomous Vehicle Orchestrator – a layer of technology that can translate a battlefield commander’s intent from natural language into machine execution. 

To solve for this critical challenge, the Defense Innovation Unit (DIU), Defense Autonomous Warfare Group (DAWG), and the US Navy have launched a $100,000,000 Prize Challenge to prototype market-ready solutions to establish a robust, scalable and vehicle-agnostic capability for understanding, tasking and coordinating autonomous systems at the fleet level. 

“This solicitation’s approach is the new standard—we’re moving fast to deliver tangible capabilities to the warfighter. Selected performers will be shoulder‑to‑shoulder with operators, and they will be proving that their capability works in an operational environment,” said Hon. Emil Michael, Acting Director of DIU and Under Secretary of War for Research and Engineering. “If a company can deliver, they will receive substantial rewards. If they can’t, we will move on. The ones who show they can perform will move immediately into follow‑on contracts so we can field these capabilities at scale for our fighting force.”

Submissions are open through Jan 25.

Small Modular Reactors: Redefining the Future of Decentralized Energy in the U.S. 🌱⚡

As the U.S. moves toward a more sustainable and resilient energy future, Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) are emerging as key players in decentralizing the power grid and increasing energy independence. With their adaptability, scalability, and rapid deployment capabilities, SMRs offer a promising solution for meeting the growing demand for resilient energy infrastructure, enhancing energy security, and reducing reliance on external sources.

In my latest paper, I explore how SMRs and Micro Modular Reactors (MMRs) provide a more flexible, cost-effective, and environmentally responsible alternative to traditional nuclear reactors. The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) is playing a critical role in accelerating SMR development, with industry leaders such as Aalo Atomics, ARC Clean Technology, General Atomics, GE Hitachi Nuclear Energy, Holtec International, Kairos Power, NuScale Power, Oklo Inc, Radiant, Terrestrial Energy, Westinghouse Electric Company, and X-energy driving innovation in this transformative technology.

SMRs are also well-suited to meet emerging energy needs for AI, hydrogen production, desalination, and rare earth material extraction, unlocking numerous opportunities to address global challenges in energy and environmental sustainability.
