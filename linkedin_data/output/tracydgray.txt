My statement as President of 
Los Angeles Commision on the Status of Women (Picture with Dolores from around 2022):

Today as we observe the last day of Women’s History Month and formerly César Chávez Day (now Farmworkers Day in Los Angeles), I think of Dolores Huerta. As President of the City of Los Angeles Commission on the Status of Women, I am committed to advancing a Los Angeles where women’s safety and equality are non-negotiable. No movement or legacy should come at the expense of women, and no leader is beyond accountability.

The recent revelations regarding Chávez are deeply troubling and stand in stark contrast to the values we in public service strive to uphold. We must confront this history with honesty and clarity. As someone with sexual assault victims within my family, “framily” and friends, I stand in unwavering solidarity with survivors and with those who have carried these experiences in silence: your voices matter and your courage is seen.

Like the civil rights movement before it, the story of the United Farm Workers has never belonged to one individual and women were vital to the success. It was built and sustained by women: organizers, farmworkers, and leaders whose contributions have too often been overlooked. I honor the leadership and courage of Dolores Huerta, Ana Murguía, Debra Rojas, and all those who continue to speak truth to power.

The legacy of the farmworker movement belongs to these women. They define its strength, its integrity, and its future. They are the true embodiment of “Sí se puede.”

Tracy D. Gray
President 
Los Angeles City Commision on the Status of Women

In honour of International Women's Day, I'm delighted to announce a strategic decision.

She Shapes AI is rebranding. Effective immediately, we are now He Shapes AI.

💪 Our new mission: supporting male entrepreneurs - particularly those from Silicon Valley who ship AI products as fast as possible, with as little attention to solving real-world problems as possible.

Our April conference and awards ceremony will no longer focus on 'AI Done Well'. It will celebrate 'AI Done Fast'. 

We are also changing the conference agenda accordingly and will have all-male panels with the occasional female moderator to brighten up the podium.

(Regarding our current finalists: they are, regrettably, still women. We're working on a workaround.)

From the next awards cycle onwards, we will prioritise applicants who are

➡️ Under 30, 
➡️ Have never left the US, 
➡️ Develop tech 'because we can' (and because it's gonna make them a lot of money), and 
➡️ Demonstrate a healthy disregard for 'responsible' and 'systemic approaches'. 

Relevance of their AI solutions to actual human needs will be disqualifying.

If this resonates and you'd like to partner with He Shapes AI, move quickly. I expect companies to be queuing up.
___________________________________________________________________

If any of this sounds like the opposite of everything we stand for, that's because it is.

The reality: She Shapes AI has built a global community across 60+ countries in less than two years. We spotlight and celebrate women driving responsible AI innovation. Our conference and awards ceremony is five weeks away. And we still don't have a single sponsor. Not one.

Our work resonates broadly - with women and men alike. The need is real. The success is proven.

So: if you or your organisation believe that AI should actually serve humanity, and that the women building that future deserve a stage - I'd love to hear from you. Our annual conference takes place on 16 April in London. And our next Awards cycle will kick off in October.
