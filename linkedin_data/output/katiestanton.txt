To my Colorado community: Weiser for Governor; Bennet for Senate.

Because I care deeply about Colorado’s future, I want to share why I’m voting for Phil Weiser in the gubernatorial primary and why I encourage you to do the same. 

My work centers on innovation and entrepreneurship, and it’s important that we make Colorado the most innovative state in the US.This will be difficult in the face of slowing growth, unnecessary state regulations, and global uncertainty. 

A governor is essentially the CEO of our state, responsible for developing a clear growth strategy, keeping Colorado attractive to existing and new high growth businesses, while overseeing an infrastructure to meet the needs of working families for childcare, healthcare, and housing.  Phil is the right man for the job.

He certainly has policy expertise—not just from serving two terms as Colorado’s attorney general and as the dean of the CU Law School, but from working with the US Supreme Court, the Department of Justice, and the White House, where he was a senior advisor for technology and innovation at the National Economic Council.

He’s worked for decades to advance entrepreneurship in our state—and made great strides. In 1999, he founded the Silicon Flatirons Center for Law, Technology, and Entrepreneurship. Through its conference series, Entrepreneurial Law Clinic, and competitions, it brought value to CU and entrepreneurs statewide.

In 2011, Phil and Brad Feld launched Startup Colorado, which focuses on serving rural entrepreneurs. In 2014, they started Bridge Entrepreneurs Network (BEN Colorado), which fosters valuable connections among Colorado’s entrepreneurs within an ecosystem that includes advisors, industry experts, and investors.

I respect Phil for his engagement in causes that matter. He supports Access Mode, an accelerator for entrepreneurs who are underrepresented in terms of access to capital and mentorship, Energize Colorado, a CDFI  supporting the small business ecosystem with low cost loans across the state, and African Leadership Group. He cares about all Coloradans and finds ways to support the underserved.

Over the last year, I learned about his priorities and sensed his passion and sincerity. He wants to tackle our state's problems with TABOR, engage young people in civic opportunities, create more affordable housing, and ensure our state leans into its strengths in tourism, creative industries, and advanced technologies to support quantum, space, AI, and nextgen energy to deliver economic vitality through new high-wage jobs. 

I am fiscally conservative and socially responsible. I prioritize limited government spending, balanced budgets, as well as a social safety net and environmental stewardship. I know the future I want for Colorado, and I strongly believe Phil Weiser is the candidate who can get us there.

The datacenter boom has intensified debates around energy shortages, grid strain, and rising utility prices. Communities across the country are pushing back, citing reasonable concerns about electricity costs, water usage, noise and pressure on local infrastructure. Expanding grid capacity to power AI while keeping electricity affordable, reliable and clean are deeply interconnected challenges. AI is driving energy demand faster than our infrastructure was ever built to support.

However, major technology shifts have historically forced infrastructure innovation, and AI is poised to do the same for energy. Demand from datacenters, electrification, and modern compute systems is creating new urgency around grid modernization, energy storage, resilience and distributed power systems.

At Moxxie Ventures, we are backing founders who are building this new energy economy. HomeBoost helps homeowners finance and manage energy upgrades. Lumen Energy helps commercial building owners transform rooftops into revenue-generating solar infrastructure. Overstory uses AI and satellite imagery to help utilities identify vegetation risk and improve grid resilience before outages and wildfires happen. SpringCycle Energy is transforming solar into baseload power at the lowest cost in the industry for datacenters, EV charging, advanced manufacturing, and utilities.

The future of AI infrastructure is not just chips and datacenters. It crucially includes rooftop solar, grid resilience, virtual power plants and smarter energy systems where homes and businesses are active participants in how energy is generated, stored and managed.

While there is understandable concern about AI’s power demands, this challenge is spurring the opportunity to build a far more resilient, clean and modern energy system. We believe some of the most important companies of the next decade will emerge at the intersection of AI, infrastructure and energy. We are proud to have closed our Series A at Dandelion, and to have added investors who will genuinely help us take the next step in what we're building - a clinical AI powered drug development platform. Raising money matters, but only in the context of the problem you're trying to solve — and I'm particularly glad to welcome Healthier Capital and Colle Capital to that work. Both bring something rarer than capital: a deep understanding of the healthcare ecosystem and the scientific seriousness to help move it forward. That combination is harder to find than it should be, and we don't take it lightly. We are equally grateful to our existing partners Primary Venture Partners, Moxxie Ventures, and Convergent Ventures, whose continued conviction in what we're building means a great deal to the whole team.

There's been a lot of justified enthusiasm about what AI is doing to healthcare - the administrative friction it's reducing, and those contributions are real. But the clinical science of drug development has remained largely untouched by this moment, and that gap is worth further contemplation and action. The trials that determine which drugs reach patients, at what doses, in which populations, are still being designed around disease trajectories and patient characteristics that were defined well before the therapies now reshaping medicine began doing so. In almost all indications, the underlying biology of who progresses, at what pace, and what that progression looks like in longitudinal data has been changing - and the infrastructure for trial design has not moved with it in any meaningful way. This means we have long, bloated and expensive trials.

That's the kind of problem that doesn't present itself in high profile issues that need immediate solutions. It accumulates quietly until it becomes very expensive to ignore - that point has already been reached though few have the data to see it as of now.

What we've been building at Dandelion is the solution to this issue. 

A platform that informs what's actually happening in patients before they ever reach a diagnosis - to understand the pre-disease window well enough that the trials designed to intervene in it are built against the world as it is, not as it was characterized a decade ago. We are now exceptionally well positioned to continue that work - using high fidelity clinical data and AI to develop treatments more precisely, for patients more accurately diagnosed. I'm grateful to the partners who understood why it needed doing, and the team that has made it possible.
