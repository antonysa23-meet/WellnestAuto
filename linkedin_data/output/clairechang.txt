About
Claire is Founder & General Partner of igniteXL Ventures, a Silicon Valley based global pre-seed and seed fund dedicated to fueling innovation at the intersection of health, technology, and consumer experiences.  igniteXL Ventures’ mission is to catalyze the future of health and wellness on a global scale by identifying and empowering exceptional founders who drive market disruptions to positively impact how we look, feel, live, and age.  igniteXL Ventures Fund 1 has invested in 30 companies, including Bubble, Buywith, Dame, Ettitude, HelloBiome, Lion Pose, Musely, and Oros, among others.  

Born in South Korea and fluent in Korean, Claire has spent the past 20 years in Silicon Valley ecosystem as a founder, investor, and advisor, nurturing her love of technology marketing and fostering global connections. The guiding threads throughout Claire’s professional journey are her superpower for connecting people, her addiction to entrepreneurship and the founder-journey, and her unique multi-cultural and global view of the world—which is critically relevant in an increasingly global society. 

In addition to her work with igniteXL Ventures, Claire is involved in several leading professional industry organizations across technology, health, and wellness. Claire currently serves on the National Retail Federation’s (NRF) Innovation Advisory Committee, the Howe Innovation Center for Disability Tech, an advisory group of the Perkins School for the Blind, and is a participating member of the AARP AgeTech Collaborative. Claire also serves as a Board Member of Buywith, a leading livestream platform and as a Board Observer of Ettitude, a fabric innovation company. 

Prior to founding igniteXL Ventures, Claire founded and operated igniteXL, a Silicon Valley-based global accelerator focused on early-stage startups from Korea to explore and expand into United States market. Over 250 Korean founders graduated from the accelerator’s Silicon Valley immersion and market access programs. Some of the notable companies include:  Flitto, KnowRe, Smart Study, Neostream, Dabeeo, Stratio, and more.  

Whether she’s in the boardroom, on the golf course, having dinner, or on the slopes, Claire loves meeting people and learning about their unique journey—and ultimately connecting the dots to make the network more meaningful, resourceful, and valuable.

We all know this, but it’s fun (and optimistic!) to acknowledge that the foundational AI models we’re all playing with today are just the beginning of the technology wave we’re about to ride. And yet, as Pam Kostka points out, only 9% of funding in 2026 has gone to emerging firms due to historically high concentrations in established/mature AI companies. 

That seems like an opportunity for investors to take an asymmetric approach and bet on what we know will be the coming tailwind of innovation. It was a key motivation for me to attend a fantastic breakfast this morning hosted by Capital F and igniteXL Ventures. Getting more involved in the SF venture ecosystem, it’s exciting to see how vertical experts are integrating AI to dissect, unbundle, and repackage value for consumers across advertising, healthcare, fintech, e-comm and beyond.

As Benedict Evans prompts regarding innovation cycles, ”Can you invent new questions?" In the music industry decades ago, the question shifted from "What if I don’t have to buy a CD?" to "What if I can get all the music there is?" Then we all lived the evolution from CDs to Napster to iPods to Spotify...and now streaming accounts for >75% of global music revenue.

We’re at that exact paradigm shift with AI today. There are incredible models yet to be built that will have profound effects on all of us for decades to come. 👏 🧪

Thank you to Dawn Dobras, Claire Chang, and Marie Oh Huber for bringing together such an incredible room of investors and operators, and to Margaret Coblentz for the intro, (we missed you!) Loved catching up with Sabrina Chueh Ellis from our Pinterest days as well. ❤️

🧩 That gap shouldn't exist. And closing it is exactly why we keep building this community.

There's nothing more powerful than hearing directly from a founder who's been there and done it.

We were so fortunate to have Randi Christiansen, co-founder of Nécessaire, Inc., share her journey with our #K2G cohort — how she went against the market, ignored the skeptics, and created the first luxury body care brand when no one thought the category deserved a premium price point.

Her core message? Customer obsession.

I talk about this often with founders as 💖 "falling in love with your customer" — knowing them so deeply, so specifically, that every decision you make is in service of delighting them.

But Randi took it further. And I got goosebumps.

👩‍❤️‍💋‍👩 Spend 50% of your time learning the customer. The other 50% figuring out how to delight them. 
🛍️ Get obsessed with 80+ customer insights — what they buy, where they shop, what frustrates them, what they splurge on, what they settle for. 
🧪 Test everything digitally before committing to physical retail. The data tells you where your customer actually is.

Thank you, Randi. Our founders won't forget this one. 🙏

One session clearly wasn’t enough — we were already talking about a follow-up before this one ended ✨

Very grateful to have Randi Christiansen join #K2G virtually and share the thinking behind building Nécessaire, Inc. — from identifying white space early to knowing when credibility matters more than scale.

Thank you Randi for being so candid — especially on mistakes, discipline, and what it really takes to build with conviction 🤍

K2G Session #21:  The Funding Formula: What Investors Really Want

What happens when top investors fly to Seoul and spend an afternoon with emerging K-Beauty founders? Real talk.

We were grateful to host Deborah Benton and Josh Dau from Willow Growth Partners during their first trip to Seoul - and even more grateful for how openly they shared their perspective with our K-Beauty to Global founders.

And perhaps the most important cultural shift for K-Beauty founders entering the U.S.: 
💥 Learn to talk about yourself. American fundraising rewards founders who can clearly articulate why they are exceptional, why they will win, and why no one else can build this the way they can. For founders raised in a culture that values humility and conformity, this is a real muscle to build — but it's non-negotiable if you want to stand out in a crowded U.S. market.

The best investor conversations aren't pitches - they're honest exchanges. That's exactly what our founders got, and we don't take that for granted.

Thank you, Deborah and Josh. 🙏

A very warm thank you to Deborah Benton and Josh Dau from Willow Growth Partners for taking time during their first trip to Seoul to spend the afternoon with our K-Beauty to Global founders!!

A few reminders:
• Growth alone is not enough — founders need to know exactly how their margins work, including packaging, shipping, and what repeat purchase behavior looks like underneath revenue.

• Repeat matters early: for non-subscription brands, investors want to see signs that customers are coming back because that says far more than one strong acquisition month.

A heartfelt thank you to Sarah Choi from Cooley LLP for spending part of the day with our #K2G founders in Seoul and making legal topics feel far more approachable than they often do 🙏🏻

The session was especially valuable because it focused on decisions founders often delay until they suddenly become urgent — fundraising structures, entity setup, and the legal terms that can quietly shape a company for years.

A few practical reminders that really stayed with the cohort:
• Most US investors still require a US entity, but flipping too early can create unnecessary cost before a lead investor is actually in place
 • Once a term sheet is signed, most major economic and control terms are already set — which is why founders need to understand them before legal drafting begins
 • Terms like liquidation preference, board control, and investor consent rights may seem technical early on, but they often have the biggest long-term impact on founder control

Would knowing that have made me a better partner? A better investor?

Thank you, Taeil. For the pizza, for the memory, and for the reminder of what it really means to show up for founders. 🙏
