Matt Curtolo, CAIA

  • 2nd

LP & GP Advisor, Bringing 20+ years in the trenches to the next generation of managers and investors.

2w • 

Had a bunch of conversations with newer LPs over the last month or so. Maybe I shouldn't be, but I'm a little surprised at how many are looking at historical performance as the whole story, rather than the preamble. As I've preached for many years, past performance is a lagging indicator at best and a red herring at worst. 

If you are a newer LP, ALWAYS treat the performance as the outcome, which can contain plenty of randomness in it. Spend the bulk of your time understanding what aspects of generating that performance (good or bad) are repeatable, durable and systematic, and what lessons have been learned that will make them better investors in the future. 

We can't always know the exact split between luck and skill, but going through the exercise to go beyond the headlines will serve you well in the long run.

Our solution is built on the concept that a centralized food system is a volatile one, and a decentralized food system is a resilient one.

For too long, the response has been emergency relief to meet a permanent need. Locally controlled food infrastructure helps communities move from dependence to self-reliance. The current system needs a long-term vision, and we see investing in local food production as the path to Canada’s autonomy, security, and long-term prosperity.

This award is a signal. That the work being done to build more equitable, resilient food systems is being seen.

That's what we actually want: more innovators choosing hard problems over easy ones. More builders working alongside communities instead of around them. More investors and funders betting on food access as infrastructure, not charity.

The communities are ready. What's needed now is the will, and the backing, to meet them where they are.

To every builder working on something that puts access, equity, and quality of life at the centre: this one's for you
