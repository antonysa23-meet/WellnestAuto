About
Alexa von Tobel, CFP®, is the Founder and Managing Partner of Inspired Capital, a $900M Pre-Seed to Series A venture fund backing fearless founders solving the hardest challenges facing humanity. In addition, Alexa is the New York Times-Bestselling Author of Financially Fearless and Financially Forward, and she hosts Inc.'s weekly entrepreneurship podcast, The Founders Project. 

Prior to Inspired Capital, Alexa founded LearnVest in 2008 with the goal of helping people make progress on their money. After raising nearly $75 million in venture capital, LearnVest was acquired by Northwestern Mutual in May 2015 in one of the biggest fintech acquisitions of the decade. Following the acquisition, von Tobel joined the management team of Northwestern Mutual as the company’s first-ever Chief Digital Officer, overseeing digital strategy. She later assumed the role of Chief Innovation Officer through which she oversaw Northwestern Mutual’s venture arm.

Alexa has appeared on the cover of Forbes Magazine, been interviewed on NPR’s How I Built This and been featured in a wide variety of publications from The New York Times to Vogue. She is a member of the 2016 Class of Henry Crown Fellows and an inaugural member of President Obama’s Ambassadors for Global Entrepreneurship. Alexa has been honored with numerous recognitions including: Fortune’s 40 Under 40, Fortune's Most Powerful Women, Forbes’ 30 Under 30, Inc. Magazine's 30 Under 30 and World Economic Forum's Young Global Leader.

Originally from Florida, Alexa attended Harvard College and Harvard Business School before settling in New York City where she currently resides with her husband and their three children.

70% of small businesses listed for sale in the U.S. never find a buyer. When that happens, businesses that have served communities for decades simply close. Teamshares was built to change that equation permanently.

We are proud to share that Teamshares is going public, beginning to trade on the Nasdaq under TMS via a business combination with Live Oak Acquisition Corporation, and we could not be more proud to have backed this exceptional team since 2020.

When we first met co-founders Michael Sutherland Brown, Alex Eu, and Kevin Rikio Shiiba, they were a five-person team with one acquisition closed and a 200-year plan. What set them apart was not just the vision. It was that they had already gone and lived inside the problem they were solving, buying and running eight small businesses themselves before ever pitching a single investor.

That foundation has driven everything since. Teamshares has now acquired 92 companies across 30+ states and 40+ industries, generating ~$500 million in consolidated revenue and $60 million in operating EBITDA. Employee owners across the network receive real equity in the businesses they help build.

What makes today more than just a milestone is what going public unlocks for the business. Teamshares runs on acquisition capital, and access to the public markets means faster, cheaper financing and the ability to use equity directly. With 475,000 businesses having come through their sourcing software since 2020 and 6 million small businesses changing hands by 2035, the runway ahead is as large as it has ever been.

As Michael said earlier this year on our Inspired podcast, Teamshares is still in the first inning.

We could not be prouder to have been alongside this team since the beginning. Congratulations Teamshares!

We recently hosted our AGM at Inspired Capital, and it was a great moment to reflect on how far the firm has come and how we continue to raise the bar.

Seven years ago, we set out to build one of the most trusted, loyal, and founder-passionate firms in venture. Not the loudest. Not the most quoted. But instead, the one that the best founders call first and rely on most.

We built Inspired for people who are built differently. The top 0.01% who don't just start companies, but pursue them as life missions. Founders who see the future a certain way and feel a profound responsibility to shepherd the world into its future state. Those who are obsessive about progress, who feel the gravitational pull of building, and for whom there is no alternative career path. Stopping is simply impossible.

That conviction has never wavered. What's grown is a portfolio of 75+ companies, including 9 unicorns and our first expected public company, Teamshares, recently filed an updated S-4. Traction across the portfolio is extremely strong -  4 companies above $300M of revenue and 5 more that have crossed $100M. 40% of our companies have crossed $10M in revenue, and 25% have crossed $30M. A portfolio built not around themes or trend cycles, but around extraordinary people solving problems that matter, with both excellence and integrity.

Through all of it, we've operated a certain way: patient when the market rewards speed, deep when some stay surface-level. Present for the 11 pm call, the impossible hire, the moment where conviction matters more than consensus. We've always believed in the intersection of west coast ambition and east coast financial discipline. Dream at scale, but build real businesses. That balance has shaped how we invest, how we partner, and what we expect from ourselves at all times.

Sitting in the Inspired office, watching our founders present what they're building, it was a really special moment. We are incredibly grateful to the founders who shared their work with our community: Alex Hawkinson (BrightAI), Jeremy Gurewitz (Solace), Harry Rein (ShopMy), Michael Sutherland Brown (Teamshares), Steven Simoni (Allen Control Systems), Dan Preston and Matthew DiStefano (Stand), Jeff Thompson (Logiqal, Inc.), Michael Witte (Equal Parts), Brian Distelburger (Windmill), and Rachel Lea Fishman (Arketa).

If you're building something the world doesn't know it needs yet, we want to hear from you.

To our next decade. This is Inspired. … more
