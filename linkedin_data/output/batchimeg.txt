9 books that influenced how I think about impact, systems, and doing meaningful work 📚

In no particular order ...

1. Built on Purpose – Betsy Fore
"Each time you choose what aligns with your deeper purpose, you rewire your brain to expect success and act accordingly."

2. Speed & Scale – John Doerr
"Perhaps that most underestimated force for averting climate disaster is the protection of Indigenous rights and way of life. Indigenous wisdom and practices, honed over centuries, will be indispensible to mankind's effort to mitigate a warming planet-and to adapt to it as well."

3. Fifty Miles from Tomorrow - William Iggiagruk Hensley, Founder of NANA
"Know your environment. Know your partners. Plan ahead. Use only as much manpower and resources as necessary. Make sure your efforts benefit the whole community."

4. The Little Book of Impact Investing – Priya Parrish, Impact Engine
"The rise of impact investing is about systems change, or how we can harness the massive power of the financial industry toward solving our most pressing problems."

5. Troubled Water – Seth M. Siegel
"It is possible to imagine a day when everyone in America who is thirsty can fill a glass of water from a sink and have no reason to wonder about the quality of the water or to worry about what drinking it might do to their long-term health."

6. Give First – Brad Feld
“Give First means you're willing to put energy into a system without knowing what you're going to get back. It's not altruism. You will get something back, but you don't have any expectations about what it will be.”

7. Arrive and Thrive – Susan MacKenty Brady, Janet Foutty, Lynn Perry Wooten
"Your authenticity is your competitive advantage. Nobody can be you the way you can be you."

8. Scaling Innovation – Madhavan Ramanujam & Eddie Hartman
"Achieving real, sustained growth requires more than a great a product. It takes deep understanding of how to connect with your customers, deliver value, and build a scalable foundation for long-term success."

9. The Business of Venture Capital – Mahendra R.
"We are here for empowering the crazy ones with our gifts of time, money, intellectual rights, and our wonderful relationships. All of this to serve the greater good of society."

Last November, we convened innovators developing solutions for a resilient critical mineral supply chain with leaders from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) and the United States Department of War. Since then, it has been fascinating to see the level of activity across both agencies.

⚡ U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) is providing $500 million in funding to expand domestic minerals processing, battery materials manufacturing, and recycling. Notice of intent due March 27, 2026. https://lnkd.in/gez7YX3g

🔗 United States Department of War is targeting all nodes across the critical minerals value chain as part of its $100B+ investment in critical minerals. Through the DIBC | Defense Industrial Base Consortium, mining to technology companies can apply for the initial request for project proposals by March 20, 2026: https://lnkd.in/gebadUZz

If you're working on something completely new, don't forget to check out programs across Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) and Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy (ARPA-E). DARPA, for example, has a few relevant programs, including: 

SMASH - seeking separation technologies capable of simultaneously extracting multiple elements from diverse feedstocks. Deadline: April 24, 2026
https://lnkd.in/gD6P8tQC

EMBER - developing a biotechnology-based separation and purification strategy for Rare Earth Elements (REEs) from under-utilized domestic sources such as phosphate mine waste, acid mine drainage, and electronics recycling processes. https://lnkd.in/gCwjQFAF

At Nomadic Venture Partners, we've been fortunate enough to provide our input to both agencies and are excited to see what comes next as we invest in the supply chains that build our future.

Critical minerals has been in the news a lot lately. 

From the $12B U.S. strategic critical minerals reserve aimed at reducing dependence on foreign supply chains to high-profile convenings on critical minerals bringing together governments and industry leaders, the race to secure mineral supply chains is certainly accelerating. Yet, a surprising amount of potential critical mineral supply already exists, sitting in plain sight as mine waste in both operating and abandoned mines.

Join us on Monday, February 16th, as we explore mine tailings, by-product recovery, and how discarded material can become a strategic source of critical minerals. 

Featuring research led by Elizabeth Holley at Colorado School of Mines, technical insights from Ryan Mathur, CTO and Co-Founder of VectOres Science, policy / NGO perspective from Ty Churchwell of Trout Unlimited, and investment perspective from our team at Nomadic Venture Partners, the discussion will examine how research, innovation, policy, and targeted investment can help turn mine waste and by-products into a reliable domestic supply. Not only are they world-leading experts, they are also some of the nicest people ever, so I'm excited for you all to learn from our guest speakers.

Congratulations to Munkhsukh Sukhbaatar! He will be taking over leadership as the first Mongolian national to head Oyu Tolgoi LLC / Оюу толгой ХХК since 2009. For the first time under Rio Tinto, a Mongolian will step into the CEO role of what is projected to be one of the largest copper mines in the world.

I've had the pleasure of hosting an event with Munkhsukh in UB a few summers ago. He began his career at CITCO in the US with MBA from UC Berkeley, later returning to Mongolia to establish an investment fund and then lead Newcom Group’s airline through the mining boom-and-bust cycle. He joined Rio Tinto in 2013 at Oyu Tolgoi, moved to Brisbane in 2016 to work on strategic development and green energy, and oversaw copper portfolio including Oyu Tolgoi.

This is a meaningful moment for the country. When people who understand the land and the communities have a voice in major decisions, it strengthens the path forward.

Leadership is also diversifying across the global industry. Newmont Corporation has named Natascha Viljoen as its next CEO. BHP is preparing to appoint a new CEO. These decisions reflect a broader recognition that experience and perspective matter in shaping how mining evolves. 

It is an exciting moment for the industry, and I look forward to what this next chapter brings.… more

In venture capital, the data is clear: gender-balanced teams generate higher returns, driving innovation, and amplifying impact. In mining, research shows that inclusion is not just an ethical imperative; it delivers real profits, safer sites, and better outcomes for communities and investors alike.

Yet, today’s reality calls for courage. More women are exiting the workforce, held back by persistent barriers: return-to-office policies that push them out. Laws in over 60 countries restricting their employment in mining, two-thirds of mining codes failing to protect women’s rights, and a daily struggle against harassment and exclusion. Career growth and leadership remain elusive; just 12% of female cabinet ministers oversee energy and natural resources, and too few sit at the table where capital is allocated, with only 2% of venture funding going to female founders.

And despite these headwinds, the contributions of women stand out as pillars of possibility. In some regions, women make up upwards of 90% of artisanal miners, processing nearly all the ore and producing over 10% of the world’s cobalt. Yet most remain without contracts, protection, or influence in the decisions that shape their futures.

Research shows that inclusion improves safety, productivity, and performance. In Chile, Ghana, and Papua New Guinea, women operated equipment more efficiently than men. One Australian mining firm found its most inclusive teams had 67% fewer injuries and higher productivity.

A new generation of women are reshaping what mining can be. They are building companies, leading research, and proving that inclusion drives performance and progress.

At Nomadic Venture Partners, we are proud to work alongside female-led startups in our portfolio and to support organizations like Women in Mining USA that are creating access, opportunity, and leadership across the sector. 

The future will be defined by those brave enough to imagine new paths. The critical minerals race, and the transformation across industries, will be won by those who create a seat at the table for all.
