About
I’m an investor and ecosystem builder committed to accelerating deep‑tech and cleantech innovation in Europe. 
With a Master’s degree in environmental physics, I began my career in renewable‑energy projects before founding the OBJEKT innovation hub and leading the Prototron fund. After serving as CEO of Beamline Accelerator, I now act as a Venture Partner there ￼.  I sit on the board of the Estonian Business Angels Network (EstBAN) and lead multiple syndicates, investing in startups like MarkeDroid, BrachyDose, MuunHealth and SafePAS.
Through SafePAS, I co-drive the commercialization of portable lab-grade analyzers (drug-testing and soil-testing tools are ready so far).  
My passion is connecting science, entrepreneurship, and capital to build impactful solutions—and when I’m not working, you’ll find me painting 🎨 or exploring the ocean’s depths 🪸🐬

This week, I’m at Forensics Europe Expo (FEE) 2026 in London with Jekaterina Mazina, representing SafePAS and the Drug Hunter | Europol-Awarded Portable Drug Detection for On-Site Use device.

It’s inspiring to see how many people here are working on the future of public safety, forensic investigations, and more effective decision-making in this field. The conversations are not just about technology - they’re about making investigations faster, more transparent, and fairer, so resources are used wisely and accountability is based on reliable data.

We wrapped the Estonian Business Angels Network (EstBAN) season inside PoCo, the pop-art museum in Tallinn — and during the art-tour (omg! it was sooo interesting) I thought, angels and pop artists are in the same business.

Stay with me: 
- Pop art took ideas that lived in private galleries and elite collections and put them in front of everyone. Art, made available to all of us.
- Early-stage investing does the same thing — just earlier in the pipeline.

A founder's idea starts out trapped: in a lab, on a slide, inside their own head. Backing it early is how that idea gets out — into the market, into our hands, into everyday life.

The room made the case better than I can. Perfectly curated by Darja Ogorodnik pitches from across the Nordics and Baltics explained solutions that don't quite exist yet but should. 
None of it would happen without someone willing to take the first risk. (The museum itself is proof: built by an Estonian tech pioneer who simply wanted more people to have access to the art he loved.)

So this a huge Thank-you to the angels who keep doing the unglamorous early part. You're not just funding companies. You're making ideas available.

Same job as the artists on the walls around us.
