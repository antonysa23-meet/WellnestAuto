About
Kelly Graziadei brings 20 years of leadership experience in tech and consumer internet with companies ranging from 3 people to 200,000. She spent 7 1/2 years at Facebook in leadership positions in sales, product marketing and media. Kelly joined Facebook in 2010 and built and led its Mid-Market Sales & Account Management team in North America. Later during her tenure at Facebook, she led Monetization Product Marketing for the Direct Response Ads & Commerce business globally, focused on inbound product strategy and global go-to market. Throughout her career, Kelly has driven dramatic scale, revenue growth and product development while establishing teams and cultures that help people perform at their full potential. In 2018, Kelly became a founding partner of f7. f7 is a seed investment fund comprised of seven female leaders who met during their tenure at Facebook. With expertise in product, engineering, sales, marketing, partnerships, HR, and operations, f7 invests in and helps bold founders grow and scale at their earliest stages.
Kelly graduated with honors from the University of Colorado with a B.S. in advertising and lives in San Francisco with her husband and two sons. Kelly is a board member of Payfare and had the honor of delivering the 2016 Commencement address at the University of Colorado at Boulder.

Today we're launching Act 2 of our Master Plan. Corvus Trident is an universal AI copilot for material handling equipment (MHE) - forklifts, order pickers, reach trucks, and pallet jacks. With Trident, we're accelerating Corvus AIMS, our AI platform for warehouses, far beyond "just" centralized inventory intelligence.

In hands-free mode, Trident actively scans and analyze every pallet that the host vehicle touches, and makes real time updates to your WMS through AIMS.

In copilot mode, AIMS and a fleet of Tridents assume responsibility for directing the shipping and receiving flow, taking tasks from your WMS (and your ERP/TMS - when available!) and steering operators between locations and tasks to achieve maximal throughput - all while collecting ground-truth data on your inventory, operations, and safety like never seen before.

Building Trident was quite fun, and quite hard. We believe that controlling every single part of the stack - from the image sensors, to the optics, to the SoC, to the AI models that run on it - is critical for delivering the best product. I think that we have delivered.

We took the extremely capable hardware and software stack we built for Corvus One, and distilled it into a beautiful, vibration-rated rugged unit which takes ~20 min to install. Vertical integration across the stack has allowed us to make breakthroughs that would be otherwise impossible. My 2 favorite examples - Trident's built-in real-time visual positioning (VPS/RTLS) capability for the host vehicle, and being able to run our inventory intelligence AI models onboard. 

Trident's VPS stack does fully infrastructure-free vision-based positioning from a cold start - even in facilities over 1 million square feet. No stickers, no UWB beacons, no reflectors, no tags. We also optimized down to the ML compiler stack, allowing us to run *all* our AI models on-device, with your data never leaving the facility.

I am excited to see what our customers do with Trident, and the network effects of having entire facilities coordinated and orchestrated through AIMS in a way that has never been possible before this.… more

Perplexity Computer is what a personal computer in 2026 should be.

It’s personal to you, remembers your past work, and is secure by default.

Hundreds of connectors, persistent memory, files, and web access, all built on top of Perplexity infrastructure.
