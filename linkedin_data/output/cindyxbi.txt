Finance software has lied to you for 30 years.

The manifesto we published today is a rejection of that model.

The bottleneck in finance is no longer data. It's execution. 

Cross-system workflows. Exception handling. The repetitive, judgment-light work that consumes your best people and scales with headcount instead of ambition.

AI accountants don't assist. They execute. Reconciliations, accruals, deferrals, journal entries — done inside your existing ERP, with a full audit trail, without being asked. Not a co-pilot. Not a suggestion engine. A system that runs while you're not watching.

The question worth asking isn't whether AI belongs in finance. It's how much of the close cycle your team should still be touching at all.

The sportsbook era is ending.

Today, Novig officially received designation from the CFTC as a Designated Contract Market, bringing the prediction market built for sports fans nationwide.

For decades, the industry has been built around a simple premise: the house wins. 

Our designation validates a different approach.

From Day 1, we built Novig on a simple belief: sports fans deserve better.

A place where prices are determined by supply and demand. Where users trade directly with one another. Where success isn't punished. Where there’s no house. And where transparency is built into the product itself.

We're building the financial market for sports, specifically designed for sports traders by sports traders. For too long sports traders have been discriminated against, and it is high time that sports event contracts are treated as a legitimate asset class. Sports fans deserve the same fair treatment and opportunity to express an opinion on sports that all other traders have. 

From the beginning, we believed sports trading works best with a national framework. 

Now, we did it, and the next generation of sports traders across the country are finally getting what they’ve been asking for.

Despite the intensity of the designation process, this approval represents the fastest designation of its kind in CFTC history — a testament of the strength of our team and product. 

More importantly, it is the culmination of our conviction that there is enormous demand for a more efficient, transparent, and ultimately more profitable way to participate in sports markets.

We've already surpassed $5 billion in cumulative volume. Federal oversight gives us the ability to responsibly scale nationwide under a single framework built on market integrity, transparency, and trust.

The opportunity ahead is massive. It marks the beginning of a new chapter for an industry that has long operated under an outdated playbook.

Our mission is unchanged: build the most liquid and efficient sports prediction market in the country. We’re well on our way.

Pi Security is live today!

About a year ago, I opened LinkedIn and found an invitation to chat about a cybersecurity startup... sent to me a month earlier. 🙃

"No way this is still actionable," I thought, boarding the southbound Caltrain (this commute...). Replied anyway - "I'm interested, if it's not too late" - closed the laptop. Missed another one. Sigh.

Got off the train, sat on a bench, opened the laptop one more time.

Message from Kane Fallon: "Can you take a call now?" What is happening?

15-minute call. Tech round the next day. Another one the day after. Two founder chats. Offer in hand within a week of that bench. (Plus the month of me not opening LinkedIn, that one's on me.)

Why cybersecurity, why now?

I've always been drawn to the industry and its romance - cryptography and computer security major, after all. But timing is everything. Start this company two years earlier and you burn runway with a bloated headcount waiting for the market. Two years later and the train has left the station. AI tools are opening an entirely new attack surface - and amplifying negligence at machine speed. Existing tools simply can't transform fast enough for AI-first development cycles and AI-armed attack groups.

It's the perfect storm. And we're building the ship for it.

Why did I join?

I looked at the founders' footprint: Guy Arazi and Yoni Ramon are both cybersecurity veterans who walked the full path - security practitioners who left very well-paid gigs because they found a gap they couldn't unsee. That's the conviction I needed to observe before signing up.

What it's been like:
→ Four people, on-site, in an SF co-working space. Scrappy, exactly how it should be at this phase. Much bigger now, with a proper office space 😄 
→ Fast pace, can't be faster - every team member has full agency over what to work on within the vision. That's how it should be, too.
→ Hiring carefully. With AI productivity tools, we don't need headcount - we need killers with 10x potential. And they get an environment that actually lets them be that 10x engineer.

Almost a year in: design partners converted into customers, big names signed (oh you'll see 👀), and we're out of stealth today - enter Pi Security. The roadmap is big and ambitious, just how it should be, yet again. 🚀 

Sometimes the best career move is sitting on a bench and opening your laptop one more time.… more

Today Pi Security is officially out of stealth, with $35M in funding.

I've been into security for as long as I can remember. Building and breaking software was my biggest passion as a kid, and it still is. I spent my career helping some of the largest companies in the world stay secure, and was part of frontier research teams responsible for creating novel mitigations and stopping issues from recurring. And yet, year after year, I kept finding the same issues and running the same processes to remediate them. When AI coding took off, that problem grew into a different dimension entirely.

When we pitched Pi Security a year ago, people thought we were crazy. A different approach, hard to box into an existing product line, unlike anything else in the space. We focused on one question: how do you turn an organization's unstructured security knowledge into institutional memory, so teams can run an agentic SDLC with the same security standards they've always held?

When Yoni Ramon and I met, we realized we were seeing different sides of the same challenge: how do you help organizations learn once, and never pay for the same security lesson twice? That's why we built Pi.

Today, Pi Security secures one of the largest superintelligence companies in the world. We'll let that speak for itself.

I'm incredibly proud of what our team has built, grateful to the customers and partners who trusted us early, and excited for what comes next.
