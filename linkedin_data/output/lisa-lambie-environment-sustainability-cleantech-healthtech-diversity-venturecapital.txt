The many faces of humanity in an emergency room.

I’ve spent the past few days at the emergency room with my father.

A kind volunteer said to me gently: “It’s rare to see this. Usually what we see here is a foreign domestic helper caring for an elderly parent, an elderly person caring for another elderly person, or someone who lives alone calling 119 and arriving by ambulance with no one beside them. Having family right here with him — your father is fortunate.”

I sat with that for a long time.

If I hadn’t happened to be back in Taipei this week, my father would have been one of those “usual” cases too.

Sitting there, listening to the laboured breathing and coughing from every bed around us, witnessing how fragile and helpless illness can make us — I have the deepest respect for the doctors and nurses working tirelessly in that room.

I still believe technology can help ease the burden on healthcare workers and caregivers. That belief hasn’t changed.

But there’s something technology cannot solve. As we age, what we long for most isn’t efficiency or innovation — it’s the presence, care, and love of family. ❤️

I don’t have a good answer for that yet. 🙏… more

Last week's XA Network's panel on healthcare innovation and investment surfaced some sharp takes on where the industry is heading. A few really stuck out:
- Annabel Lin laid out the staggering imbalances in healthcare infrastructure — entire countries like Vietnam relying on a single radiologist, while developed markets stare down a $47 trillion chronic disease burden.
- Rio Hoe (Ease Healthcare) made the case for vertical focus in digital health. Their move from women's health to obesity management wasn't just strategy — it was about building deep patient trust in one lane.
- Natasha Hui Jin Ng of BetaLife shared a refreshingly practical biotech playbook: build commercial revenue streams alongside your therapeutic pipeline. It de-risks the long R&D runway that sinks so many startups.
- Michael Blakey of Cocoon Capital flagged what might be Singapore's biggest missed opportunity — world-class research that hasn't yet been matched by commercial muscle.

A lot to chew on, especially around how Southeast Asia's health tech scene is positioning itself for the next decade. Thanks to everyone who participated in the thoughtful discussion.
