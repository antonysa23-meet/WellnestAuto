About
Kennedy is Managing Partner and Co-Founder of Smart Society Capital. SSC invests in growth-stage energy and enabling technologies in the US and Europe. Kennedy also Co-Hosts The Smart Society Show with Former MP and UK Energy Minister, Chris Skidmore. 

Kennedy was previously Co-Founder and CEO of UK-US enterprise software scale-up Topia, which she led from an idea during her MBA at London Business School to global scale, fuelled by $100M+ in venture capital funding, acquisitions in Estonia and the US, and a fabulous team. Prior to Topia, Kennedy worked in investment banking and private equity.  In 2020, Kennedy was a candidate for US Congress focused on innovation and energy.

Kennedy has been recognized with numerous awards for her tech and entrepreneurial leadership including London Business School’s Distinguished Alumna Award, Management Today’s 35 Under 35 and The Women of the Future, Women in IT, Stevie Awards and CloudNOW Awards Entrepreneur of the Year honors.

Kennedy holds a B.A. in History from Yale University and an MBA from London Business School.  At Yale, Kennedy was an NCAA Division I and Ivy League Champion gymnast. Growing up, she was a national elite gymnast in the US.

View company: Voltiris
Voltiris

1mo • Edited • 

⚡️ The Netherlands’ first hectare-scale spectral-filtering solar project for strawberry production.
 
We’re excited to announce our partnership with Genson Group, a key supplier to Bakker Barendrecht, to install nearly 2,000 Voltiris spectral-filtering modules across a full hectare of strawberry greenhouse in Someren. This is more than a solar project. It’s a new chapter for premium strawberry production 🌱🍓

Here’s how it works: our modules split incoming sunlight. PAR light passes through to support crop growth, while the heat-carrying near-infrared is captured and converted into on-site electricity using 3M dichroic filters. ⚡️ 
 
💬 “A cooler and more stable climate during peak summer leads to larger, high-quality berries and more predictable harvest timing,” said Bart Goorts, co-owner and operational manager of Genson Someren. The installation is planned for summer 2026, with full operations from September onwards.
 
We are grateful to the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) | Partner in Sustainable Development for their support in bringing this installation to life! After tomatoes, we’re now opening strawberries as a new crop category for commercial AgriPV worldwide. 🍅➡️🍓

Read the full story on Hortidaily.com: https://lnkd.in/eaEi3HV4

The question of whether energy generation and premium crop production can co-exist? Answered. 💡⚡️
