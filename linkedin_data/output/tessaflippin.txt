About
Tessa Flippin is the Founder and Managing Partner of Capitalize VC, investing in pre-seed stage tech companies serving the rapidly evolving global commerce industry. Capitalize invests in emerging B2B commerce-enablement technology that serves retailers, CPG brands, creators and their supply chains.

Tessa began her career in venture capital as a venture investor at TechNexus, a Chicago-based fund, where she invested in e-commerce and supply chain tech at the seed stage.

Prior to venture capital, Tessa founded La Plataforma, an e-commerce tech company connecting peer to peer payments to retailers and convenient stores. 

Tessa graduated from Stanford University and went on to receive her MBA from Columbia Business School. Tessa is a Kauffman Fellow and actively serves on the boards of BLCK VC Midwest and Chicago:Blend working to bring diversity to the tech ecosystem.

Some communities give you contacts. A rare few change how you operate. 

Two years with Kauffman Fellows and Class 29 were the second kind.

What stays with me is the people. I watched my class take real swings: new funds, new ventures, big pivots, hard transitions. Not to mention the ups and downs of life. Seeing them push made me push too. Growth here is contagious.

It's also the least transactional room I've been in. A late-night text had me on the phone with someone in the broader Kauffman network within the hour. Someone I barely knew, in another part of the world, who simply showed up. People here cheer louder for your wins than their own.

The catch: you get out of it what you're willing to put in, not in effort but in honesty. The fellows who helped me navigate change were the ones I'd let in. Bring your whole self, and they meet you there.

Two years ago, I set out on a journey with the Kauffman Fellows. Last week, I had the incredible privilege of sharing some thoughts at the graduation of Class 29. 👨🏻‍🎓 

"The best accountability comes from sitting across peers who know exactly how high the stakes are, and who push you to execute better simply by sharing how they do it."

Eleven years ago, when I first started my adventure in venture capital, I remember looking in absolute awe at the investors who wore the Kauffman patch. They were individuals I deeply respected, admired, and wanted to learn from. The version of me back then could have never dreamed of just being accepted into this room—let alone standing at the podium to deliver reflections at our graduation.

While the path here was a dream a decade in the making, the last two years inside the program were a masterclass in navigating deep change. As a cohort, we didn't just coast on past wins; we knew had to re-learn the game in real-time; we faced the wildy steep learning curves of a rapidly evolving industry - together.

We walked into this fellowship looking for investment frameworks and market benchmarks. What we actually found was a lifelong crucible of trust, radical honesty, and peer-to-peer accountability.

Standing at the podium looking out at the room, I was reminded that exceptional venture investing isn't just about capital allocation or superficial networking. It’s about building an environment where you can be pushed to your absolute limits by people who genuinely want to see you succeed.

Here are the 3 core pillars I shared with our cohort for the road ahead:

1. The Lead Investor as a Partner: True venture excellence isn't found in a polished pitch deck; it's forged in the post-investment trenches helping founders scale execution.

2. The Power of the Crucible: Your network is only as strong as your willingness to be challenged by it. True peer accountability requires radical, unprompted sharing—putting your hardest, messiest, and most unvarnished operational challenges on the table before anyone asks. That is the ultimate hedge against complacency.

3. The Silent Architecture: Behind every successful investor or founder is a foundational support system. Our partners and families built the runway that allowed us to fly for the last two years. To my support system, Katherine Liew, thank you!

A massive massive thank you to my forum group - Kofi Ampadu, Allison Myers, Patricio Aznar Leon de la Barra, Tessa Flippin, Lucas Dzierwa de Lima and Kiswana Browne. Thank you for refusing to let me coast, for providing the ultimate sounding board, and for pushing me to be more rigorous in my thinking every single day.

To my mentor Hian Goh for helping me see venture through the eyes of my island home, and to the incredible Kauffman Fellows team, Fernando, Daniela, Kirsten, Madeleine, Katlyn, Bailey, Emily and Alana - thank you for building and sustaining such a rare, impactful community.… more

It's here! I got my copy of "I Bottled My Mother" The Mrs. Meyer's Story!

Growing up I had the privilege of seeing Monica Nassif in action. I was low key her third daughter (from another mother). I would just show up unannounced at their house looking for Aundrea Nassif and end up staying for dinner, cards, dessert, and sleepovers on the weekends.

I remember when Monica left Kilter to start Caldrea and later, Mrs. Meyers. I remember on our weekends and summer breaks from school we would go to the Mrs. Meyers office to help box and ship product. We made an assembly line out of it, we gave ourselves quotas, we argued over our favorite scents, we made it fun. I remember the holiday parties, we'd work the cash register, we'd take orders and sell out of all the product.

So much of my childhood was shaped by Monica's decision to launch Mrs. Meyers and build it into the incredible, beloved brand it became. Seeing a woman, mother and wife decide to tackle the odds and fearlessly pursue an extraordinary life is something that has stuck with me and helped shape my own career in entrepreneurship and venture capital. 

Monica, thank you for leading the way. You are an inspiration!… more
