About
I founded Behind Genius at 22, one of the youngest women in the world to start a venture firm. I raised my second fund at 25. I got lucky finding my calling early…thank you to HBO for making Silicon Valley.

Although I spend most of my time writing and investing now, I was an engineer first.

I studied mechanical engineering and computer science at San Diego State University, graduating in 2020. That year, I spent days as an early employee at WorkOS, and nights writing and self-publishing a children’s book for adults on venture capital, Seed to Harvest.

In theory, Behind Genius focuses on “technical storytellers” applying frontier technology to meaningful problems across the US and Canada.

In practice, we’ve made 50+ investments since 2021 across a $5M Fund I and $8.9M Fund II, connected our founders to $9M in additional investment, and built a content engine reaching over 10 million people.

We’ve backed an ex-DeepMind researcher building AI for the factory floor in Toronto, an award-winning restaurant operator building voice AI tools in San Francisco, and a PhD engineer building self-driving trains in St. Louis.

I believe meaningful businesses have the potential to reach $500M–$1B+ in annual revenue — and to improve the systems around us. This belief is informed by our annual study of the last 100 $1B+ exits.

Alongside investing, I write about venture, company building, and storytelling. I find joy in helping others articulate what they’re building and why it matters.

My story has been featured in Fortune, Forbes 30 Under 30, The Washington Post, and more, and I regularly speak at universities and conferences.

If you reach out, include a book you’ve loved recently. It tells me more than most pitches.

We've refined our investing methodology over the past five years at Behind Genius, and the What it Takes study has shaped how we think about venture-scale outcomes.

Five years ago, I used to think it was a bit gauche when investors asked pre-seed founders, "What's your path to $100M in revenue?"

After analyzing IPO filings and studying the outcomes of venture-backed companies, I realized that question may have been shy.

The average company going public today is doing more than $500M in revenue.

Additionally, venture-backed companies need to be large enough not just to reach an IPO, but to thrive after (hello, six-month lockup!).

In studying successful outcomes, one of my biggest lessons has been that market size is one of the most important variables in building an enduring company. Understanding it deeply is non-negotiable. 

A special thank you to Chinwuba E. for helping pull this together. 

Would love to hear any comments or questions you have on the study below!

View company: Behind Genius
Behind Genius

1mo • 

Incorporating 45 new $1B+ exits since our first study last April, our 2025 What It Takes study tracks how the drivers of scale, timelines, sectors, and valuation dynamics are evolving in today's market. 

This annual intensive study of the 100 most recent $1B+ IPOs, M&A, and direct listings is spearheaded by Chinwuba E.. 

Last year, this study helped us identify an source an overlooked but proven market (with a $3B exit) and invest in a stealth applied AI company in the same space led by domain experts. Our lessons learned also helped identifying key market patterns in investments in Intermezzo and NOX METALS. 

This year, we added a new section "Revenue at Exit," where we analyzed  revenue data from S-1 filings to understand what the new benchmarks are. Chart below. 

An interesting finding: The average annual revenue was $508M, skewed higher by outliers like Instacart ($2.9B). But the densest cluster (roughly 30%) of companies sits in the $200M to $400M range. Examples: GitLab, HashiCorp, Sweetgreen, Ibotta, NerdWallet, Samsara.

Being able to "show our work" on potential outcomes and demonstrate our conviction is one part of why founders are so excited to partner with Behind Genius. 

In Siddharth Ram's words on why they chose to partner with us - "While not fundraising, we had an opportunity to talk with [Behind Genius] and immediately saw the value they could add. Their perspectives - in particular Chinwuba's 2025 What it Takes Study - felt like a huge value addition to us." 

If you are interested in learning more, please visit our 2025 What it Takes blog below to read more about: 
- What is the average and median valuation of recent $1B+ exits?
- How concentrated are outcomes among the top deals?
- How is the exit market recovering after the 2022 downturn?
- What revenue levels do companies typically reach before going public?
- Which sectors are producing the most billion-dollar outcomes?
- How long does it actually take to reach a $1B+ exit?

We continue to work behind the scenes on studying and publishing original research on successful exits, overlooked markets, and application opportunities for new technologies. 

We're already starting on our 2026 study, which is shaping up to be a huge year for M&A and IPO - in our 2025 study, we predicted $1T+ in potential exits could happen. 

This study is meant to provoke discussion, so please let us know below if you have any questions or comments on this year or next year's study! … more
