About
Julia Monfrini Peev is the Founder and Managing Partner of PACE Healthcare Capital, an early-stage venture capital firm investing in healthcare technology companies that improve outcomes, expand access, and lower the cost of care.
 
Over her career, Julia has deployed more than $1 billion in healthcare investments across North America, Europe, and Latin America through leadership roles at CDPQ and Monitor Clipper Partners.
 
The daughter of entrepreneurs, Julia is passionate about partnering with founders tackling some of healthcare's most important challenges. At PACE, she combines institutional investing discipline with a hands-on approach to helping entrepreneurs build enduring, category-defining businesses.

Capita3 is excited to announce their recent investment in Backpack Healthcare.  

One in five children has a mental health condition. Most of them will never receive care. We invested in Backpack Healthcare because they built a model that makes serving those families economically viable — and then proved it works.

Over 40 million children are covered by Medicaid or CHIP, and nearly two-thirds of mental health providers don't accept them. Backpack's 24-month clinical residency program employs provisionally licensed therapists under structured supervision, producing a cost per session 15% lower than a traditional practice. That structural advantage makes Medicaid economics real. Layer in AI tools that have saved 40,000+ clinical hours, a 6.6% no-show rate against an industry norm of 15–30%, and a statewide school contract with Maryland, and you have a platform scaling without compromising the mission behind it. Revenue has grown multiple times over since early 2024. Seventy-seven percent of patients with depression show measurable improvement. The platform holds an NPS of +77.

Founder and CEO Hafeezah Muhammad built this because her son needed care she couldn't find. Twenty years in technology and operations leadership gave her the tools to build it at scale. As other digital health platforms pull back from Medicaid, Backpack is built to advance — with the margins, infrastructure, and partnerships to capture market share others are leaving behind.

Capita3 is proud to invest alongside co-leads PACE Healthcare Capital and ECMC. Backpack's existing relationships with major national health plans and state agencies signal where this platform is headed.

Follow Backpack Healthcare and learn more at hellobackpack.com.

What an electrifying night in New-York city, at the launch of the Alloy Health x Chief partnership.

Anne Fulenwider ran Marie Claire magazine for almost a decade. One of the most powerful editorial seats in media. She helped shape how millions of women saw themselves. 

Yet, she and Alloy’s co-Founder, Monica Molenaar, saw a massive unmet need - and opportunity - to support the millions of women navigating perimenopause and menopause. They both made a bold career turn (which is, in many ways, what Chief is all about), and built Alloy Health into one of the leading companies supporting women through this stage of life.

PACE Healthcare Capital has backed Alloy Health since their Seed round in 2021. While I always love hearing the story One.More.Time, hearing Anne in conversation with Alison Moore, CHIEF’s brilliant CEO, and dozens of CHIEF members last night, made me realize both how far we’ve come in five years... and how much more there is to do. 

Want another alarming statistic about menopause? 

1 in 4 women have passed on a leadership opportunity because of menopause symptoms. Women finally reach the peak of our careers, and voila, our biology will sweep a quarter of us under the rug. That’s not just a women's health issue (it also is): that's a major workforce issue. 

The good news? We know how to solve it. And Alloy Health is just getting started.

Thank you Anne Fulenwider, Monica Molenaar, Alison Moore, the Alloy Health team and Chief for such a brilliant evening - and tackling one of the most important and underappreciated challenges women face today.

We love helping digital health companies find the right resources, scale, and grow into something that lasts (and exits!). AMOpportunities is one of those stories with special PACE connections.

Kyle Swinsky started the company in Madison, came to Chicago for his MBA at The University of Chicago Booth School of Business, and built AMO taking advantage of some of the best things Chicago, Madison, and the Midwest had to offer, helping medical students and international graduates find the clinical rotations that classroom training alone cannot provide. He and his co-founder, Ben Bradley, chose to grow in the Midwest, given its ready density of talent, customers, and health-system partners.

One of the first resources that helped AMO take off was the University of Chicago’s New Venture Challenge, followed by early backing from the UChicago Startup Investment Program. PACE joined as an investor and growth partner, working alongside Kyle and Ben through the typical challenges of entrepreneurship, as well as the unique, like the pandemic, all the way to AMO’s acquisition by AIM Equity Partners.

Looking back in a sitdown with our team earlier this year, Kyle put it this way: “Your job as an entrepreneur is to build something scalable that can be handed off. PACE really helped us realize that over time, because they helped us build scalable functions you could trust.” That is the kind of partner PACE tries to be.

PACE’s Founder and Managing Partner Julia Monfrini Peev will be at the New Venture Challenge today to help judge a great group of up-and-coming startups. We hope to connect with great health innovators there!
