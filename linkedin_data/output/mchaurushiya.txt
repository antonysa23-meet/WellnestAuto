Last week my mother Purnima Chaurushiya retired from her 32-year career as a pathologist at Pomona Valley Hospital Medial Center.  Among her many accomplishments, 20 years ago she served as President of the Medical Staff.  She probably doesn’t know this but I still keep (and read) the newsletter pieces she wrote during that time.  She'll say that her job meant she didn't spend as much time with us as she wanted but I remember her always being around.  And when she wasn't, we could always show up unannounced in her office, conveniently located 15 feet from a back delivery door, a memorable time being right after my sister and I got tattoos (we were sent away with a stern “don’t do it again”).  In high school I stocked the hospital ORs which required wearing scrubs and a mask.  I loved when people would mistake me for my mom and yell, “Hey Dr. C!” In college I interned in the hospital’s IT department and got to have lunch with her most days. And a few years ago I took my baby daughter in so she could  absorb some of that Dr. C juju and grow up to be just like her. Today she embarks on her next journey (challenge?) serving as Distance Learning Manager for my niece and nephew in NYC.  Congrats Dr. C on an amazing career and exciting new chapter ahead.… more
