I’m excited to officially announce Sugar Free Capital’s Fund I.  Our LP base includes institutions as well as the family offices of leading technical luminaries from Nvidia, Jane Street, Citadel, Renaissance Technologies, Crusoe, and The Boston Consulting Group.

Our thesis is rooted in the MIT founder ecosystem, where gritty and intense builders take on the impossible.  We closed our first fund in just six months last year, and over the past nine months, we’ve been quietly backing the next generation of technical founders, often MIT alums, across defense, gaming, physical AI, and infrastructure. 

One of the best pieces of advice I ever received came in my first week at MIT, from one of my first faculty advisors: “This will be the highest density of smart people you’ll ever be around.” Stepping into the real world, I realized how right that was. 

Sugar Free Capital lets me recreate that same dense bubble of genius - and I don’t take it for granted.

To our founders: Thank you for letting me be part of your life’s work.
To our LPs: Thank you for your conviction in me and in our thesis from the very start.
To the SFC team:  Aaron, Stephanie, Jack, and Andrew: your energy, resilience, and growth mindset inspire me every day.

And to those who gave me my earliest opportunities - Kent at Bessemer, Sam and Dinesh at Comcast Ventures, and Jim at Crosslink/Tuck - I’ll remain forever grateful for your guidance and belief.

Ultimately, the best companies are built at lightning speed by founders with both urgency and extraordinary technical depth.  If you’re a technical founder building the impossible, we’d love to hear from you: team@sugarfreecapital.com.

We’re just getting started.

Warmly,
Sheena 

Thank you to TechCrunch and Dominic-Madori Davis for sharing our story.

We are looking for a summer intern (MIT student preferred) to join us Sugar Free Capital this year. NYC or US based remote option.

We’re looking for intellectually curious, proactive thinkers who have a bias toward action and great taste. Ideal candidates have:
-Intensity: curiosity and unmatched urgency to become an expert quickly

-Top notch communication skills: succinctly relay technical concepts into layman’s terms
