Excited to share that Plot raised a $10M round led by Camille Ricketts at XYZ Venture Capital and Dustin Moring at Mischief Ventures! 

We're grateful to have Alexis Ohanian Sr. and the team at Seven Seven Six 7️⃣7️⃣6️⃣ continue to invest in Plot and excited to welcome Scott Stanford from ACME Capital.

For the past 1.5 years, we've been building the video intelligence layer to understand what's actually happening in social video. 

Most of what people think, buy, eat, wear, and care about is showing up in videos now. Most of it never gets tagged.

We had strong conviction early on that the world had moved to video but the tools used to understand consumers hadn't made that shift.

What surprised us was how many different teams ended up needing the same underlying intelligence.

→ Community teams wanted to understand and engage their biggest fans.
→ Research teams wanted answers faster to hyper-specific questions.
→ Influencer teams wanted a better way to find creators, vet them, communicate, and track their ugc.

Today Plot is used by teams at Visa, PepsiCo, Fenty Beauty, Benefit Cosmetics, Canva, Mastercard, CAVA, lululemon, Yahoo, TurboTax, Dutch Bros, and many others.

ARR has grown 3.8x yoy. NRR is 132%. Almost all of that growth has come from inbound demand and word of mouth. 

I've spent my entire career in social. First at Apple, then running an agency, and now building Plot. For years, the conversation was about proving social mattered.

What excites me now is what happens when social teams finally have infrastructure built specifically for them.

The next chapter is about building the agentic layer on top of it. Software that can actually do the work.

→ An analyst that can answer questions across millions of videos.
→ A community manager that helps identify, measure, and engage your biggest advocates at scale.
→ An influencer marketer that can source creators, vet them, and handle the operational work that slows teams down.

I've always believed the best technology gives people more time to focus on the part they're uniquely good at.

For marketers, that's creativity. That's the Plot bar.

Thank you to our customers, investors, and team for believing in what we're building. 

We’re hiring across the board!… more

Today, I'm thrilled to share that Garden is being acquired by SIM IP for $150m — creating the world's leading IP monetization and licensing platform. 

SIM IP started off as our customer, and as we spent more time with them among hundreds of other users, we realized that to truly change the market we operate in, we had to vertically integrate to build a one-of-one software enabled business. 

To say this journey has been surreal would be an understatement.

I started Garden from my Stanford dorm room as a senior with a laptop and a thesis: reasoning models and better search could help average people understand patents. Little did I know the impact it would have in unlocking the trillions of dollars of untapped value sitting in patent portfolios around the world.

I was a 22-year-old first-time founder who had never raised a dollar, never hired anyone full time, and if we’re being honest, had no idea what I was doing at some points. So many people took a bet on me when they had every reason not to. I will never forget that.

To my team — you are the reason we're here. You believed in a weird idea about AI and patents before it was obvious. You worked nights, weekends, and through moments when the outcome was far from certain. We faced rejection over and over before our use case became obvious. This is your win.

To our investors — thank you for backing a kid with a 5 page memo and a dream. Your conviction gave us the runway to build something real. We made a lot of pivots before we got to something tangible, so your raw conviction in our team is something I will never take for granted.

To our customers — thank you for taking a bet on us and helping usher in the everyday use of reasoning models in patent litigation and prosecution.

To our competitors - thank you for forcing us to be better everyday.

As part of SIM IP, Garden becomes the technology engine behind a pure-play licensing and royalty company that is scaling nine-figures in revenue. We're not just analyzing patents anymore — we're building the infrastructure that will define how innovation is valued and monetized for decades to come.

The mission hasn't changed. We're still obsessed with the same problem: less than 2% of patents ever generate revenue for their inventors. That's a market failure. And now, we have the scale, capital, and team to fix it.

I'm eternally grateful for how far we've come — and even more excited for what's next.

Today I’m excited to announce DataLane’s $22M Series A! 

Local businesses make up 43% of the U.S. economy, yet they remain one of the hardest markets to reach.

Why?

Because 99% of go-to-market tech is built for B2B companies selling to other enterprises. These GTM tools don’t work when selling to local business owners who spend their days on-site, not on LinkedIn.

I experienced this firsthand in college when I started Potluck, a food delivery pooling app. 

We sold to mom-and-pop restaurants and it was incredibly hard to identify specific businesses that were a fit, much less the specific person who could say yes to working with us. I spent hours every day combing through Google listings and Facebook pages, only to end up stonewalled by front line workers who didn't even know who actually owned the business. 

Turns out, I wasn’t alone. Today I’m constantly talking to RevOps leaders and sales directors at Fortune 500 companies who are still running into these same problems selling to restaurants, retail shops, home service contractors, and healthcare clinics. Companies are spending 8 figures on offshore BPO teams to cobble together incomplete account lists that are out-of-date one month later. It’s time to finally fix this.

DataLane is the first solution specifically designed for sales & marketing teams selling to local businesses. 

With recent advances in state-of-the-art entity resolution models, it’s now possible to map 2 billion data points across 20 million local businesses.

This foundational data allows us to solve problems that have held back revenue organizations for years:

- Sales reps spending 30-40% of their time manually researching data
- Teams focusing on the wrong accounts with no clear visibility into their TAM 
- Revenue leaders lacking the market intelligence they need to make strategic growth decisions

We started DataLane last year and now support many of the largest local business sales teams at companies like DoorDash, Square, and Paychex. We’re on track to 5x this year and are positioned for an even stronger 2026. 

As DoorDash shared with Axios: “DataLane’s capabilities ensure we quickly and efficiently engage the local businesses that will benefit most from our solutions.”

If you sell to local businesses and haven’t yet seen a step change in results from AI, we would love to help: david@datalane.com

And, if you want to work on some of the hardest data & AI problems, we’re hiring for engineering, sales, and BizOps roles! … more
