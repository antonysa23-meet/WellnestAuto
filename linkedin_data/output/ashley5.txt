Reshared by Ashley Smith

Round names are meaningless right now. 🌱 🌱 🌱 🌱 🌱 

I spent some time talking to Dominic-Madori Davis at TechCrunch about seed round pricing and she published a piece last night that I'm quoted in along with friends Jonathan Lehr at Work-Bench and Shanea Leven at Empromptu.ai. Go read it. She nails the current sentiment in the market. And what I get asked everyday from VCs, LPs, and founders.

The TechCrunch piece captures what's happening. Pete Martin, who raised a $5M seed at a $25M post back in 2024, put it plainly: today "it's pretty typical" to see a $10M seed at a $40-45M post if you're an AI company.

Jonathan Lehr at Work-Bench said what I've been telling every founder I talk to: higher seed valuations mean less margin for error. Less room for experimentation, less tolerance for pivots, more scrutiny if progress doesn't match the capital raised.

Both of those things are true. Here's what didn't fit in my quotes.

The round name is a rounding error on the thesis right now. A $300M seed isn't a seed. $40M YC Demo Day pricing isn't a seed by any definition most of us were trained on. But the labels have stuck around even as the numbers detached from the risk profiles they were designed to describe.

For funds like mine, the math stops working above ~$60M post. So we've moved earlier. Before the product. Sometimes before the founder has left their job. That only works because we're highly specialized and our network is deep in the AI infra market.

For founders: the headline rounds are outliers, not the market. Carta data has seed sitting around $25-30M post this quarter. Price yourself into a corner early and the bar you have to clear for your Series A, which is at record-high valuations right now. It gets very hard very fast.

I wrote more on all of it over on Substack. Link in comments.


It’s not your imagination: AI seed startups are commanding higher valuations | TechCrunch

I asked 12 investors one question: "What's your hottest take on the VC market today?" 🌶️🌶️🌶️🌶️

“Using AI agents to screen seed founders is like using a high-tech ouija board. The criteria everyone’s scraping for today would have screened out the three $30B+ companies in Founder Collective’s Fund I." - Amanda Herson, General Partner at Founder Collective

“Most AI “technical moats” are just temporary API arbitrage. The real moat is product taste, workflow ownership, and distribution.” - Terri Burns, Founder at Type Capital

““Won’t the model just do this?” is the laziest question in venture.” - Lauri J. Moore, Partner at Bessemer Venture Partners

“VC firms should be less focused on investing in AI/software startups and more focused on consuming AI/software as users themselves.” - Nnamdi Iregbulem, Partner at Lightspeed

“Pattern matching is just bias masquerading as data” - Me :), GP at Vermilion Cliffs Ventures

“VC market in one word right now = kingmaking.” - Robby (Amanda Robson), Solo GP at Modern Technical Fund

“Heavy discounts on secondary sales are an inefficiency tax, a failure of process that can and should be avoided. - Kate Beardsley, Founding Partner at Hannah Grey VC

“Venture (especially early stage) is currently a low status job, and that’s a good thing!” - Ashley Mayer, co-founder and GP at Coalition Operators

“The more the VC grapevine and FOMO drive deal heat, the more founders value independent, first-principles conviction.” - Ariel Winton-Jones, Solo GP at The Aligned Fund

“Inspired by Lorne Michaels on producing, seed investing is an invisible art. If you’re any good at it, you leave no fingerprints.” - Jaren Glover, GP at 47th Street

“VCs will make more money on college graduates than college dropouts in the next 10 years.” - Charles Hudson, Managing Partner at Precursor Ventures

“Founders win this vintage. Grow fast enough, dilute almost nothing.” - Maria Palma, General Partner at Freestyle Capital
