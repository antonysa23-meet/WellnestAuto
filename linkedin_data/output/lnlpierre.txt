About
La Keisha Landrum Pierre is a force in venture capital: a purpose-driven investor, strategic operator, and three-time founder who brings clarity and structure to complexity. Her career reflects a singular focus: reshaping industries and building systems that create financial and societal impact at scale.

As General Partner and Co-Founder of Emmeline Ventures, La Keisha leads an investment firm investing in women-led businesses driving innovation in women’s health, wealth, and well-being. Emmeline Ventures stands as a fresh answer to a system—fueling founders who are building businesses and rewriting paradigms.

La Keisha is also the Executive Chairwoman of the Civic Media Lab, an initiative she co-founded in partnership with Omidyar Network, Luminate, MacArthur Foundation, and the National Endowment for Democracy. 

As the former CEO of Sahara Reporters Media Group, La Keisha built and scaled the organization into one of the most influential digital media platforms in West Africa and globally. Under her leadership, Sahara Reporters redefined the role of media in advancing governance transparency, secured multimillion-dollar partnerships, and became a global model for impact-driven digital storytelling. Today, as Chairwoman, she continues to guide its strategic partnerships and mentor the next generation of leaders.

Her career has been shaped by her ability to see opportunities at the intersection of strategy, community, and impact:

Founding and working with startups at the Harvard Innovation Lab, including one that exited to HudlHealth.

Collaborating with the Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society at Harvard Law School to advance research at the nexus of technology and inclusion.

Driving innovation and equity in the administration of Cambridge, MA’s first Black LGBTQ mayor.

La Keisha is a sought-after speaker who has shared her expertise at premier institutions and events, including Harvard, NYU Stern, USC, Carnegie Mellon, Women’s Capital Summit, AWS Founders Events, LA Tech Week, and more. Her talks challenge conventional thinking about venture capital, explore the power of operational excellence, and highlight the role of innovation in driving systemic change.

Authenticity fuels everything La Keisha does. Her work is driven by the belief that venture capital is more than funding—it’s a lever to create systems that empower individuals and communities to thrive. She welcomes connection, collaboration, and dialogue with those who share her vision for a more equitable future.

Why this matters:

#Endometriosis is more than just a medical condition. It affects education, careers, mental health, and personal well-being.

Supporting innovation in this space means more timely diagnoses, better therapies, and solutions that consider the whole person, not just the symptoms.

By celebrating and investing in women-led solutions for #endometriosis, we are creating a future where women are seen, heard, and cared for where their health is treated as a priority, not an afterthought.

✨️Spotlighting A Remarkable Fertility Health Unicorn Founder Gina Bartasi

Some founders build successful companies. While a much smaller group helps shape the future of an entire industry. Gina Bartasi belongs to that group.

Before #fertility became one of the most important and rapidly evolving sectors in healthcare innovation, Gina was building solutions for people navigating one of life's most personal and often challenging journeys, family building. 

1️⃣Through FertilityAuthority: she brought greater transparency and access to fertility information at a time when patients had few trusted resources to guide them.

2️⃣Through Progyny: She helped move fertility benefits into the mainstream, expanding access to reproductive healthcare for employees across the country and changing how employers thought about supporting family-building journeys.

Her next chapter became one of the most influential companies in women's health.

When Gina founded Kindbody, she saw an opportunity to rethink fertility care from the ground up. She envisioned a future where care was more accessible, more transparent, more technology-enabled, and ultimately more patient-centered. At a time when fertility clinics often operated within traditional models, Kindbody introduced a modern approach that combined clinical excellence with a more thoughtful and consumer-focused experience.

The company's impact has extended far beyond fertility treatment itself. Kindbody has helped shape broader conversations around reproductive healthcare, workplace benefits, access to care, and the future of family building. Its growth reflects not only the strength of the business but also the importance of the problem it set out to solve.

What makes Gina's story especially remarkable is that Kindbody was not the culmination of her work. Throughout her career, she has consistently identified gaps in healthcare and built companies designed to address them. That same entrepreneurial vision can be seen in her more recent ventures, including YourJoy.ai, focused on personalized longevity and preventive care for women, and Joi+Blokes, which is expanding access to proactive health optimization for both women and men.

Across each chapter of her career, Gina has made a common theme emerge, which is a belief that healthcare should be more accessible, more transparent, and more responsive to the needs of the individual. That conviction has guided her work for decades and continues to influence how healthcare is delivered today.

Emmeline Ventures is proud to include Kindbody in our #Fertility Market Map and to spotlight leaders whose work continues to shape the future of family building around the world. As we celebrate #Fertility Month, we recognize not only the progress of the industry but also the entrepreneurs whose vision helped make that progress possible.

✨ Very Excited to continue week 3 of #June with the Emmeline Ventures #Global #Fertility #Report: Examining the role #VC is playing in shaping tomorrow’s #families.

#Fertility #health #innovation is one of the most fascinating areas of healthcare to study right now, partly because of how quickly the sector is evolving and partly because of how deeply personal the experience is for so many families.

Emmeline Ventures put a tremendous amount of effort into understanding the companies building across the #fertility health ecosystem, the problems they are working to solve, and the capital supporting their growth. Who is writing checks and to whom. 

As we dug deeper, we saw a sector that was far larger and more dynamic and active than we had initially realised.

Across 257 companies, we've tracked more than $9.28 billion in disclosed funding and $22.59 billion in aggregate valuation.

Along the way, we came across several statistics that made us pause and think more deeply about the state of the ecosystem.

• Women founded nearly 60% of the companies in our dataset.

• Those same companies account for just 32.5% of disclosed funding.

•Fertility Monitoring (19 companies, 68.4% female-founded) occupies a strategically pivotal position in the ecosystem.

The numbers themselves are important, but what interested us most was the story behind them.

Many of the founders building in #fertility health are not approaching these challenges from a distance. They are building after spending years navigating fertility #care, reproductive health journeys, delayed diagnoses, treatment decisions, and #healthcare systems that often leave patients carrying far more of the burden than they should.

That perspective shapes the questions they ask, the products they build, and the opportunities they see.

The report also highlighted a sector undergoing remarkable change.

Artificial intelligence #AI is beginning to reshape fertility care. New diagnostic tools are emerging. Digital platforms are expanding access in new ways. Conversations around reproductive longevity are becoming more mainstream, and entirely new categories of companies are taking shape.

At the same time, much of the innovation remains concentrated in a relatively small number of markets, raising important questions about who receives #funding, where innovation is occurring, and which populations remain underserved.

This research does not provide all the answers, but it has given us a much clearer picture of where #fertility health stands today and where the next wave of #innovation may emerge.

We're excited to share our #Fertility #Health #Venture #Capital Report and would love to hear your thoughts.
