Post

Storied design firm Pentagram used Midjourney to create illustrations for a recent brand project. 

It caused quite the uproar. 

Some hated that they used AI. 
Some were disappointed that they didn't go far enough in their use of AI. 

The designers started with handmade human inputs, creating a montage with cut-out paper and paint. Then used AI to create variations for a website project.

Observation: 
Pentagram, a 50 year old design firm, known for its craft (typefaces), is experimenting with new creative tools. 

Is this a sign that generative AI is now —definitively— part of the design stack?

What is your take?

Post

Did Cursor + Anthropic's MCP just make billion-dollar businesses like Zapier obsolete?  

MCP lets AI agents connect directly with APIs, databases, and services—cutting out low-code tools like Zapier. Cursor just integrated it into their code agent, meaning you can now build end-to-end apps and workflows without manual automation tools.

Why This Is a Big Deal:

1.  No More Static Triggers → AI Agents React Dynamically

Before: Zapier waits for an explicit trigger (“When an email arrives, do X”).
Now: MCP-powered agents monitor, understand, and act when needed—no predefined trigger necessary.

2. No More Manual Workflow Design → AI Builds Workflows on the Fly

Before: You set up rigid automation chains in Make or Pipedream.
Now: AI decides the best workflow based on real-time needs—no need to predefine connections.

With the pace of product improvements in software agents in the last few months, millions of people could generate end-to-end software or automation flows in minutes within the year. 

Strange? Inevitable. 

While Cursor and Windsurf are more focused on developers today, the market for the "prosumer" app creator will eclipse this market.  

Fun fact: Zapier is the OG of seedscaling, getting to $230M ARR with only $1.4M in funding.

The US government just pulled a live frontier model offline for the first time. Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 is now gone for every user worldwide, three days after launch.

This is unprecedented, and I predict we'll see a few things unfold from here:

1. US frontier models might be forced to KYC every developer and user. That's probably a death knell to user growth.

2. Closed-source model companies will start moving aggressively up the stack toward the application layer. They can beat out app-layer competition on token unit economics alone.

3. The inference infrastructure around supporting multiple models just became more important than ever. (If you're building in this space, please reach out!)

Also, other things to pay attention to this week:

→ SpaceX completed the largest IPO in history at ~$2.2T. Impressive narrative shift from space company to AI infrastructure company months before listing.

→ Apple rebuilt Siri on a licensed Google Gemini model at ~$1B/year. The most valuable hardware company is leasing its frontier model from Google, and betting on the orchestration layer. 

→ OpenRouter launched Fusion, a multi-model synthesis layer that matched frontier performance at half the cost.

→ CZ Biohub released ESMFold2, an open-source protein AI that designed and lab-validated therapeutic binders in days. A direct challenge to DeepMind's closed-model moat.

→ Amazon disclosed 2.5B gallons of data-center water use, close to 5% of metro Seattle's annual consumption. Water is becoming a real constraint on the AI buildout.
