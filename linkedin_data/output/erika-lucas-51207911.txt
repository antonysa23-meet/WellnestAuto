About
I am a trusted advisor to leaders and institutions working to create a more equitable and sustainable economy, one that works for people and long-term growth. I build and advise on systems that move people from potential to power, without burnout, exclusion, or short-term thinking.

As Co-Founder of StitchCrew, I help design and scale inclusive economic systems that support small business owners through access to capital, culturally relevant programs, and policy advocacy. Our work sits at the intersection of entrepreneurship, workforce development, and economic mobility, with a focus on durable outcomes, not just participation.

I also co-founded VEST, a peer network and investment fund supporting women professionals and investing in women-led companies. Through VEST and as host of the VEST Her Podcast, I examine the invisible systems and power dynamics that get in the way of women building collective power and influence. 

Earlier in my career, I served as a Partner at a Private Equity firm and as Global Director at the Oklahoma Department of Commerce, where I led economic and workforce development initiatives across public and private sectors. This experience allows me to bridge capital, policy, institutions, and people, translating between worlds that too often operate in silos.

Today, I advise executives, boards, and organizations on how to:

• Build equitable, scalable systems that actually work in practice
• Design programs and policies that drive inclusion and economic performance
• Support entrepreneurship and workforce participation without extractive models

I serve on the boards of Arvest Bank, the Oklahoma City–County Health Department, and Diversion Hub.

As a speaker, I engage audiences ranging from Fortune 500 companies to national trade associations and economic development organizations on economic, entrepreneurship, and workforce strategies for a more balanced and resilient economy.

📎 Learn more at: www.ErikaLucas.co

In the latest VEST Her podcast episode we sat down with Cyndi A. Munson, M.S., Oklahoma House Democratic Leader and candidate for governor, for a conversation about the tension between playing it safe and stepping into bigger opportunities.

We talked about the hidden costs of staying small, finding your voice in rooms where you may be the only woman, navigating ambition without apology, and how resilience, mentorship, and systems change shape what’s possible for women.

Cyndi shared her journey from being told she was an unlikely candidate for public office to becoming the first Asian American woman elected to the Oklahoma Legislature and now a candidate for governor.

It’s an honest conversation about leadership, courage, self-doubt, and what happens when we stop letting other people define our potential.
