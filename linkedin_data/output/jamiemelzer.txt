About
Jamie is the managing partner at Altra Venture Partners, which has deployed over $50M to date in top Series B through Pre-IPO venture backed technology companies. 

Prior to Altra, Jamie was an analyst at Brigade Capital investing across asset classes including distressed debt, structured credit, high-yield credit, bank debt, CDS, and public and private equity. Prior to Brigade, Jamie was an II ranked high yield research analyst at Bank of America. 

Jamie graduated from the University of Kansas with a BS in Business and Finance and was a founding member of the Finance Scholars program.

Another NYTW kicked off with a bang!

Yesterday, I joined Goodfin’s “Tech IPOs: The Path from Private to Public” Summit at the Nasdaq as part of an all-star, all-female panel on the evolving private markets.

We spent the morning digging into a theme I think about every day at Altra: the best venture-backed companies are staying private longer than ever, and more of the value creation is happening behind closed doors.

A few takeaways that stood out:

✨  Savvy investors are reallocating part of their public-market portfolio into late-stage private markets, where companies now spend 12+ years compounding value before an IPO.

✨  Syndication, when done thoughtfully, is a necessary part of healthy financial markets - $50B+ rounds simply cannot get done without it. But not all SPVs are created equal, which makes manager selection and deal diligence more important than ever.

✨  Late-stage private companies look more and more like public‑equities, but “price” is just one piece of the puzzle; legal and structural risk, counterparty risk, company approvals, transfer restrictions, and liquidity risk all matter.

At Altra Venture Partners, we see these dynamics play out every day - and we believe that diligent late-stage investing is a core tool for investors who want to build exposure to the next generation of category‑defining tech companies.

Huge thank you to Anna, Mika, Justine, and the Goodfin team for hosting such a thoughtful conversation, to Atlas for the fun post-event interview, and for the surreal experience at the NASDAQ closing bell.

I’m launching a podcast tomorrow!!! 🥳 
It is my pleasure to bring “Beyond the Raise” to the world.

Beyond the Raise shares real conversations about businesses that actually work and endure - not just raise headline rounds.

Here’s the origin story -

One day over coffee, I was ranting (lovingly) about customer acquisition cost and how it impacts business models. My friend stopped me in my tracks and said - “Have you ever thought about starting a podcast? Because I would listen to this on repeat if I could.”  (Yes, we are both nerds 🤓 )

It hit me in that moment - I talk about business building all of the time. For me, it’s not work, it’s a passion.

So I asked myself...

…what if I could bring these organic conversations to more people?

And so, Beyond the Raise was born.

Each week, I interview founders, operators, and investors from around the world to unpack their real playbooks for building in the modern (AI-centric) age. Our guests share the systems, decisions, and playbooks behind products that work, customers who stay, and go-to-market motions that actually scale.

We believe:

✨ There’s never been a better time to build.

Space, robotics, AI, and new platforms are unlocking entirely new markets and capabilities.

✨ There’s a gap in getting to viability.

There’s no single place, accelerator, book, etc. to learn how to design and build a viable business in the real world (outside the spreadsheets and slides).

✨ Today, the playbook is mostly hidden.

Most business-building know-how lives in private conversations and small circles (requiring you to pay for advisory or consultant services, or get the right investors on board early).

✨ We can help you build a business that lasts.

Beyond the Raise pulls out the real business-building playbook from behind closed doors, one conversation at a time.

We may not answer all of your questions in one episode - but we can inspire you to reach new heights and expand your perspective on what moves the needle.

…and isn’t that the name of the game?!

This was a very topical week for venture secondaries - #IYKYK - which made it Altra-fun to discuss the evolution of late-stage secondaries at Camp Hustle.

Here’s what we covered in our session:

✨ Companies are staying private longer than ever.

In 2005, it took 5 years to IPO at a $200M valuation, on average

In 2026, it takes over 12 years to IPO at valuations well into the billions - and now possibly trillions, with the SpaceX IPO on the horizon.

…that’s a lot of value accruing to private market investors.

✨Decreasing IPO volume creates liquidity pressure.

With companies staying private longer than ever, the frequency of IPOs has decreased.

…but a lot of existing stakeholders still need liquidity along those 

Enter secondaries.

✨Secondaries are a key (yet complex) liquidity strategy.

Private companies are running formal employee tenders.

Early stage investors are debating when/where/how to take chips off the table.

There are seemingly more opportunities to buy secondaries than ever…

…but as an investor, secondaries aren’t as easy to participate in as you’d think.

Smart secondary investing requires an ability to assess pricing, structural risk, counterparty risk, company approval processes, transfer restrictions, and more.

As the market for secondaries matures, so does the intensity of the diligence process.

Since inception, we’ve built Altra with an institutional mindset and rigor. 😎

Big thank you to the Hustle Fund team for inviting Jamie and me back to talk about The Rise of Venture Secondaries.

The best venture-backed companies aren’t rushing to IPO anymore - they’re staying private longer than ever, accruing more and more value behind closed doors.

…investors need to reallocate capital into late-stage, VC-backed companies to regain the same innovation and growth profile that used to be accessible in public markets.

Every day, I see this shift at Altra Venture Partners as we invest category-defining technology giants through secondaries, primary rounds, GP and LP-led buyouts, and more.

It was a pleasure to share some of these insights with David Weisburd on his recent episode of How I Invest.

Here are some of the key market themes we unpacked:

• Secondaries are becoming essential as startups stay private for longer, creating a growing pool of stakeholders that need liquidity.
• Secondaries, once a fringe piece of the venture market, has grown into a meaningful part of the ecosystem and an asset class of its own. 
• The devil’s in the details - smart secondary investing requires an understanding of pricing, structural risk, counterparty risk, and more.
• The late‑stage secondary market now looks a lot like public‑equity investing, with the top 10 U.S. unicorns (SpaceX, Stripe, OpenAI, etc.) representing over $3 trillion in market value.

… and more.

Check out the podcast (link in comments), give it a listen, and tell us what you think!

Thank you for having me, David Weisburd, and for the intro, Eric Bahn!

If you’re looking to learn more about the late-stage venture market, shoot me a message or leave a comment below. We’ll reach out to share more.… more

That gap is exactly where Altra Venture Partners was built.

Jamie Melzer saw something in 2022–2023 that most investors missed: when rates spiked and public markets corrected, private markets lagged. The best late-stage companies didn’t need to raise, so valuations stayed artificially high… until liquidity pressure forced discounts.

That created a rare window.

A few insights that stood out:
• Some of the best private tech companies were trading at 40–80% discounts in secondaries—not because fundamentals broke, but because liquidity disappeared.
• The biggest risk in this market isn’t always the company. It’s transaction risk—whether you can actually access and close the deal.
• Late-stage private markets are evolving into a new asset class, with different players, shorter duration, and more liquidity than traditional venture.

One takeaway: inefficiency creates opportunity—but only if you can navigate the structure.

Co-hosting “Founder Secondaries” with Corient is already a highlight of our SF TechWeek! 🥳 

Here is what we covered -

✨ Secondary markets are here to stay.

…even as IPOs start to pick up (e.g. CoreWeave, Klarna, Figma, Circle),

companies are staying private longer than ever (avg is 12 yrs!!).

Early stakeholders are turning more to secondaries than ever as a means of liquidity,

whether they need it to pay for a house, return DPI to their own investors, etc. 

We see secondaries start to appear most often around Series B. 

✨ Secondaries are a strategic lever for startups.

In providing liquidity to early stakeholders (and employees), startups can drive employee satisfaction and retention.

…but they’re not just about $$ today!

Secondaries can be about $$ tomorrow, too.

We see companies thoughtfully choose secondary partners, especially before a new phase of growth or upcoming IPO.

Secondary partners often include primary round strategics; future capital providers for your Series C/D/E++ or employee tenders ; and/or long-term crossover investors that won’t immediately sell as soon as you IPO.

If you’re a founder exploring this today, we hope you consider Altra Venture Partners as a partner in that process!

✨ The best time to run a secondary process is alongside/after a primary round. 

The further you get away from a primary round, the more challenging secondaries can be.

When price and terms aren’t pre-set, there’s more negotiation to be done. 

✨ Taking some chips off the table as a founder can be a good move. 

Now we’re not telling you to sell everything, and every situation is different! Talk to Corient about this!!

…but taking some chips off to take care of your family, have liquidity in an emergency, etc. is increasingly normal (and encouraged) for founders. 

-

I’ll leave you with the same question we left the audience with: 

How can you make secondaries strategic, for yourself, your employees, and your company?

Tell us in the comments!
