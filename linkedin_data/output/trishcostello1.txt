WOMEN’S HEALTH: INVESTING IS WHERE CHANGE HAPPENS

Women’s health is the hot issue in the press and in policy discussions today. At Portfolia, we’re thankful that it’s finally getting the attention that it deserves. Women’s health and our very lives are compromised daily, because of the lack of resources and solutions focused on women’s health – both in research and in creating and scaling women’s health solutions.

The bright side is that there are a plethora of emerging medical technologies and processes that can positively impact women’s lives – from puberty to end of life, from daily health to specific conditions that impact women differently. We don’t need to be ignored, to have our needs met last, or to be handled like ‘small men.’ Investing is where change happens.

We are at the dawn of a new approach to women health, designed and customized specifically for our needs over our healthspan. The women investors of Portfolia have been instrumental in creating this change, yet we are only at the beginning.

New breakthroughs are emerging daily – better solutions for painful periods, safer pregnancy and childbirth, sexual and reproductive health, menopause, bone loss, all types of cancers, dementia, inflammatory disease, heart disease, pain management, sleep, addiction, mental health, even the first male birth control pill. Investing is where this change happens.

No matter how important these solutions can be to our lives, and how profitable the future financial returns, these innovations and companies often go without the investment capital they need, if women are not investing.

Who better than us, to greenlight the companies we want in the world, help make them successful, benefit from the solutions they offer, and share in the financial rewards?

Portfolia created the first investing fund, focused on women’s health -- and we’re the most active investors in women’s health in the world today -- now with 47 companies in our portfolio. We'll highlight each of these companies over the next 30 days.

Thousands of women, and a few good men, make up the Portfolia investment community, investing nearly $70 million together, backing the companies they believe in, for returns and impact. Investors can join for a minimum $10,000 investment.

BTW, do you know who owns the majority of wealth today? Women! And women over 50, own more wealth than any other demographic in America. Think of the possibilities if we actually invested it in a way that enhances our lives and health.
 
I’m thankful that some of our leaders today are shining the light on the lack of research and attention to women’s health. Advocacy is important but it’s not enough. Like you, I’ve come too far and accomplished too much in my life to leave my health solutions to others to decide.

Investing is where change happens for women’s health.

For ourselves, for our sisters, daughters, and friends. For my little granddaughter.

2mo • Edited • 

Congrats to Laura Yecies and Osteoboost Health Inc., which has closed an $8 million financing round to scale manufacturing, expand clinical research, and broaden commercialization of its FDA-cleared medical device designed to treat low bone density! 🚀

The round was led by Ambit Health Ventures, with participation from Emmeline Ventures, Disrupt Health Impact Fund and existing investors Esplanade Ventures and Portfolia.

The company’s device, a Class II prescription product, is positioned as a non-drug treatment for postmenopausal women with osteopenia, a condition that often precedes osteoporosis. Osteoporosis affects nearly 10 million Americans, with an additional 44 million estimated to have low bone density, according to the company.

The wearable device is worn around the hips and delivers low-magnitude vibration therapy to the lumbar spine and hips, areas particularly vulnerable to fracture. The treatment approach is based on research originally funded by NASA - National Aeronautics and Space Administration.

In a double-blind clinical trial conducted at the University of Nebraska Medical Center, patients using the device at least three times per week for 30-minute sessions over 12 months saw bone density loss reduced by 85% in the spine and 55% in the hips, the company said. The device addresses a gap in care, according to clinicians, particularly for patients with osteopenia who may not yet qualify for drug therapy.

The company said its device was designated a Breakthrough Device by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration in 2024 and later cleared through the De Novo pathway. It began shipping in May and has since been prescribed by more than 2,500 physicians, including clinicians at more than 30 academic medical centers. The product was also named to TIME’s 2025 list of Best Inventions.
