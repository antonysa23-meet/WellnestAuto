About
We founded Magnify Ventures to partner with entrepreneurs who are boldly transforming how modern families and communities live, work and care for one another.

For most of the last decade, I advised and invested in high caliber founders solving important problems with technology, as General Partner of Core Ventures Group and a member of Broadway Angels, a world-class all female investor collective.

A common thread across my experiences – from start-ups to public companies – is my passionate belief that innovation and an underlying social mission can not only create companies people love to work for, but products and services that can change the way we think and engage with the world.

Prior to venture investing, I was a serial entrepreneur and company builder, including serving as COO for the Western operations of DeNA, and founding executive of Current TV, the Emmy and Peabody-award winning cable network known for its early innovations in interactive television and social media. Other executive roles were with market pioneers Moxi Digital (precursor to Apple TV) and ReacTV (personalized video newscasts). 

I began my career as a strategy consultant with Booz Allen, working with the world's leading media and technology conglomerates.

Justin Young

  • 3rd+

AVP at Unum Ventures | Corporate Venture Capital | Corporate Strategy & Innovation

1w • Edited • 

Spent two days at #LionForum in Hyannis Port to discuss technology and policy questions shaping the current landscape. On a panel with Joanna Drake and Susan Reece Stowell, we debated radical solutions to the caregiver crisis, a structural problem the market has under-addressed. 

Credit to Christopher McKelvy and Judd Olanoff for convening a serious, cross-disciplinary group with rich content. 

I left with the following takeaways: 

1. Value will accrue to the trust layer, not the intelligence layer. When everyone can generate a clinical assessment or a care plan, the assessment isn't the moat.
2. This is a labor story before it's a technology story. The care economy is the largest white space hiding in plain sight, and the temptation is to frame it as an automation play. That's a misread. The winners will be the ones who rearchitect human work, who decide what only a person should do, and design everything else around protecting that.
3. The capital structure itself is being rearchitected. When the unit economics flip from "scale headcount" to "scale trust," the financing model has to follow.

Co-Founder, CEO @ Savi Security

2w • 

After my mom was targeted with an AI-powered fake kidnapping scam, I took a two year trip to the darker side of the boom.

I spent my evenings testing the rudimentary guardrails of the newest AI tools. I mapped cybercriminal groups on a wall in my office and cataloged their emerging tactics. I lurked in the seediest Telegram channels and created burner crypto wallets to buy ready-made scam kits on the dark web so I could see how they worked.

Whenever somebody happened to mention a scam in casual conversation, my eyes would inappropriately light up as I too eagerly solicited all the details.

I learned things that blew my mind and scribbled hundreds of pages of notes with no idea what I was going to do with them.

In a past life, this work might have culminated in a briefing in a windowless conference room to people with the authority to act on it.

But my security clearance expired years ago. So I turned it into a book instead.

Dark Side of the Boom publishes today.

Check it out at https://lnkd.in/gcCs7XxH and share if you think others might be interested.
