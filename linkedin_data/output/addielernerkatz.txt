About
Addie is the Founder & Managing Partner of Avid Ventures, a boutique venture capital firm based in New York City backing exceptional founders building transformative technology companies at the earliest stages.

As a career institutional investor, Addie spent the prior decade at General Catalyst, General Atlantic, and Goldman Sachs’s Special Situations Group. At these firms, she helped to deploy over $450M across eighteen investments in fintech, consumer internet, and software companies spanning North America, Europe, and Israel. While at General Catalyst, Addie realized she could uniquely support her portfolio companies including Rapyd, Monzo, Shift Technology, and Chief by applying her growth-stage skill set and perspective to help early-stage companies evolve into proven growth businesses.  

Addie is an active leader of All Raise’s New York City chapter, where she serves as a member of the Steering Committee to help promote and advance women in the venture capital and technology ecosystems. In 2020 she was recognized as one of NYC Fintech Women’s “50 Inspiring Fintech Females” in the “Money Makers” category. She was also featured on the 2019 Forbes “30 Under 30” Venture Capital list and honored on the 2022 list as one of three All-Star Alumni.

Partner at Avid Ventures | Forbes 30 Under 30

11mo • 

I’m thrilled to share that Avid Ventures has led the Seed for Juno, an intelligent travel operations and expense management platform.

Juno streamlines scheduling, approvals, policy navigation, traveler comms, payments, and support for the most complex, coordination-heavy business travelers – including those who sit outside the core T&E system and lack corporate cards, such as candidates, contractors, vendors, healthcare workers, production crews, university staff, and many others.

It’s a special privilege getting to back the Juno founders, Devon Tivona and Sam Felsenthal, a second time. They’re better positioned than anyone to tackle this massive, underserved segment within a trillion-dollar industry they know inside and out, with the perfect market timing. 

The backstory: 

My first-ever VC investment was in the Series A of Devon and Sam’s last company, Pana, a B2B travel company I backed shortly after joining Bessemer in 2019. The founders stood out as product-obsessed, commercially sharp, and deeply thoughtful. I spoke to over a dozen customers before investing and received glowing feedback, and loved trying the product myself when I flew to Denver to meet the team. 

About a year after investing, we were signing Fortune 500 customers — and then COVID hit. Corporate travel volume collapsed overnight, and Pana’s largely transactional revenue nearly vanished. Devon and Sam fought to extend runway, but ultimately sold the company to Coupa. While this was an impressive feat during an unpredictable time, everyone around the table knew Devon and Sam had unfinished business. Over the next five years, I watched the corporate travel market evolve (even becoming a travel advisor myself!) and hoped they’d start something new. 

Today, we at Avid (Addie, Zeeza, Irene) are proud to back this exceptional team alongside Madrona (Steve Singh - founder of Concur), Matchstick Ventures (Nicole Glaros - an OG investor in Pana), Bungalow Capital (Matthew B. Ziskie - also a Pana investor), and other leaders in travel and enterprise software.
