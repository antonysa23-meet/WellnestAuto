About
Lisa Carmen Wang, known artistically as Baddie Li, is a musical artist, speaker, and founder of the Bad Bitch Empire® a global movement awakening feminine power, wealth, and freedom. Through her music and media, she empowers women to reclaim their voices, embody their worth, and rise as the creators of their own empires.

She is the author of the best-selling book The Bad Bitch Business Bible and the visionary voice behind the Bad Bitch Empire podcast, inspiring millions of women to break free from good-girl brainwashing and reclaim their unapologetic power.

A former USA Hall of Fame gymnast turned entrepreneur, investor, and creative powerhouse, Lisa has spent her life breaking ceilings — from winning four USA national championships, launching a venture fund, and leading a movement that redefines success through feminine leadership.

Over the past decade, she has helped women raise over $50 million in capital, spoken on more than 100 stages worldwide, including Forbes Power Women Summit, Harvard University, PepsiCo, Capital One, Mobile World Congress, and YPO World Entrepreneur Forum, and been recognized as Forbes 30 Under 30, Entrepreneur Magazine’s 100 Most Powerful Women, and Red Bull Hero of the Year.

Today, through the Bad Bitch Empire, she fuses music, media, and feminine awakening to ignite a new generation of women to unleash their inner bad bitch and live in their full power, pleasure, and freedom.

“Everything works out for me.
I am on the right path.
I believe in my truth.”

I’ve repeated some version of this to myself over and over in the past year… as I’ve felt my inner artist come bursting out.

And here’s what I’ve realized:

I didn’t suddenly become someone new.
I just stopped overriding who I already was.

For years, I operated inside the definition of a “professional woman” I (and most women) had been taught: polished, composed, agreeable.

But if I’m honest… that version of me was also quieter, safer, and more contained.

It was optimized for approval. Not for power. Not for creativity. Not for full self-expression.

Something shifted when I started trusting myself more than the noise.

When I stopped asking, “How will this be perceived?” and started asking, “Is this true for me?”

These affirmations became anchors:

→ Everything works out for me (even when I can’t see how yet)
→ I am on the right path (even when it looks unconventional)
→ I believe in my truth (even when it’s misunderstood)

Because the real shift isn’t just internal, it shows up in how you move:

Speaking more directly.
Creating before you feel ready.
Letting go of being fully understood by everyone.

Not becoming “less professional”, but redefining what that even means.

So I’ll leave you with this:

✨ Where are you still filtering yourself… even though you know you’ve outgrown that version of you?
