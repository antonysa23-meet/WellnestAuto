Today, the Department of War will execute Operation Windlord, the first C-17 airlift of a nuclear reactor, in partnership with the Department of Energy and Valar Atomics.

Three C-17s Globemasters carrying the 8 modules of the Ward250 reactor will fly from March ARB to Hill AFB.

We're honored to be accompanied by Secretary of Energy Chris Wright, Undersecretary of War Michael Duffey, Senator John Curtis, and US Chief Technology Officer Ethan Klein on this historic flight.

A press conference with Governor Spencer Cox will be held after touchdown at Hill.

Ward250 is a passively safe modular reactor designed for high production rate and rapid deployment. We're taking an iterative approach to testing, starting at 100kW (250kW peak), and gradually stepping up to the full design architecture of 5MWe as we gain hardware experience.

5MWe of C-17 transportable, rapidly deployable nuclear power is a groundbreaking unlock for the American warfighter.

At 5MWe, a Ward reactor can power 5,000 homes or sustain a brigade-scale FOB with continuous power for communications, computing, and mission-critical systems.

Today's flight is an early demonstration of this capability. After landing at Hill, Ward250 will be transported to our test site, where we are working around the clock to prepare for power operations by July 4th under the Department of Energy's Nuclear Reactor Pilot Program.

It's been an incredible honor preparing for this operation with the Air Mobility Command and the amazing teams at the DOE's Office of Nuclear Energy.

I'm particularly grateful to the 62nd Airwing's Prime Nuclear Airlift Force (PNAF), responsible for lifting Charlie flight today.

At Valar Atomics, we believe that abundant and scalable energy is the backbone of a strong and prosperous nation. This is as true for America's warfighters as it is for her factories.

The Valar team is focused on safely and rapidly scaling to meet these critical needs.
