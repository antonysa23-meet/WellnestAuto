I’m truly humbled and grateful to have hosted our Silicon Valley–Saudi Deeptech Showcase yesterday in Riyadh, KSA. Radiate Ventures has dedicated our last 6 years in growing our ecosystem partners and helping US and global deeptech startups enter the GCC.

Saudi Arabia is central to Radiate Ventures’ mission to back transformative technologies shaping the future of our planet. The Kingdom’s bold vision, investment in innovation, and commitment to solving global-scale challenges align perfectly with the groundbreaking companies we support.

Yesterday’s event brought together founders from across the United States, Europe, and Australia, showcasing technologies in semiconductors, energy transition, construction tech, water infrastructure, mining tech, neurotech, medical robotics, enterprise AI, and more. Thank you @Alternative Investment Summit (AIM) for inviting me to speak at your prestigious conference in London this week! 

Panel on: “What are the Biggest VC Risks and Challenges for 2023?”

🟠 The venture capital industry has undergone significant shifts lately. While growth valuations have taken a hit, the early stage investments are still performing strong. We are also seeing tectonic shifts in parts of the world that investors typically overlook.

🟠 The recent crisis at SVB highlighted the importance of founders coming together during tough times. It's a wake-up call for all entrepreneurs to build resilience and risk management practices into their businesses.

🟠 The current environment is an excellent opportunity to see VC outliers and for strategic, long-term investments. Venture debt is also poised for a huge opportunity. 

🟠 Geopolitical diversification is also crucial, with Europe-based investors investing in the Middle East and Asian funds investing in Europe. Moreover, improving humanity for the next 20-30 years is a priority, with AI enabling emergency tech, elderly care, helpful community building, and anti-aging solutions.

🟠 Looking ahead, 2023-2025 will likely be very good vintages, providing LPs with an excellent opportunity to deploy into the asset class. 

🟠 As investors, we must examine the ethical values of founders and how they manage risks. Portfolio management and vintage diversification are key elements for long-term success.

Aalo has received DSA approval from DOE for our 10 MW reactor, zero-power criticality!

This is the equivalent of an NRC license in DOE space. We've been working towards this milestone at Aalo for 2.5 years.

This was a very ambitious project under the DOE reactor pilot program for a bunch of reasons:

➡️ 10 MW, commercial-scale system.
This is a system with the full-scale amount of fuel, moderator, controls, etc, necessary for powering AI data centers. Proving out this supply chain is a big deal. In the coming weeks we'll be taking it to zero power criticality, and then going to full-power operation in the months that follow. We'll be getting the factory set up in parallel to mass-produce it.

➡️ Built everything ourselves, from scratch.
We started with a green field, and within 4 months finished the building, facilities, policies & procedures, reactor, shielding, instrumentation, control, etc. People thought this would be impossible to do on this timeline for a reactor of this size, but we've done it. Hyperscalers care about speed, and we're showing that moving fast is possible at commercial scale.

➡️ Achieving criticality before July 4th.
We set out to ensure we met the president's order, and we are on pace to do so. We only started hiring at Aalo 2.5 yrs ago. It was questionable how realistic it was to take on all of the above while being one of the younger nuclear companies, but we are on track to pull it off.

Insanely proud of the team for all the hard work, nights and weekends that went into this.
